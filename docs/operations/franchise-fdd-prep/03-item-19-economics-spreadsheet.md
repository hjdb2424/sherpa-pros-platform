# FDD Item 19 — Per-Hub Financial Performance Representations

## 1. Required FDD Disclosure Language (Item 19 Header)

> **Item 19 — Financial Performance Representations.** The Federal Trade Commission's Franchise Rule permits a franchisor to provide information about the actual or potential financial performance of its franchised and/or franchisor-owned outlets, if there is a reasonable basis for the information, and if the information is included in the disclosure document. Sherpa Pros LLC is providing the financial performance information set forth below. This information represents historical and projected results based on Sherpa Pros LLC operating data from one corporate-owned Sherpa Hub (Atkinson, New Hampshire — opened in Phase 1) and industry-comparable home-services franchise benchmarks.
>
> **Some outlets have earned this amount. Your individual results may differ. There is no assurance that you will earn as much.** Written substantiation for the financial performance representation will be made available to the prospective franchisee upon reasonable request.

This disclaimer language is mandatory and must appear immediately above any earnings number presented in Item 19.

## 10. Sensitivity Analysis — Mid-Case Year 3 ($1.25M Base)

| Variable | -20% Case | Base | +20% Case | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue ±20% | $1,000,000 → EBITDA $169K (16.9%) → FCF $141K | $1,250,000 → EBITDA $269K (21.5%) → FCF $241K | $1,500,000 → EBITDA $369K (24.6%) → FCF $341K | Most sensitive variable; pro-recruiting velocity is the key driver |
| OpEx ±15% | OpEx $451K → EBITDA $349K (27.9%) → FCF $321K | OpEx $531K → EBITDA $269K | OpEx $610K → EBITDA $190K (15.2%) → FCF $162K | Wage inflation in tight Northeast labor market is the primary OpEx upside risk |
| Materials margin share ±10% | Mat margin 14.4% → Mat rev $180K → revenue mix shifts → EBITDA $249K | Mat margin 16% → Mat rev $200K → EBITDA $269K | Mat margin 17.6% → Mat rev $220K → EBITDA $289K | Materials margin share rises with platform-wide adoption — secular tailwind |

**Three-variable simultaneous downside (Revenue -20%, OpEx +15%, Materials margin -10%) — the worst plausible scenario:**
- Revenue: $1,000,000
- COGS (36%): $360,000
- OpEx (+15%): $610,000
- EBITDA: $30,000 (3% margin)
- Debt service: $28,200
- Pre-tax free cash flow: **$1,800** (essentially breakeven)

This worst-plausible scenario must be disclosed alongside the Mid case in Item 19. It demonstrates that the franchise model has downside protection (still cash-flow-positive even in three-way stress) but that material returns require execution against the Mid case or better.

## 12. Hub #1 Atkinson NH — The Proof Point

Hub #1 is corporate-owned (not franchised) per the franchise spec §10 decision matrix. Hub #1 economics are NOT subject to franchise royalty and marketing fund payments — those are corporate-internal eliminations. For Item 19 substantiation purposes, Hub #1 P&L is "as if franchised" — meaning the corporate substantiation team adds back the royalty and marketing fund line items and presents the Hub as if it had been franchisee-owned.

| Hub #1 Mid-Case Year 2 (As Reported Corporate vs As-If Franchised) | As-Reported | As-If Franchised |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue | $1,050,000 | $1,050,000 |
| COGS | $378,000 | $378,000 |
| OpEx (excluding royalty + marketing fund) | $362,650 | $362,650 |
| Royalty (8%) | $0 (corporate-internal) | $84,000 |
| Marketing Fund (2%) | $0 (corporate-internal) | $21,000 |
| EBITDA | $309,350 (29.5%) | $204,350 (19.5%) |

The "As-If Franchised" column is the substantiation reference for Item 19. Corporate operations enjoy a higher-EBITDA Hub #1 (no royalty paid to itself); franchisees see the post-royalty EBITDA, which is the right disclosure number.
