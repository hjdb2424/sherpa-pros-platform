1. Program at a Glance

The Massachusetts SBTA Program is administered by the Massachusetts Growth Capital Corporation (MGCC) and Empowering Small Business Inc., funding 52+ nonprofit technical-assistance providers across the state. Eligible MA small businesses receive services (legal review, accounting, marketing, business plan development, financial modeling, web/IT) valued at up to $150,000 per business per year — delivered free or low-cost through these nonprofit partners.

Critical structural difference from the other 5 grants in this packet: SBTA is not a cash grant to Sherpa Pros. It is a referral pathway to a nonprofit that will perform services on Sherpa Pros' behalf. The application is therefore a two-part outreach: (1) identify the right nonprofit partner aligned with our needs and our identity (founder profile, sector, geographic), then (2) work with that nonprofit to access the SBTA-funded services.

10. Tips & Gotchas

  1. SBTA is services, not cash. Set expectations internally: this packet does not bring money to the bank account. It brings legal, accounting, marketing, mentorship hours that would otherwise cost $50K–$150K.
  2. Pick partners by fit, not by award size. EforAll's cohort acceptance is more strategically valuable than its raw $-equivalent because of the mentor + investor network it unlocks.
  3. Stack partners. A single SBTA-funded partner will not deliver $150K of services. Two or three partners across different scopes get to the cap.
  4. MA-resident decision-makers respond to MA-resident voices. When Phyrom is in Boston for the EforAll intake call, follow up in person if possible.
  5. Identity-aligned partners (BECMA, CWE) require honest self-identification. If applicable, they unlock material extra service capacity. Phyrom should confirm whether his Khmer-American background fits BECMA's mandate (focused on Black-owned businesses) before applying — a more accurate fit may be Asian-American-focused MA small-business support organizations (e.g., Asian American Civic Association). Worth a separate discovery call.
  6. MA foreign-LLC registration is the gating eligibility item across all partners. File before partner outreach.
  7. Avoid "Wiseman" externally — use "code-aware quote validation" (per Sherpa Pros brand bible / GTM spec §3.3).
  8. The legal scope (Lawyers Clearinghouse) is the highest-ROI single SBTA engagement — the 1099 vs W-2 opinion alone would cost $15K–$25K from a paid MA labor-and-employment firm and is critical-path before MA scaled operations.
  9. EforAll cohort timing matters. If the next Boston cohort is full, ask to be on the waitlist + scheduled for the following cohort. Do not skip EforAll — the network is the thing.
