## 3 — The pro problem FW Webb sees daily

Every morning, FW Webb branch managers watch the same scene:

- Pros arrive at 6:30 a.m., wait 15-30 minutes at the counter
- Pros run **2-4 supply-house stops per day** instead of 1
- Pros lose **60-90 minutes per day** to materials runs and truck-loading
- Pros call the branch from a job site asking, "can you bag this and have it ready?"
- Some weeks, 1 in 5 pros bails because the line is too long

**FW Webb already solves the materials side. Nobody is solving the pro's *day*.**

---

## 4 — The Sherpa Pros stack today

**6 products** the pro uses:

1. Sherpa Marketplace (job dispatch)
2. Sherpa Hub (physical pro lounge + materials staging)
3. Sherpa Materials engine (orchestrated supply via partners — including FW Webb)
4. Sherpa Quick Job catalog (productized 30-90 min jobs)
5. Sherpa Home subscription (pro-on-call for homeowners)
6. Sherpa Pro Profile + Reviews

**8 platform capabilities**: dispatch, identity + RBAC, scheduling, payments, reviews, training, training-credentialing, embedded insurance + protection products

**Vertical integration** — owned end-to-end, not bolted on

---

## 5 — The shared customer

The pro who walks into FW Webb at 6:30 a.m. is **the same pro** who:

- Needs jobs dispatched to fill the gaps in their week
- Needs reliable materials staged before they arrive
- Needs a back-office (insurance, payments, scheduling, reviews) they don't have to build
- Needs a place to grab coffee, take a call, print a permit, and hit the road

**FW Webb owns the materials relationship. Sherpa Pros owns the workflow + dispatch relationship.**

The pro doesn't see two vendors — they see one frictionless morning.

---

## 6 — Pilot proposal

**5 Sherpa Hubs co-located inside 5 FW Webb branches**

- ~800 sq ft sub-leased per Hub (Plaistow NH, Haverhill MA, Manchester NH, Woburn MA, Warwick RI)
- Sherpa Pros operates every Hub: hires Hub Manager, staffs, equips, stocks, markets
- FW Webb provides: floor space, security, utilities, restroom access, branch adjacency
- Co-located format: pro grabs materials at FW Webb counter → walks 30 ft to Sherpa Hub for dispatch + workspace + Materials staging
- 12-month pilot, expansion option to 25-50 Hubs Year 2

---

## 7 — Win-win economics

**FW Webb wins:**

- New rent revenue (~$1.5K/mo × 5 Hubs = $90K/year, near-100% margin)
- Increased pro foot traffic in pilot branches (target +15-20%)
- Materials sales lift from Sherpa Materials orchestration (target +25% pilot-region pro spend)
- Brand association with the modern pro-network — defensive moat vs marketplace competitors

**Sherpa Pros wins:**

- 5 Hub locations at near-zero capex vs standalone buildout
- Day-1 anchor foot traffic (FW Webb pros are pre-qualified members)
- Materials orchestration partner already on-site
- Speed-to-market: 5 Hubs operational by Month 6 from LOI

## 10 — Sherpa Materials integration

In pilot regions, **FW Webb becomes the primary supplier** for Sherpa Materials orchestration:

- Top-200 SKUs default ship-to-Hub from FW Webb branch
- Zinc API routing reweighted toward FW Webb where price within 7% of next-best
- Uber Direct dispatch from Hub: 4 daily waves (6:30 / 8:00 / 10:00 / 12:00)
- Pickup-by-pro flow for stocked items (QR scan + grab + go)
- Per-Hub margin attribution: 40% buffer / 35% pickup / 25% staged-Uber-Direct (per Hub spec §13)
- **Preferred-vendor lock-in** = pilot-period exclusivity in pilot regions
