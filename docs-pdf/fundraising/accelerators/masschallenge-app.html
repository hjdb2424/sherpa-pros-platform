3. Application Narrative

MassChallenge evaluates applicants on four explicit criteria: Innovation, Impact, Scalability, and Team. Map every answer to these four pillars.

Q1: Company name, one-line description, and stage

Sherpa Pros — the national licensed-trade marketplace (international roadmap), launching first in NH, ME, and MA, built by a working general contractor. Pre-seed; live web + mobile beta with active GMV across NH, ME, MA. Founded and headquartered in New Hampshire; Boston metro is core to our Phase 1–2 specialty-lane execution — the playbook we'll template into every metro after.

Q2: Innovation — what is novel about your solution? (~200 words)

Three things no incumbent residential-services platform does:

  1. Code-aware quote validation in real time. Every quote on Sherpa Pros runs through a proprietary code intelligence layer that reads NEC (National Electrical Code), IRC (International Residential Code), MA Electrical Code, NH RSA, and 284 NH municipal codes before the homeowner ever sees the bid. We flag missing permits, wrong materials, and quotes that fall below code-compliant minimums. To our knowledge, no consumer-facing residential-services platform validates quotes against code at the matching layer. This required three years of working-GC operational knowledge encoded into a software product — not buildable by a software-only founder.

  2. Permit + rebate awareness baked into the dispatch. The platform knows which jobs trigger Mass Save heat-pump rebates ($10K+/home), National Grid Turnkey EV charger rebates, electrical panel upgrades as gateway work for both, and which jobs trigger Boston ISD permits. Lead-gen platforms cannot do this because their economics depend on lead volume, not job appropriateness.

  3. Founder-built by a working contractor. Our founder, Phyrom, runs HJD Builders in NH — actively, on jobsites, this week. Sherpa Pros is the platform he wished existed. Every product decision is informed by daily contractor reality, not market research.

Q3: Impact — what is the measurable impact, especially for Massachusetts? (~250 words)

Massachusetts has the largest construction labor shortage in the nation. 2.5% construction unemployment (lowest in 17+ years). 30% of MA residential construction workers are 55+ (vs. 26% nationally). Project completion times have stretched from 7 to 11 months. Carpenters, electricians, and roofers are the most acute shortages — exactly the licensed trades lead-gen platforms cannot deliver on.

Sherpa Pros' measurable Massachusetts impact across three dimensions:

1. Climate impact via licensed clean-energy installs. Mass Save 2026 heat-pump rebates are ~$10K/home, with ~75K heat pumps installed in MA since 2019 and a state goal of 50% YoY growth in low-income install pipeline. National Grid Turnkey EV chargers add a parallel pipeline. Sherpa Pros surfaces licensed installers verified against Mass Save certification + EPA cert + electrical panel upgrade qualification, in a way the static Mass Save Find-an-Installer listing cannot. Direct climate impact: every accelerated heat-pump install reduces a MA home's emissions ~3 tons CO2/year.

2. Economic impact for Massachusetts trades. Pros pay 5% during beta, 10% standard — roughly half the all-in effective cost of Angi, which charges $30–$100/lead shared with up to 40 contractors. Founding pros (the first 10–12) keep 5% forever. Direct economic impact: more dollars stay in MA-resident contractor pockets, not Manhattan-headquartered platform OpEx.

3. Housing-stock impact via old-house specialty lane. 50%+ of Boston housing was built pre-1950. Plaster, slate, brownstone repointing, and triple-decker exterior work are skill categories Angi/Thumbtack cannot filter for. Sherpa Pros builds the Old-House Verified badge category (USPTO trademark filing in W4). Direct impact: preservation of MA's irreplaceable historic housing stock through verified specialty matching.

Q4: Scalability — how does this scale? (~200 words)

Repeatable metro playbook. Phase 1 (months 3–6) operates the Phase 1 launch metros (Portsmouth NH, Manchester NH, Portland ME) as full marketplaces. Phase 2 (months 6–12) scales to Boston metro plus the Phase 1 launch metros, $1M+ GMV. Phase 3 (months 12–18) replicates to Providence RI, Hartford CT, with NYC specialty-only beachhead. Phase 4 expands to Philadelphia, DC, SF Bay, Chicago — and onward nationally and internationally. Each metro launch follows the same 60-day playbook: beta cohort recruit → first $25K GMV → utility partnership outreach → Pro Success Manager hire → metro live.

Three revenue lines stack:

  • Marketplace take: 5% (beta) → 10% (standard) → 12–15% (Phase 3)
  • Pro subscription: $49/month (predictable recurring revenue)
  • PM tier: $1.50/unit/month at scale (replaces $4/unit incumbent vendor-management tools, multi-year contracts, NOI-impacting product)

Unit economics: A standard pro doing $8K GMV/month at $49 sub + 10% take = $849/month/pro. 200 pros = $2.0M ARR before PM tier turns on. PM anchor at 1,000 units = $1.5K/year per anchor; 3 anchors = $4.5M ARR Phase 3.

Defensibility scales with adoption. Code intelligence layer compounds as Mass Save / National Grid / utility partnerships layer in. Old-House Verified trademark + USPTO filing locks specialty lane.

  1. Frame everything against the four evaluation pillars. Innovation. Impact. Scalability. Team. Reviewers literally score against this rubric. Each answer above maps explicitly to one pillar.

  2. Massachusetts impact must be quantified. "We accelerate heat-pump installs" is too vague. "We surface Mass Save certified installers in real time, and each accelerated install reduces a MA home's emissions ~3 tons CO2/year" is what passes review. Pull specific numbers from the GTM spec and TAM doc.

  3. Climate angle is a force multiplier. MassChallenge funds clean-energy, sustainability, and climate-adjacent startups disproportionately. Lead the Impact answer with Mass Save + EV. Mention Greentown Labs membership inquiry in the answer (signals coordinated NE climate-startup positioning).

  4. Mention Massachusetts sponsors explicitly.   6. Show NH/ME geographic commitment, then transition to MA. MassChallenge wants to fund startups that will employ Massachusetts residents and serve Massachusetts customers. Sherpa Pros' Phase 1 plan does both (Boston specialty lanes, MA-based hires by Phase 2). Make this visible in the Scalability answer.

  7. The deck PDF must reflect MA emphasis. If submitting the existing deck v1, ensure slide 3 (Why Now) and slide 6 (Market) show Boston metro and Mass Save data prominently. If they currently lead with NH, swap to MA-first language in the MassChallenge submission deck variant.
