Sherpa Pros Brand Manual — Outline

0. About This Outline

This document is the structural outline for the Sherpa Pros Brand Manual (Volume 2 of the Franchisee Manual Set; Volume 1 is the Operations Manual per 10-operations-manual-outline.md). The full Brand Manual is approximately 80 pages when complete and is delivered to every franchisee at signing as a perpetual brand-license under the Sherpa Brand License Agreement (FDD Item 22 exhibit).

The source of truth for brand standards is docs/operations/brand-portfolio.md. This Brand Manual is a franchisee-readable distillation of that internal document, organized for operational use by a Hub Manager who needs to know how to use the wordmark on a vehicle wrap, post on Instagram in-brand, or hire a local printer for grand-opening signage without violating brand standards.

1. Front Matter (Pages 1–6)

  • Page 1: Title page — "Sherpa Pros Brand Manual, Volume 2" + brand wordmark + Edition / Date
  • Page 2: Letter from the founder (Phyrom) — why brand consistency matters; the pros and clients deserve a brand they can trust to look the same in every Hub
  • Page 3: Manual revision history + version control (semantic versioning: 1.0, 1.1, 2.0)
  • Page 4: How to use this manual — index, search tips, and quick-reference page numbers for the most-asked questions
  • Page 5: Where to ask questions — Brand Lead corporate email; turnaround time commitment (within 2 business days)
  • Page 6: Critical "Do Not" summary — single page summary of the highest-stakes brand violations with consequence ladder (warning → cure period → audit fee → notice of default)

2. Brand Voice + Tone (Pages 7–14)

Section 2.1 — Voice Pillars

  • The working contractor is the customer, not the marketing target
  • Verified-licensed-trade quality over scale-at-any-cost growth
  • Founder-built, contractor-empathetic
  • Plain-spoken, technically accurate, no jargon-dressing

Section 2.2 — Tone by Context

  • Marketing copy (warm + confident + earned)
  • Customer support (empathetic + clear + quick)
  • Pro-facing communication (peer-respectful + technically literate)
  • Crisis communication (calm + accountable + actionable)
  • Internal staff communication (direct + collaborative)

Section 2.3 — Word Lists

  • Words we use (always): pro, verified, licensed, kit, dispatch, materials, Hub, member, code, score, training event, lounge
  • Words we never use: gig worker, contractor (when meaning a Sherpa pro — say "pro"), hustle, side job (in a derisive sense), DIY (when describing pro work), price-shop, race-to-the-bottom
  • Abbreviations spelled out on first use: Federal Trade Commission (FTC), Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD), Letter of Intent (LOI), Net Promoter Score (NPS), Profit and Loss (P&L), Stock Keeping Unit (SKU), Optical Character Recognition (OCR), Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)

Section 2.4 — Editorial Standards

  • Sentence-case headlines (not Title Case)
  • Oxford comma always
  • Numbers under 10 spelled out except for measurements + currency
  • Currency formatted with comma separator + cents only when required ($45,000 not $45,000.00; $45,000.50 only when cents matter)
  • Percentages spelled out in body copy ("two percent") but numeric in tables ("2%")
  • Dates: month-day-year long form in marketing copy ("April 25, 2026"); ISO 8601 short form in operations docs ("2026-04-25")

3. Visual Identity (Pages 15–34)

Section 3.1 — Wordmark + Logo Variants

(Per docs/operations/brand-portfolio.md §2.1–§2.5)

  • Primary wordmark — full color (sky blue + orange red blocks)
  • App icon variant — square iOS-style with letters S + P
  • Single-color variants (3): all sky blue, all orange red, all white
  • Tagline lockup — wordmark + Fraunces italic tagline below
  • Product lockup — wordmark + thin sky-blue rule + product name in Manrope small caps
  • HJD Builders LLC attribution lockup — wordmark + thin amber rule + "Built by HJD Builders LLC"

Section 3.2 — Wordmark Usage Rules

  • Minimum size: 80px wide digital / 0.75 inch wide print
  • Minimum clear space: equal to height of the SHERPA block on all sides
  • Approved background colors: white, warm cream (#FAF7F0), dark navy (#1A1A2E)
  • Background contrast requirements: 4.5:1 minimum per Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) AA

Section 3.5 — Iconography

  • 6-product portfolio icon set (Sherpa Hub, Marketplace, Materials, Home, Manager, Rewards/Flex) — locked custom illustration
  • 8-platform-capability icon set (codes engine, Materials engine, Dispatch, Threads, Smart Scan, Mobile, Guard, Score) — locked custom illustration
  • Operational iconography (kits, equipment, rentals, training, member, dispatch wave) — locked custom illustration set
  • Weather + status icons — Lucide icon set under license (accent only; never primary)

Section 3.6 — Photography Style

  • Editorial documentary: working pros in real job environments (no stock photography of "models pretending to be contractors")
  • Natural light preferred; overcast Northeast light is the brand default
  • Color grading: warm, slightly desaturated; no Instagram-trendy filters
  • Subjects: real Sherpa Pros pros + clients with signed model release; no actor-model substitutes
  • Composition: medium-tight shots, not wide vista shots; the work + the worker are the subject
  • File naming + tagging conventions for the Sherpa Pros asset library (Cloudinary or equivalent)

4. Signage Standards (Pages 35–46)

Section 4.1 — Storefront Exterior Signage

  • Approved sign types: channel letters + halo lit (preferred); blade signs for urban locations
  • Wordmark application: full-color primary (corner-illuminated halo) on light-toned building facades; all-white reversed on dark facades
  • Minimum size: SHERPA block 18 inches tall on storefront primary
  • Approved sign vendors: 2–3 corporate-vetted national sign vendors (FastSigns franchise network alternative + Allied Signs + local-market vetted partner per market)
  • Permitting + landlord approval workflow (corporate Brand Lead reviews proposed signage before installation)

Section 4.2 — Interior Wayfinding

  • Member entrance vs receiving dock entrance differentiation
  • Directional signage to: pickup counter, training room, member lounge, restrooms, emergency exits
  • Sign substrate: brushed aluminum + dimensional letters (premium feel; matches editorial brand voice)
  • Universal accessibility per Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) signage requirements

Section 4.3 — Vehicle Wraps + Branded Apparel

  • Vehicle wraps: full-wrap design template available from corporate Brand Lead; 2-color minimum (sky blue + dark navy on white)
  • Branded apparel: corporate-approved vendor (Custom Ink + Bonfire alternatives evaluated per market)
  • Embroidery vs screen print rules per apparel item
  • Hat + jacket + polo + t-shirt + hoodie standard SKU set with corporate-managed reorder cycle

Section 4.4 — Training Event Signage

  • Corporate-supplied training event banner template
  • Sponsor co-branding rules (manufacturer sponsors get 30% banner real estate maximum)
  • Permitted modifications by franchisee: event title, date, time, RSVP link only

5. Member-Facing Materials (Pages 47–58)

Section 5.1 — New Member Welcome Packet

  • Welcome letter from Phyrom (templated; franchisee personalization in PS line only)
  • Member benefits one-pager
  • Hub map + hours
  • Contact card (Hub Manager direct line + email)
  • Branded hat + sticker pack (gift)

Section 5.2 — Training Event Flyers

  • Corporate-supplied template (Adobe InDesign + Canva versions)
  • Permitted modifications: event title, date, time, manufacturer-sponsor logo lockup, RSVP link
  • Distribution: Hub bulletin board, Hub member newsletter, local trade-association partnerships

Section 5.3 — Monthly Member Newsletter

  • Corporate-supplied template (responsive HTML email)
  • Standard sections: Hub Manager note (franchisee-written, Brand-Lead-edited), upcoming training events, new pro members highlight, manufacturer spotlight, code-change bulletin (corporate-supplied), contractor wisdom column (rotating)
  • Send cadence: 2nd Tuesday of each month
  • Brand-Lead review required before send (24-hour turnaround commitment)

Section 5.4 — Hub Bulletin Board

  • Physical bulletin board layout template
  • Required elements: training event calendar, current Sherpa Score leaderboard (top 10 pros at this Hub), monthly member spotlight, code-update bulletin (printed), Hub Manager office hours
  • Refresh cadence: monthly

6. Social Media Co-Op Rules (Pages 59–66)

Section 6.1 — Brand Account vs Personal Account

  • Brand account (Hub-specific): franchisee posts under "Sherpa Pros [City]" handles; corporate template + brand voice; Brand Lead pre-approval required for first 30 posts
  • Personal account: franchisee may post anything legal under personal name + handle; may not use Sherpa Pros wordmark, branded photography, or claim to speak for Sherpa Pros LLC

Section 6.2 — What Franchisee CAN Post Under Brand Account

  • Hub events (training events, member milestones, anniversary, holiday hours)
  • Member spotlight (with member's signed photo release)
  • Manufacturer spotlight (with manufacturer's signed brand co-marketing release)
  • Local community events the Hub sponsors or participates in
  • Local trade association engagement (NHHBA chapter meetings, Habitat for Humanity build days)
  • Hub-Manager-led "tip of the week" with Brand-Lead pre-approval

Section 6.3 — What Franchisee CANNOT Post Under Brand Account

  • Pricing claims (always corporate-controlled)
  • Item 19 financial performance representations (FDD-controlled — federal regulatory exposure)
  • Competitive comparisons (legal exposure)
  • Political content
  • Religious content
  • Controversial industry positions (immigration policy in trades, OSHA enforcement debates, union/non-union polemic) — refer to corporate
  • Any content using a celebrity contractor without corporate co-approval (Item 18 disclosure exposure)

Section 6.4 — Trade-Press Representation Rules

  • Local press inquiries: franchisee may speak to local press about local Hub operations only (member events, community engagement, training events). Cannot speak to corporate strategy, financial performance, franchise system size, or platform technology.
  • Corporate press inquiries: route to corporate Public Relations lead within 24 hours; franchisee does not respond directly.
  • Trade press national publications (Builder Magazine, Pro Magazine, Plumbing & Mechanical, Air Conditioning Heating & Refrigeration News): all responses corporate-controlled.

Section 6.5 — Approved Social Media Platforms

  • Instagram (primary — visual brand)
  • Facebook (community engagement + event RSVPs)
  • LinkedIn (B2B + recruitment)
  • TikTok (Phase 4 Year 2+ — controlled rollout under corporate Brand Lead approval)
  • Twitter/X (corporate only — no franchisee accounts)
  • YouTube (corporate primary; franchisee Hub channels Phase 4 Year 2+ with co-approval)

7. Prohibited Brand Usage Examples (Pages 67–74)

(Per docs/operations/brand-portfolio.md §3.4 prohibited usage section, expanded with case studies)

Section 7.1 — Color Violations

  • Recoloring SHERPA or PROS blocks outside locked palette (NEVER purple, teal, green, holiday recoloring, client-color adaptation, agency-creative-test recoloring)
  • Visual examples: 3 prohibited recolor examples + correction

Section 7.2 — Form Violations

  • Rotating wordmark (not 5°, not 15°, not 90° for vertical signage)
  • Stretching wordmark (no aspect-ratio distortion)
  • Outlining wordmark in stroke (kills the diagonal-seam clarity)
  • Adding drop shadow or 3D effect (against editorial brand voice)
  • Visual examples: 4 prohibited form examples + correction

Section 7.3 — Background Violations

  • Wordmark on busy photographic background without color block layer
  • Wordmark on photo with insufficient contrast
  • Wordmark on competitor-platform color (Angi-purple, Thumbtack-blue, TaskRabbit-green, ServiceTitan-orange-similar)
  • Visual examples: 3 prohibited background examples + correction

Section 7.4 — Voice Violations

  • Marketing copy that uses "gig worker" or "side hustle" (degrades Sherpa Pros' verified-licensed-trade positioning)
  • Marketing copy that frames pros as "labor" instead of as the customer
  • Marketing copy that does not include required FDD disclosure footer (federal regulatory exposure)
  • Examples: 3 prohibited copy examples + correction

Section 7.5 — Co-Branding Violations

  • Co-branding with unverified contractors not in Sherpa Pros pro network
  • Co-branding with manufacturers not on the corporate-approved supplier list
  • Co-branding with competing platforms (any platform on the competing-marketplace list maintained by corporate)

Section 7.6 — Consequence Ladder

Violation Severity Consequence
Minor (typography, low-stakes color) Warning + 14-day cure period
Moderate (background, voice) Warning + 30-day cure period + Brand Lead training session
Major (federal regulatory exposure, competitor co-branding, recoloring) Notice of default + 30-day cure period + audit fee per FDD Item 6
Severe (intentional brand misappropriation, knock-off products under Sherpa branding) Notice of default + termination per FDD Item 17

8. Brand Asset Access (Pages 75–80)

Section 8.1 — Where to Get Brand Assets

  • Sherpa Pros Brand Asset Portal (corporate-hosted; access provisioned at signing)
  • Wordmark files (SVG, PNG, EPS) — full color + 3 single-color variants
  • Logo lockup files (tagline lockup, product lockup, attribution lockup)
  • Color palette swatch files (Adobe ASE, Sketch palette, Figma library)
  • Typography files (Google Fonts links + JetBrains Mono direct download + license documentation)
  • Photography library (curated; updated monthly by corporate Marketing Lead)
  • Iconography (SVG + PNG)
  • Templates: signage, vehicle wrap, apparel, training event flyer, monthly newsletter, social media post, Hub bulletin board

Section 8.2 — Customization Rights

  • Franchisee CAN customize: training event title, date, time, location, sponsor logo lockup; monthly newsletter Hub Manager note (within Brand Lead editing)
  • Franchisee CANNOT customize: wordmark, color palette, typography, photography style, voice + tone, footer disclosure language, icon set

Section 8.3 — Asset Refresh Cadence

  • Wordmark + logo lockup: stable (next major update Phase 5+ at earliest)
  • Color palette: stable (annual review by corporate Brand Lead)
  • Typography: stable (Fraunces + Manrope + JetBrains Mono locked)
  • Photography library: monthly addition of new editorial shoots
  • Templates: quarterly refresh of seasonal templates (training event seasonal themes, holiday hours, summer / winter program variants)
  • Iconography: stable (next major update Phase 5+ at earliest)

Section 8.4 — Reporting Brand Issues

  • Brand violation observed in the wild (a competing platform misuses Sherpa marks, a fraudster impersonates a Hub) — report to corporate Legal within 24 hours
  • Brand confusion in the market (clients ask about a service we don't offer because they saw a misleading ad) — report to corporate Brand Lead within 5 business days
  • Local market opportunity (an unmet brand need) — submit to corporate Marketing Lead via FAC channel

Section 8.5 — Annual Brand Audit

  • Once per year, corporate Brand Lead conducts an audit of every Hub's brand application (signage, social media, member-facing materials, vehicle wraps, apparel)
  • Audit findings shared with franchisee within 30 days; cure period 60 days for minor + moderate findings; immediate cure for major findings
  • Audit findings tracked over time; trend reports shared at Annual Franchisee Summit
