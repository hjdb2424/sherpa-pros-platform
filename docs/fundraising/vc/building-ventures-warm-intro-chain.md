## 1. Building Ventures partner profiles (research, current to 2026-04-22)

### 1.1 Firm summary

Building Ventures is a Boston- and San-Francisco-based venture capital firm founded in 2017 by the former Borealis Ventures built-environment team. Fund II closed September 2022 at $95 million. As of August 2025, the firm has 36 portfolio companies. Top sectors are real estate / construction technology (23 investments) and enterprise applications (20). The firm is a "sapling stage" investor — meaning seed and Series A, but not strictly bound by either label. Stated check size is $100K to $10M, sweet spot $1M. ## 5. Pre-meeting research brief — for the 30-min Building Ventures meeting

> **Read this on the plane to Boston the morning of the meeting.**

### 5.1 Top 3 questions BV partner is likely to ask

Based on Devitte's stated thesis, recent Building Ventures investments, and the standard pre-seed VC due-diligence pattern:

1. **"What's the unfair advantage that makes this defensible against Angi and Thumbtack catching up?"**
   *Answer:* Three layers. (a) The code-intelligence engine — built over three years by a working GC, not licensable, requires constant code-revision tracking. (b) The licensed-only verification at onboarding — Angi cannot retrofit this without losing 80% of their pro side. (c) The founder identity — every contractor I onboard says "this was built by a guy who actually does the work" within their first 60 seconds. None of that is in a software roadmap; it's in a relationship.

2. **"What's your founding-team story and why are you the only one who can build this?"**
   *Answer:* I'm Phyrom, working NH GC (HJD Builders), 12 years buying leads, 2 years building this on nights and weekends. Built the entire stack myself initially — Next.js 16, Stripe Connect, PostGIS, Clerk, Twilio. Now hiring two people with the first round. The platform's code-aware quote validation only works because I knew which line items to check. Nobody from a non-construction background would have built it this way.

3. **"What are the unit economics and what's the path to a real Series A?"**
   *Answer:* 5% take during beta, 10% at scale (per spec §5.2). Average residential job is $4,800; a heat-pump install is $14,000+. Pro subscription $49/mo at scale. PM tier $4 per unit per month decreasing to $1.50 with volume. 10-pro beta cohort hitting roughly 200 jobs/month by month 6 = $96K-$192K Gross Merchandise Value per month at 5% take = $4.8K-$9.6K monthly platform revenue. Series A trigger is ~$1M annual recurring revenue across all three lines + 4-metro coverage + 1,000+ verified pros.

### 5.2 Top 3 questions Phyrom should ask Devitte

This qualifies BV as the right partner — not just signing the first term sheet.

1. **"How do you think about co-investors at the seed stage? Specifically, who would you naturally syndicate with on a residential-marketplace deal — and would you lead or follow?"**
   *Why ask:* Devitte's recent investments co-lead with SignalFire (Joist AI) and Harlem Capital (Faura). If he says "we'd lead and bring NFX or Pear in" that signals a $1.5M+ round structure. If he says "we'd follow another lead" that signals a smaller commitment and Phyrom should keep talking to other firms.

2. **"Of your portfolio companies, which one was the worst-performing in year 1 that you stuck with anyway, and what made you stick?"**
   *Why ask:* This question reveals Devitte's actual founder-support behavior under stress. The answer separates "VCs who ghost when traction stalls" from "VCs who add value in the trough." If he can't name one or his answer is generic, that's a red flag.

3. **"If we close at $500K-$1M now, what does the bridge look like to a $3M Series A in 12-18 months — what milestones matter most to you?"**
   *Why ask:* This is the test of whether Devitte will sign first and disappear, or actually own the runway plan. Specific milestone callouts (GMV, take-rate, cohort retention, geography expansion) signal he has a real plan. Vague answers signal he's deal-spraying.

## 6. Decision criteria for Phyrom — how to read the meeting in real time

### 6.1 Signals that BV is the right anchor (move fast, prepare term sheet conversation)

Watch for any **two or more** of these in a single 30-min meeting:

- **Devitte (or whoever takes the meeting) returned the meeting request inside 48 hours of the warm intro landing.** (Speed is signal.)
- **He references a *specific* Sherpa Pros feature during the meeting** — e.g., "the code-checked badge on the homeowner-facing quote" or "the Mass Save heat-pump specialty lane." This means he visited sherpa-pros-platform.vercel.app before the call.
- **He brings up a portfolio company *unprompted* as an analog.** ("This reminds me of how BuildFactory thinks about prefab.") Means he's already pattern-matched.
- **He asks about co-investors *during* the meeting.** ("Have you talked to NFX yet?") Means he's mentally syndicating.
- **He offers a follow-up *during* the meeting** — second call, partner team intro, customer reference. Means he's moving inside his fund.
- **He asks for the deck *after* the meeting.** Means he plans to circulate it internally — the most reliable diligence signal at the seed stage.
- **He volunteers a timeline.** ("If we move on this, what's your close date?") Means he's pricing the deal already.

### 6.2 Signals BV is interested but not the lead (run them in parallel; don't anchor)

- **60-day delay to follow-up.** Means partner team has not converged on conviction.
- **Requests for "more data" without specifying what data.** Means the partner is unsure what would change his mind — which usually means it won't change.
- **"Would love to see this with co-investors lined up"** = he wants someone else to lead. Means he'll write a follow check, not a lead check.
- **Generic LinkedIn engagement post-meeting** ("nice to chat, stay in touch") with no specific next step. Means it's a "maintain optionality" not a "we're moving."
- **Asks Phyrom to re-pitch to a non-investing partner** (the Head of Marketing, the Venture Partner, etc.) — means the GP isn't yet championing internally.

If two or more of these appear: keep BV warm, but accelerate Brick & Mortar (Tier 1 #2) and Foundamental (Tier 1 #3) immediately.

### 6.3 Signals to pass — move on to Brick & Mortar / Foundamental immediately

- **Questions focus on flaws not opportunity.** ("How will you handle 1099 vs W-2 misclassification risk?" "What about insurance liability for unlicensed pros slipping through?") Note: these are valid questions, but if they consume more than 50% of the meeting, the partner is hunting reasons to pass, not reasons to invest.
- **No follow-up within 2 weeks of meeting**, despite Phyrom sending the deck and 1-pager promptly afterward.
- **Generic "stay in touch" close** with no specific calendar follow-up offered.
- **He references a competing portfolio company as a *direct competitor***, not an analog. ("We already have BuildFactory in the marketplace lane; we're concentrated.") Means BV believes its marketplace bet is placed.
- **He suggests Phyrom is "too early"** without specifying what milestone would make him "ready." Means it's a polite no.

If two or more of these appear: write a clean follow-up email thanking Devitte, ask for one introduction to a peer firm (this is a low-cost ask he'll usually grant), and move on. Do not chase. Do not re-pitch in 90 days. The window is open or closed, not stuck.
