2. Coverage Spec Sheet (Broker-Readable Format)

2.1 Business Description

Sherpa Pros LLC is a New Hampshire-domiciled, technology-enabled marketplace connecting residential homeowners and property managers with licensed trade contractors for on-demand and project-based work. Our service categories include electrical, plumbing, Heating Ventilation and Air Conditioning (HVAC), general contracting, painting, landscaping, roofing, and specialty trades (Mass Save heat-pump installers, Electric Vehicle (EV) charger electricians, plaster, slate, masonry). Every contractor on the platform must hold an active state license in their trade and must upload a current Certificate of Insurance naming Sherpa Pros LLC as Additional Insured before accepting any work.

The platform was built by a working New Hampshire General Contractor (HJD Builders LLC) and differentiates from existing lead-generation platforms (Angi, Thumbtack, TaskRabbit, Handy) on three structural dimensions: (1) licensed-only contractor pool, (2) code-aware quote validation that runs every customer scope against the National Electrical Code, International Residential Code, Massachusetts Electrical Code, and New Hampshire RSA before any bid is accepted, and (3) a marketplace transaction model (pros get matched and paid for jobs) rather than a lead-gen model (pros pay to bid).

3. Discovery Questions for Each Broker Intro Call

Specific, vendor-knowledgeable questions — not generic small-business intake. Ask each broker the same eight, score the answers, fill the decision framework table.

  1. Minimum-volume requirement for marketplace Tech Errors & Omissions coverage — what's the smallest book you'll write a marketplace endorsement for? Pre-revenue OK, or do you need a trailing-twelve-month gross merchandise volume floor before underwriters engage? (For Embroker specifically: is your Startup Package gated to Series A + funding only, or is there an early-stage track?)

  2. Other construction-services marketplace clients — do you currently broker for any home-services, contractor, or construction-marketplace platforms (e.g., Houzz Pro, BuildZoom, Sweeten, Steady, Jobber, ServiceTitan-distributed marketplaces)? May we reference 1–2 named clients of similar scale to ours? Without that, we're paying you to learn our risk on someone else's claim history.

  3. Marketplace endorsement structure — is the marketplace carve-out a separate Marketplace Liability policy, a rider on the Tech Errors & Omissions policy, or a Commercial General Liability endorsement? What carrier underwrites it (Hiscox, Travelers, Beazley, Tokio Marine HCC, others)? What exclusions does the standard endorsement carry that we should negotiate out?

  4. Premium credit for code-validation-driven claims-frequency reduction — our platform validates every customer scope against National Electrical Code, International Residential Code, Massachusetts Electrical, and New Hampshire RSA before any bid is accepted. We can produce timestamped validation logs as part of the evidence chain on every job. Is there a credit available for that, or is the underwriter going to treat us as a generic marketplace? What evidence do they want to see to grant the credit?

  5. Multi-state expansion handling — Phase 0 is NH/ME/MA, Phase 1 is all New England, Phase 3 is national. Does our policy form scale automatically as we add states, or does each new state require an endorsement, separate filing, or premium recalculation? Worst-case: are there states (e.g., New York, California) where the marketplace endorsement won't be admitted and we need a separate non-admitted policy?

  6. Claims management when both Sherpa Pros and the contractor are named — when a homeowner sues both the contractor (primary Commercial General Liability target) and Sherpa Pros (deep-pocket secondary target), how does your claims team coordinate defense between the two carriers? Do you have a tender-and-defend protocol that pushes back on Sherpa Pros being kept on the caption when our marketplace endorsement should kick us off it? Who pays the defense cost during the coordination period?

  7. Premium financing options if Phase 0 bind happens before first close — we're running parallel non-dilutive grant + Wefunder + accelerator + Venture Capital tracks. First close is 60–90 days out. Can we monthly-pay the premium, premium-finance through a platform like Imperial PFS or Gateway, or quarterly-pay with a deposit? Or do we have to pay annually upfront?

  8. Year-2 and Year-3 renewal pricing trajectory — if we hit our Phase 1 plan (50 pros, 200 jobs/month, $200K gross merchandise volume) by Month 6 and Phase 2 (200 pros, 1,000 jobs/month, $1M gross merchandise volume) by Month 12, what's the rough premium at renewal? What's the worst-case multiple if we have one Tech Errors & Omissions claim in Year 1? What's the best-case if we have zero claims and the validation log proves our claims-frequency theory?

Bonus question (only ask if call is going well): We're also designing a Phase 1 consumer-paid embedded-insurance product (Project Protection at checkout — Standard free / Plus $89 / Premium $249, with $25K to $100K caps). Would your firm have appetite to either underwrite that directly or partner us with a Managing General Agent that does (Cover Genius, Boost Insurance, Sure)?
