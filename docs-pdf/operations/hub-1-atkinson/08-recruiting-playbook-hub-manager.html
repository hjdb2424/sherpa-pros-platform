Hub #1 Atkinson NH - Hub Manager Recruiting Playbook

Purpose

Operational playbook for sourcing, screening, and closing the Hub Manager #1 hire. Companion to 07-hub-manager-job-spec.md.

Critical-path target: Signed offer by Phase 1 Month 4. On-floor by Phase 1 Month 5.

1. Target Source List (12+ Specific Sources)

A. FW Webb branch managers (NH/MA primary, ME secondary)

The single best source. FW Webb is the partnership target for Hubs #2-#10 (per integration spec section 4); a Hub Manager who already knows the FW Webb operational model is uniquely valuable.

Specific NH branches to target:

  • FW Webb Manchester NH
  • FW Webb Salem NH
  • FW Webb Concord NH
  • FW Webb Portsmouth NH
  • FW Webb Nashua NH (border)

Specific MA branches (commute-feasible to Atkinson):

  • FW Webb Lawrence MA
  • FW Webb Methuen MA
  • FW Webb Tewksbury MA
  • FW Webb Haverhill MA
  • FW Webb North Andover MA

Targets within each branch: Branch Manager + Assistant Branch Manager + Counter Manager. LinkedIn search "FW Webb" + "branch manager" + "New Hampshire" / "Massachusetts."

B. Lowe's Pro Desk leads (NE region)

Lowe's Pro Desk is structurally similar to Hub will-call counter. Pro Desk leads are accustomed to professional contractor customers (vs. consumer DIY).

  • Lowe's Salem NH Pro Desk
  • Lowe's Plaistow NH Pro Desk
  • Lowe's Nashua NH Pro Desk
  • Lowe's Methuen MA Pro Desk
  • Lowe's Haverhill MA Pro Desk

LinkedIn search: "Lowe's" + "Pro Desk" + "New Hampshire" / "Massachusetts."

C. Home Depot Pro leads (NE region)

Same logic as Lowe's. Home Depot Pro is the larger of the two and has more experienced leads in the NH/MA market.

  • Home Depot Salem NH
  • Home Depot Plaistow NH (closest to Atkinson)
  • Home Depot Methuen MA
  • Home Depot Lawrence MA
  • Home Depot Haverhill MA
  • Home Depot Tewksbury MA

LinkedIn search: "Home Depot" + "Pro" + "New Hampshire" / "Massachusetts."

D. Independent supply-house counter managers

Plumbing supply (besides FW Webb): Davis & Warshow, Granite Group, Aubuchon Hardware Pro Plumbing departments. Electrical supply: Granite City Electric, Northeast Electrical Distributors, North Coast Electric. HVAC supply: RE Michel, Johnstone Supply.

  • Granite Group plumbing branches in NH/MA (5 branches)
  • Granite City Electric branches in NH (3 branches)
  • RE Michel HVAC branches in NH/MA
  • Local independent supply houses (Atkinson Lumber, Plaistow Plumbing & Electric, etc.)

E. NHHBA member companies (NH Home Builders Association)

Some NHHBA member companies (mid-size GCs and trade subs) have branch / division managers who may be open to a stable salaried operations role outside field work. These candidates often have construction-trade fluency that supply-house candidates lack.

  • Pull NHHBA member directory
  • Identify 8-12 mid-size NH GC and trade-sub companies
  • Reach out via NHHBA networking events + Phyrom's existing NHHBA relationships

F. Manchester NH Chamber of Commerce + ABC NH

Manchester Chamber + Associated Builders & Contractors (ABC) NH chapter both have membership rosters that overlap with construction-adjacent operational professionals.

  • Manchester Chamber of Commerce member directory
  • ABC NH member directory + chapter event attendance

3. Sourcing Cadence (Weekly Targets)

Phase 1 Month 2 - Source build

  • Week 1: Build LinkedIn target list (50+ named candidates across categories A-F)
  • Week 2: Send first wave of 25 outreach messages
  • Week 3: Send second wave of 25 outreach messages
  • Week 4: Follow up with non-responders + post job on LinkedIn / Indeed / NHHBA board

Phase 1 Month 3 - Phone screens

  • Week 1: Phone screen all responding candidates (target: 8-12 phone screens completed)
  • Week 2: Continue phone screens + advance top 3-5 to on-site
  • Week 3: On-site interviews top 3 candidates
  • Week 4: Reference checks on finalist

Phase 1 Month 4 - Offer + close

  • Week 1: Offer extended to finalist
  • Week 2-4: Negotiate + close + signed offer
  • If finalist declines: pivot to second-choice candidate immediately, offer Week 3

4. Phone Screen Question Bank (30 min)

Opening (5 min)

  1. Walk me through your current role - what does a typical day look like?
  2. What drew you to consider this Sherpa Hub role?

Operational depth (10 min)

  1. Tell me about a time you made a P&L decision that had a measurable impact - what was the lever and what was the result?
  2. How do you manage inventory shrink in a counter-and-warehouse environment?
  3. Walk me through how you handle a short-shipment from a key supplier on a customer-promised delivery date.
  4. How do you train a new associate on a counter / warehouse role - what's your day-1, day-5, day-30 framework?

Customer + community (5 min)

  1. Tell me about your strongest professional contractor customer relationship and what makes it work.
  2. What would you do if a key member is upset about an inventory stockout that was your fault?

Compensation + fit (5 min)

  1. What's the salary range you're targeting?
  2. What's your earliest available start date?

Wrap (5 min)

  1. What questions do you have for me?

5. On-Site Interview Question Bank (3 hr)

Tour + observation (45 min)

  • Walk through Hub buildout site - watch what candidate notices
  • Ask: "If you were running this Hub day 1, what would you change about this layout?"

Operational scenario interviews (90 min, 3 scenarios at 30 min each)

Scenario 1: Materials dispatch failure "It's 5:45am. Your 6:30am Uber Direct wave is supposed to leave with materials for 4 jobs. Your dock-staged inventory shows you're short 1 critical item for the highest-revenue job of the day. Your supplier (FW Webb) doesn't open until 7:00am. Walk me through the next 90 minutes."

Looking for: triage, communication (pro on the affected job), backup-supplier thinking, escalation, recovery action, documentation for post-mortem.

Scenario 2: Member-conflict resolution "A long-time Founding Pro just walked into the Hub at 11am, visibly upset. He paid for a kit that was supposed to be ready at 8am, and his crew has been sitting at the job site for 3 hours. Walk me through how you handle the next 5 minutes."

Looking for: emotional regulation, customer focus, accountability, recovery action (refund, credit, expedite), and follow-up plan.

Scenario 3: P&L pressure "Three months in, your Hub is running 30% under revenue target and 15% over opex target. Phyrom asks you to identify $2,000/month in opex cuts without breaking member NPS. Walk me through your first 5 candidate cuts and how you'd evaluate each."

Looking for: structured thinking, business-case construction, member-impact awareness, willingness to make hard calls.

P&L + strategy (30 min)

  • Show candidate the integration spec section 9 Hub P&L model
  • Ask: "Where do you see the biggest revenue lever and the biggest cost risk?"

Founder + culture fit (15 min)

  • Phyrom personal-fit conversation
  • Long-term arc: "Where do you want to be in 3 years?" - listen for whether candidate sees themselves growing into Operations Director / multi-Hub role

6. Reference Check Template (3 references, 30 min each)

Hi [Reference], I'm checking references for [Candidate] who's interviewing for a Hub Manager role with us at Sherpa Pros. Do you have ~30 minutes to chat?

1. How long did you work with [Candidate] and what was your reporting relationship?
2. What were [Candidate]'s top 2 strengths?
3. What were 1-2 areas where [Candidate] had room to grow?
4. Walk me through a specific situation where [Candidate] demonstrated P&L ownership.
5. How did [Candidate] handle a difficult conversation - either with a customer, a supplier, or a direct report?
6. Did [Candidate] ever miss a critical commitment? How did they handle it?
7. Inventory accuracy + shrink - how did [Candidate] manage that?
8. Would you hire [Candidate] again? In what role?
9. Is there anything I should know that I haven't asked?

Thanks - this is very helpful. Confidential of course.

7. Decision Framework

Green flags:

  • 5+ years branch / department management with demonstrable P&L
  • Specific shrink-control + inventory-accuracy anecdotes
  • Self-aware hard-conversation handling
  • Energized by founding-team responsibility
  • NH-rooted (commute is sustainable, community ties)

Yellow flags:

  • Long tenure but no clear P&L ownership
  • Excellent counter / customer skills but no team management
  • Salary expectations 20%+ above range (probe for negotiability)

Red flags (pass):

  • No P&L responsibility
  • Career trajectory shows pattern of 1-2 year jumps
  • Reference checks reveal accountability avoidance
  • Compensation expectations 30%+ above range with no flexibility
  • Cannot articulate why this role vs. their current role

8. Backup Plan if Search Stalls

If by Phase 1 Month 4 no offer is signed:

Option 1: Extend search 30 days

  • Phyrom personally takes Hub Manager interim role for first 60 days of operations
  • Continue search with broader geographic range (open to relocation candidates from MA, ME, VT)
  • Increase salary range to $85-95K to widen pipeline

Option 2: Hire interim Hub Manager (contractor)

  • Engage a contractor / interim manager from Robert Half or a construction-ops-specific recruiting firm for 90 days
  • Use the 90 days to continue full-time hire search at the right level
  • Soft-open proceeds on schedule

Option 3: Phyrom runs the Hub for 90 days

  • Acceptable Phase 1 contingency given Phyrom is on-site at HJD HQ
  • Adds load to Phyrom but keeps soft-open + grand-open on schedule
  • Continue hire search in parallel
  • Hub Manager hired by end of Phase 1 Month 6 / start of Phase 2
