## 3. THE PROBLEM

### Lead-gen platforms waste contractors' money. Massachusetts has the worst trades labor shortage in the country.

The home-services platforms most homeowners try first are built on a broken model. Contractors pay to bid on leads. Homeowners get matched to whoever paid the most or responded fastest. Licensed work gets handled by people who shouldn't be doing it.

**The four big platforms — what's actually wrong with them:**

- **Angi (NASDAQ: ANGI).** $1.03B in FY2025 revenue, down 13% year-over-year. **41% service-quality inconsistency** in user-reported reviews. **Not BBB-accredited.** **1,800+ BBB complaints 2023–2026.** Trustpilot 2.1 out of 5. **Vermont Attorney General settled with Angi in October 2025 for $100K** over misleading "Certified Pro" terminology. Contractors paying $200/month for the Pro subscription plus $30–$100 per shared lead, often shared with up to 40 competitors. ([Source: Adapt Digital 2026 platform comparison](https://adaptdigitalsolutions.com/articles/homeadvisor-vs-angieslist-vs-houzz-vs-porch-vs-thumbtack-vs-yelp-vs-bark/) · [Angi BBB profile](https://www.bbb.org/us/in/indianapolis/profile/contractor-referral/angi-0382-3041007/complaints))

- **Thumbtack.** Private company, ~$400M FY2024 revenue. **Documented deposit-theft cases of $300–$6,500.** **BBB pattern-of-complaints review completed November 2025.** Widely-reported fake leads (invalid phone numbers, non-existent customers). ([Source: Thumbtack BBB Complaints page](https://www.bbb.org/us/ca/san-francisco/profile/internet-service/thumbtack-inc-1116-367066/complaints))

- **TaskRabbit (acquired by IKEA 2017).** **22.5% effective platform fee** (15% service + 7.5% trust/support). Doesn't cover electrical, plumbing, HVAC, or roofing. Last-minute cancellation problems. ([Source: TaskRabbit business model breakdown](https://oyelabs.com/taskrabbit-business-model/))

- **Handy (acquired by Angi parent for $165.5M in 2018).** Limited trade coverage. No code intelligence. Strategic deprioritization since acquisition.

**And then it gets worse.** Massachusetts has **the largest construction labor shortage in the nation** — 2.5% construction unemployment, the lowest in 17+ years. **30% of MA residential construction workers are 55+** (vs. 26% nationally). Project completion times have stretched from 7 to 11 months. Carpenters, electricians, and roofers are the most acute shortages — and those are exactly the licensed trades the existing platforms can't filter for. ([Source: WBJournal — MA largest construction worker shortage in the nation](https://wbjournal.com/article/help-needed-massachusetts-has-the-largest-construction-worker-shortage-in-the-nation/) · [Bisnow — Boston construction labor shortage worsens](https://www.bisnow.com/boston/news/construction-development/as-bostons-construction-labor-shortage-persists-firms-look-to-new-solutions-126075))

The market exists. The demand is there. The platforms aren't matching the licensed labor that's left.

## 4. THE SOLUTION

### Jobs, not leads. Licensed only. Code-aware. Permit-aware. Rebate-aware.

Sherpa Pros is the licensed-trade marketplace that thinks like a contractor. Built by a working GC. Four things no lead-gen platform does — and none of them can copy without rebuilding from scratch:

**1. Jobs, not leads.** Contractors get paid for completed work, not for the chance to bid. **5% take rate during beta. 10% at scale.** That's roughly half of what a contractor pays Angi all-in today. Homeowners post a job once. The platform dispatches the right licensed pro. No bidding war. No lead-share fatigue.

**2. Licensed only.** License and insurance verified at onboarding. Re-checked at renewal. No "find a guy" matchmaking. If the trade requires a license in NH, ME, or MA, the pro on the platform has it.

**3. Code-aware quote validation.** Every quote is checked against the relevant code base — NEC for electrical, IRC for general construction, MA Electrical Code, NH state code (RSA), Boston ISD permits — **before the homeowner sees the quote.** The platform catches missing line items, wrong materials, undersized panels, and code violations. Homeowner sees a green "code-checked" badge. The contractor saves the rework. The homeowner doesn't get burned.

**4. Permit-aware and rebate-aware.** The platform knows which jobs need a Boston ISD permit, which qualify for **Mass Save heat-pump rebates** ($8,500 max in 2026), which qualify for **National Grid Turnkey EV charger** installation rebates, and walks both sides through the paperwork.

**Built by a contractor — that's the moat.** The code-intelligence layer required a working GC plus a 3-year codebase to build. National lead-gen platforms can't copy it without a structural rebuild. They have no incentive — their entire P&L runs on lead-share fees. Ours doesn't.

## 11. RISKS

### Required SEC risk disclosures — read carefully.

This is not the complete list. **The full risk disclosures are in the Form C filed with the SEC and in the offering page footer.** You should read both before investing.

### 11.1 Risks specific to early-stage investing

- **You can lose your entire investment.** Most early-stage startups fail.
- **Your investment is illiquid.** You cannot sell your SAFE for at least one year (per SEC Reg CF rules), and likely much longer in practice — there is no active secondary market for unconverted SAFEs.
- **The valuation is set by the founder, not by an independent third party.** The cap is based on comparables and judgment, not an audited valuation. (See FAQ Q23 for the methodology.)
- **Your SAFE could convert into a class of stock with no voting rights, no dividends, and limited information rights.**
- **Future fundraises will dilute your future ownership percentage.** This is normal for any growing startup but should be expected.

### 11.2 Risks specific to marketplaces

- **Marketplace liquidity risk.** Two-sided marketplaces are notoriously hard to bootstrap. Both supply (licensed pros) and demand (homeowners) must scale together. If supply outpaces demand, pros leave. If demand outpaces supply, homeowners can't get matched. **The founder's primary mitigation:** start small with a curated 10-pro beta cohort in a metro the founder knows personally (Northern Triangle: Portsmouth, Manchester, Portland), then expand only after liquidity proves out.
- **Take-rate compression risk.** Competitors could cut their effective take rate to match Sherpa Pros' 5–10% range. Angi, Thumbtack, and Houzz have entire P&Ls built on lead-fee economics — they cannot easily switch — but other entrants could. **Mitigation:** the code-intelligence layer is the real moat, not the take rate. Take rate is the price; the code work is the product.

### 11.3 Risks specific to licensed-trade work

- **Regulatory risk on contractor classification (1099 vs. W-2).** Various state attorneys general (including in MA) have challenged gig-platform contractor classifications. Sherpa Pros' pros are independently licensed contractors with their own insurance — defensible — but a regulatory action could force re-structure. **Mitigation:** written legal opinion on 1099 vs. W-2 commissioned in Phase 0 Week 4 (see GTM spec Risk R5).
- **Liability risk on completed work.** If a pro damages a homeowner's property or causes injury, the homeowner could attempt to involve Sherpa Pros as a defendant. **Mitigation:** Platform liability insurance (~$800/year), 7-day Stripe escrow hold, 4.5-star rating floor, full pro license/insurance verification at onboarding.
- **Fraud risk on either side.** Pro fraud (no-shows, deposit-theft, code violations) or client fraud (chargebacks, false claims) is endemic in the industry. **Mitigation:** Stripe Connect 7-day escrow hold, dispute resolution workflow, pro deactivation on confirmed fraud.

### 11.4 Risks specific to competitive pressure

- **Incumbent response.** Angi, Thumbtack, or Houzz could launch a "licensed-only" or "Mass Save certified" feature. They've shown no urgency to do so because it would require rebuilding their lead-fee model — but the risk is real. **Mitigation:** speed to market in NE (founder is local; competitors are corporate), USPTO trademark on "Old-House Verified," and exclusive utility partnerships (Mass Save Network application; National Grid Turnkey relationship).
- **New entrant.** A well-funded new entrant could attempt the same positioning. **Mitigation:** the code-intelligence layer is a multi-year build, the founder-as-GC narrative is unforgeable, and the Northern Triangle beachhead is too small to attract well-funded national entrants until we're already established.

### 11.5 Risks specific to this raise

- **The Wefunder round may not hit its minimum target.** If we don't hit $250K within 90 days, all investor funds are returned. The company would then rely on the other three parallel funding tracks (grants, accelerators, VCs) to trigger Phase 1.
- **The valuation cap in this offering is a starting point.** Subsequent priced rounds may be at a much lower valuation in a down-market scenario, in which case the 20% discount controls — but you would still own less of a less-valuable company. (See FAQ Q18.)
- **Founder is solo with a 60-hour weekly cap.** Burnout risk is real. **Mitigation:** AI agent leverage on lower-priority work, Upwork US contractors for offload (~10–15 hr/wk SDR + ~10 hr/wk content), hard 60-hour cap, Phase 1 hires close the gap. (See GTM spec Risk R8.)
