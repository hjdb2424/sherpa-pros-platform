## 1. Ideal Customer Profile (15+ pros)

### 1.1 Project mode (10–12 pros) — code-aware, multi-bid, $1,500+ jobs

#### 1.1.1 General Contractor #1 (NH Seacoast)
- **License:** NH does not require a state GC license; instead requires a town-level building permit + (if working >$1K residential) a written contract per RSA 358-Q. Verified by HJD network reputation.
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability minimum, $500K workers' comp (if W-2 staff)
- **Years in business:** 5+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $25K–$150K (kitchen, addition, basement, full renovation)
- **Personality signals (recruit-friendly):** complains about Angi/Thumbtack lead waste, has a sub network they manage themselves, mid-career (10–25 yrs in trade), already runs QBO, has a website but isn't slick
- **Why they sign:** Tired of paying for leads that go nowhere. Wants jobs, not bid sheets.

#### 1.1.2 General Contractor #2 (NH Manchester / Greater Manchester)
- Same as 1.1.1 but Greater Manchester / Concord / Nashua metro
- Greater Manchester/Nashua/Concord Area Home Builders Association (GMNHBRA) member preferred
- Bonus signal: NHHBA member badge

#### 1.1.3 Plumber #1 (NH Seacoast — master license)
- **License:** NH Master Plumber License (NH OPLC Mechanical Safety & Licensing Board, 6+ months as journeyman + exam, $330 fee). Look for license # on truck, website footer, or NH OPLC lookup.
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability, $1M auto, workers' comp
- **Years in business:** 5+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $200–$8K (small repair to full re-pipe). 70% jobs <$2K.
- **Personality signals:** Owner-operator or 1–3 trucks. Answers his own phone. Has a Bill Trombly-style "License #1 in NH" pride angle. Active on local Facebook contractor groups.
- **Why they sign:** Drain cleaning is volume game; small-job lead-gen costs $40–$75 per lead with 1-in-5 close rate on Angi.

#### 1.1.4 Plumber #2 (NH Manchester / Concord — master license)
- Same as 1.1.3 but Manchester/Concord metro
- Will likely cover BOTH Project + Quick Job lanes (toilet installs, valve replacements)

#### 1.1.5 HVAC / Heat Pump Specialist (NH or MA — Mass Save HPIN certified)
- **License:** NH or MA HVAC license + EPA 608 cert + Mass Save Heat Pump Installer Network (HPIN) certification (the gold ticket — $10K+ rebates per home)
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability, $1M auto, $500K workers' comp
- **Years in business:** 5+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $12K–$25K (mini-split or whole-home heat pump install)
- **Personality signals:** Mitsubishi Diamond Elite or Rheem Pro Partner badge. Active Mass Save listing. Talks about rebates fluently.
- **Why they sign:** Mass Save lead pipeline is 3–6 month wait — they need a faster funnel for ready-to-buy homeowners.

#### 1.1.6 Painter (full-project, ME Portland metro)
- **License:** ME doesn't require painter license, but lead-paint-certified (EPA RRP) for pre-1978 homes — almost mandatory in Portland's old housing stock
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability, $500K workers' comp
- **Years in business:** 5+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $4K–$25K (full-house repaint, exterior, multi-room interior). Project mode only — won't take single-wall work.
- **Personality signals:** Has portfolio website, posts before/afters on Instagram, charges by the project (not hourly), references-on-request
- **Why they sign:** Project-mode painters lose to low-bid hacks who skip prep — code-aware quote validation defends their bid.

#### 1.1.7 Landscaper (ME Portland or NH Seacoast)
- **License:** ME doesn't require landscaper license; NH same. Pesticide applicator license if spraying. ICPI / NCMA paver cert is a strong signal.
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability, $1M auto, workers' comp
- **Years in business:** 5+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $5K–$50K (design-build, hardscape, irrigation install). Seasonal — Apr–Nov peak.
- **Personality signals:** Family-owned, multi-generational, has a yard/equipment lot. Doesn't do mowing-only.
- **Why they sign:** Seasonal pipeline pressure — need a steady January–March booking funnel for spring start.

#### 1.1.8 Licensed Electrician (MA Boston metro — Mass Save / EV / panel)
- **License:** MA Master Electrician (Class A) or Journeyman (Class B). Mass Save HPIN-affiliated electrician is gold (panel upgrades for heat pumps).
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability, $1M auto, workers' comp
- **Years in business:** 5+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $1.5K–$8K (panel upgrade, EV Level 2 charger, knob-and-tube replacement)
- **Personality signals:** Tesla certified installer badge, Charge Home Solutions-style EV focus, has done Newton/Brookline panel upgrades before, knows the Boston ISD permit dance
- **Why they sign:** EV + panel + Mass Save backlog is enormous in Boston — they're turning work away because their lead pipeline is chaotic.

#### 1.1.9 Old-House Specialist (MA Boston / Brookline)
- **License:** MA Construction Supervisor License (CSL) — Unrestricted (1–4 family) or Restricted. CSL # is public via mass.gov/dpl lookup.
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability, $1M auto, workers' comp
- **Years in business:** 10+ years (hard requirement — old-house work needs scarred experience)
- **Avg ticket:** $15K–$200K (plaster restoration, slate roof, brownstone repointing, historic kitchen)
- **Personality signals:** Talks about horsehair plaster, calcimine ceilings, ship-lap clapboards without prompting. Has Brookline/Cambridge/Back Bay project portfolio.
- **Why they sign:** No platform filters for old-house work. Their leads are word-of-mouth — slow, lumpy, hard to scale.

#### 1.1.10 Roofer (MA Boston / Newton — slate + asphalt)
- **License:** MA Home Improvement Contractor (HIC) registration + MA CSL. Slate work needs the experienced kind.
- **Insurance:** $1M general liability, $1M auto, workers' comp
- **Years in business:** 10+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $8K–$45K (asphalt re-roof) or $20K–$120K (slate restoration)
- **Personality signals:** Family-owned 30+ years, NRCA member, OSHA-trained crew, slate or copper portfolio
- **Why they sign:** Slate work is high-margin but rare leads — old-house specialty filter unlocks consistent pipeline.

### 1.2 Quick Job mode (4–5 pros) — pre-priced, fast-match, $200–$1,500 jobs

#### 1.2.1 Dedicated Handyman (MA Boston)
- **License:** MA HIC registration (required >$1K). General handyman license not required for non-trade work.
- **Insurance:** $500K general liability minimum (Quick Job CGL threshold per `quick-job-lane.md` §8)
- **Years in business:** 3+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $150–$600 (TV mount, drywall patch, door knob, smoke detector)
- **Personality signals:** Same-day response, Yelp 4.5+, owner-operator, takes credit cards, phone-first
- **Why they sign:** TaskRabbit takes 35% — Sherpa Pros at 5% beta is a 7× better deal.

#### 1.2.2 Small-Project Painter (NH Seacoast or ME)
- **License:** EPA RRP lead cert if working pre-1978 homes
- **Insurance:** $500K general liability
- **Years in business:** 3+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $200–$900 (single accent wall, single room, touch-ups)
- **Personality signals:** Works alone or with one helper, prices per job (not hourly), lives near service area
- **Why they sign:** Volume play — small jobs are 5–10× more frequent than full repaints, and Quick Job dispatch fills slow weeks.

#### 1.2.3 Light Plumber (NH Manchester or Portsmouth)
- **License:** NH Master or Journeyman Plumber (small jobs still need the license per RSA 153)
- **Insurance:** $500K general liability minimum (Quick Job tier); $1M preferred
- **Years in business:** 3+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $150–$500 (faucet swap, valve replacement, garbage disposal install, fill valve repair)
- **Personality signals:** Owner-operator, single truck, "no job too small" tagline, fast emergency response, 24/7 availability
- **Why they sign:** Service-call work is bread-and-butter; Sherpa Pros pre-priced flat-rate menu eliminates haggling and same-day pay (24-hr Stripe hold) beats Net 30.

#### 1.2.4 Light Electrician (MA Boston suburbs — Newton / Brookline / Somerville)
- **License:** MA Journeyman or Master Electrician
- **Insurance:** $500K general liability minimum; $1M preferred
- **Years in business:** 3+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $125–$400 (ceiling fan, GFCI outlet, light fixture swap, smart switch install)
- **Personality signals:** Sole-prop or 2-person shop, takes 1–3 jobs/day, prefers small fast work over big project chaos
- **Why they sign:** Small electrical work has near-zero advertising — they survive on referrals. Sherpa Pros gives them a steady pre-qualified queue.

#### 1.2.5 Move-In-Ready Handyman (PM tier crossover, Boston or Portsmouth)
- **License:** MA HIC or NH equivalent (per state)
- **Insurance:** $500K general liability minimum; $1M if PM work
- **Years in business:** 5+ years
- **Avg ticket:** $300–$1,200 per turn (paint touch-up, drywall patch, smoke/CO replacement, faucet, lock change)
- **Personality signals:** Already does turnover work for 1–3 small property managers, can do 2–3 turns/day, has a small van and crew of 1
- **Why they sign:** PM tier ($4 → $1.50/unit) is the play — wants to be the "preferred vendor" for a 200-unit PM portfolio without juggling 40 spreadsheets.

## 6. Demo Flow Checklist (30-min Zoom or in-person)

Phyrom runs every demo personally per Workstream B Task B2 Step 3.

**Pre-demo (Phyrom prep, 5 min):**
- [ ] Confirm pro's trade, metro, license type
- [ ] Pull up sample job in their trade in their metro (use mock data if no live job yet)
- [ ] Pin Stripe Connect onboarding URL ready to share

**Demo flow (30 min):**

- [ ] **(2 min) Founder hook:** "Hi, I'm Phyrom. I run HJD Builders out of NH. I built this because Angi was wasting our time. I am looking at fifteen pros to be the founding cohort. Today I want to show you what I built and ask if you want one of those spots."

- [ ] **(5 min) Post a job:** Share screen, walk through the client side. "Here is what your client sees. They post the job, the system already knows the building code for their address."

- [ ] **(5 min) Match:** Show Dispatch matching the job to the pro in <2 minutes. "Within 2 hours, every job gets matched to 3 licensed pros. You are one of them. No paying for leads. The job comes to you."

- [ ] **(5 min) Bid + quote:** Pro views the job, submits a bid. Show the code-aware quote validation flag a missing scope item. "This is the part Angi cannot do. Every quote is checked against your state and town code before the client sees it. So you stop losing jobs to a guy who left half the work out of his bid."

- [ ] **(5 min) Close + Stripe payout:** Show client accepting bid → payment protection → completion → Stripe payout in 24–48 hr (Quick Job) or 7 day (Project). "Money hits your bank in days, not Net 30."

- [ ] **(3 min) Founding Pro benefits:** Half-price forever (5%), no $49 sub, direct line to me, marketing exposure, 90-day commitment with mutual termination.

- [ ] **(5 min) Q&A + close:** Answer trade-specific questions. Then: **"Sign the agreement now and I will have you live by Friday."**

**Demo deliverables:**
- [ ] Send OpenSign Founding Pro Agreement within 1 hour of demo close
- [ ] Send onboarding checklist link
- [ ] Send 3-min Loom recap (in case pro wants to share with partner / spouse)
- [ ] Update pipeline tracker — move to "agreement sent"

## 7. Onboarding Completion Checklist (72-hour SLA)

Per Workstream B Task B2 Step 4. From "agreement signed" to "active" status in 72 hours max.

### Hour 0–24: Verification + profile

- [ ] **License verified** — SDR runs state board lookup (NH OPLC, ME OPL, MA mass.gov/dpl) and confirms license # is active, current, and matches name on Founding Pro Agreement
- [ ] **Insurance certificate uploaded** — General liability $500K (Quick Job) or $1M (Project), workers' comp if W-2 staff, auto $1M for any pro with vehicle. Pro emails ACORD certificate or uploads PDF.
- [ ] **Profile completed:**
  - Photo (headshot + 1 on-job-site)
  - Bio (3 sentences — who, trade, metro)
  - Services (selected from category list)
  - Service area (zip code radius)
  - Trade-specific certifications (Mass Save HPIN, EPA RRP, ICPI, etc.)

### Hour 24–48: Stripe Connect + payment readiness

- [ ] **Stripe Connect Express onboarding complete** — pro receives email from Stripe, completes flow, returns to platform with `account.updated` webhook firing
- [ ] **Bank account connected** for payouts (instant or 2-day standard)
- [ ] **W-9 submitted** (1099-NEC reporting >$600/yr)

### Hour 48–72: First job + go-live

- [ ] **First job posted to them within 7 days** — Phyrom drives demand from HJD client list if needed (per Task B3). Acceptable: real promo job at $150–$500 from HJD friends/family acting as founding clients.
- [ ] **First payout processed** within 7 days of first job completion (Quick Job mode: 24-hr release)
- [ ] **Founding Pro badge enabled** in profile (permanent)
- [ ] **Welcome SMS from Phyrom personally** within 72 hours
- [ ] **Pro added to weekly NPS feedback flow** (per Task B4)
- [ ] **Status flipped to "active" in pipeline tracker**

**SLA enforcement:** if any step exceeds the 72-hr window, P personally calls the pro to unblock — most blockers are KYC delays at Stripe (covered by R2 in spec §10) or missing insurance certificate (request directly from pro's insurance broker).

## 8. Key Risks & Mitigations (specific to recruiting)

**R1 — Beta pros sign up but don't transact** (per spec §10 R1): Phyrom drives demand from HJD client list, free promo video per beta pro, friends-and-family seed jobs at $150–$500 to break the ice.

**R2 — Stripe Connect KYC delays >48hr** (per spec §10 R2): backup rails Tilled / Bridgepay; Mercury for banking. P notifies pro of delay personally — never let pro think the platform forgot them.

**R5 — 1099 vs W-2 worker classification challenge** (per spec §10 R5): pros are independently licensed contractors with own insurance, own tools, set own prices, accept/decline jobs. Defensible. Legal opinion in flight (Task A7).

**Recruiting-specific risk: HJD warm list yields fewer conversions than expected.** Mitigation: SDR pivots earlier to supply-house + Mass Save channels, P personally reaches out to NHHBA + MEHBA EDs in Week 2 (not Week 3) to compress timeline.
