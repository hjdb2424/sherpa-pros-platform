Strategic framing — why utility CVCs care about Sherpa Pros

Massachusetts and the broader New England region have aggressive electrification mandates. Utilities are funded and scored by their state Public Utilities Commission (PUC) on how fast they convert customers off fossil heating and onto heat pumps + EVs. Slow throughput = lower PUC scorecards = lower allowed rate-of-return on capex. The throughput bottleneck is licensed installer capacity, not consumer demand or rebate budget.

Sherpa Pros directly addresses the bottleneck by:

  1. Aggregating verified Mass Save / Heat Pump Installer Network (HPIN) licensed pros into one dispatch surface
  2. Reducing homeowner-to-installed-pump time (the key PUC throughput metric)
  3. Surfacing rebate-eligible jobs to pros at the moment of dispatch (not retroactive)
  4. Code-validating quotes before homeowner accepts (reduces rework and re-claim)

Strategic value to a utility CVC, beyond capital return: faster electrification rollout, cleaner utility scorecard, defensible installer-network IP, customer-experience gains.

Critical reframing — Eversource has no dedicated CVC

Per public reporting current to Q1 2026, Eversource Energy does NOT operate a standalone branded "Eversource Ventures" venture-capital fund. The Mass Save Program Administrator office (Eversource MA) is the operational counterpart that controls heat-pump program budget — that is where the strategic conversation starts. If a pilot lands well, capital follows naturally via Eversource's program-budget mechanisms or via Energy Capital Ventures (if green-molecule angle can be stretched to electrification — probably not).

This document treats Eversource as a strategic utility partner (with possible follow-on capital) rather than a pure CVC. The cold email is structured accordingly.
