## Stage 2 — Discovery Call Script (45 Minutes)

**Trigger:** Discovery Call scheduled by VPFD via Calendly link.
**Format:** Video (Google Meet or Zoom)
**Attendees:** VPFD (lead), prospect, optionally Phyrom for first 25 prospects
**Recording:** With prospect consent only — recording goes into franchise-prospect file under mutual NDA

### Pre-Call Preparation Checklist (VPFD)

- [ ] Review prospect's inquiry form responses
- [ ] Pull credit-bureau pre-screen (soft pull — no permission required for marketing screen)
- [ ] Review LinkedIn + public-record search on prospect
- [ ] Confirm territory of interest is in an allowed-sales state (Phase 4 Year 1: NH/ME/MA/VT only)
- [ ] Prepare Discovery Call agenda PDF for prospect

### Call Agenda (Time-Boxed)

**0:00–0:05 — Introductions + agenda walkthrough**

> "Hi {{first_name}}, thanks for making time. I'm [VPFD Name], Vice President of Franchise Development at Sherpa Pros. Today's call is 45 minutes. I'll spend the first 15 minutes telling you about Sherpa Pros and the Sherpa Hub franchise opportunity. The next 20 minutes I'll ask you questions about your background and your interest. The last 10 minutes are yours — any question you have. We're recording with your consent for our internal file. Are you good with that?"

**0:05–0:20 — Sherpa Pros + Sherpa Hub franchise overview**

> "Sherpa Pros is the largest licensed-trade marketplace in New England. We connect verified pros with clients on demand. We've shipped six core products: Sherpa Hub, Sherpa Marketplace, Sherpa Materials, Sherpa Home, Sherpa Success Manager, and Sherpa Rewards plus Flex. The Sherpa Hub is the physical pickup point — 1,500 to 3,000 square feet, located in your local market, where verified pros come to grab kits, rent equipment, attend training, and meet other pros in their trade community.
>
> Sherpa Hub franchise is the only franchise we offer at this time. Initial Franchise Fee is $45,000. Total initial investment range is $185,000 to $420,000 depending on your market. Royalty is 6 percent of gross revenue in Year 1, 8 percent in Year 2 and onward. Marketing Fund contribution is 2 percent. Technology fee is $200 a month flat. You get a protected territory — 5-mile radius in major-metro markets, 15-mile in suburban, 30-mile in rural.
>
> Our Item 19 financial performance representations disclose Year 1 revenue between $385,000 (low) and $740,000 (high), ramping to $1.05 million Year 2 mid case and $1.25 million Year 3 mid case. Mid-case Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization at maturity is approximately 21 percent. Mid-case franchisee pre-tax cash flow at maturity is approximately $236,000.
>
> Item 19 includes the standard FTC disclaimer: some outlets have earned this amount; your individual results may differ; there is no assurance that you will earn as much. Substantiation is from Hub #1 in Atkinson NH (corporate-owned, opened in Phase 1) plus industry-comparable home-services franchise benchmarks.
>
> Any questions on the basics so far?"

**0:20–0:40 — Qualification questions (the heart of the call)**

> Question 1 — Industry experience:
> "Walk me through your career in construction-adjacent work. Specifically: years, roles, and your relationship with the local trade community — trade associations, supply houses, building inspectors, town hall."

> Question 2 — Operating experience:
> "Have you ever owned or run a business with a Profit and Loss statement you were responsible for? Tell me about it — revenue range, employee count, biggest operational lesson."

> Question 3 — Capital position:
> "What is your current liquid net worth — cash plus marketable securities not encumbered by other obligations? And what is your total net worth? You don't need to give exact numbers, but ranges. We require $200,000+ liquid and $400,000+ total."

> Question 4 — Cash to invest:
> "How much cash do you have available for the franchise investment? Our Initial Investment range is $185,000 to $420,000. Most franchisees finance $185,000 via Small Business Administration 7(a) loan and put $117,500 of cash in. How does your situation compare?"

> Question 5 — Operator vs investor mindset:
> "We do not approve absentee ownership. Every franchisee is an operator — present at the Hub full-time during the first 24 months. Are you prepared to do that, or were you thinking of this as a passive investment?"

> Question 6 — Territory + geography:
> "What city or market are you interested in? Why that one specifically — what's your local connection?"

> Question 7 — Risk tolerance + time horizon:
> "The Initial Term is 10 years. Time to monthly cash-flow positive is 14 to 22 months. What's your time horizon for this investment?"

> Question 8 — Existing competing business:
> "Are you currently running, employed by, or invested in a competing licensed-trade marketplace, equipment-rental business, or job-kit assembly business? This is a hard disqualification — we need to know."

> Question 9 — Litigation + bankruptcy:
> "Have you ever been a plaintiff or defendant in franchise litigation? Have you filed personal bankruptcy in the last 7 years? Any felony conviction in the last 10 years?"

> Question 10 — Why Sherpa Pros (not competitor):
> "Why are you interested in Sherpa Pros specifically — versus opening an independent equipment rental, joining a competing franchise, or starting your own platform?"

**0:40–0:50 — Prospect's questions**

> "What questions do you have for me?"

(Most common prospect questions — VPFD should have answers ready: Item 19 ranges in detail; FW Webb partnership status; existing pro count in their market; competitive landscape vs Angi/Thumbtack/TaskRabbit; technology platform demonstration availability; reference franchisees they could speak to (after FDD delivery).)

**0:50–0:55 — Next steps**

> "Here's what happens next. Based on this conversation, [VPFD assesses internally and communicates]:
>
> Path A (proceed): 'I think you're a strong fit on the qualifications we discussed. Our next step is mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement so we can share non-public information. I'll email the NDA today; once it's signed by both parties, we deliver the Franchise Disclosure Document. The FTC requires a mandatory 14-calendar-day waiting period after FDD delivery before any binding signature. Then we schedule Discovery Day at Hub #1 in Atkinson NH. From inquiry to signed Franchise Agreement, the typical cycle is 60 to 120 days.'
>
> Path B (need more info): 'I'd like to think more about a few things you raised — particularly [X]. I'll email you within 5 business days with our assessment.'
>
> Path C (decline with rationale): 'Based on our conversation, I don't think this is a fit because [specific rationale]. We may invite you to reapply after 12 months if [circumstances change]. Thank you for your time, and we wish you the best.'

**0:55–1:00 — Close + send confirmation**

> "Great talking with you. You'll have a follow-up email from me within {{timeframe}} with [next-stage materials | thoughtful decline rationale | request for additional information]. Thank you, {{first_name}}."

### Post-Call Documentation (VPFD)

- Complete Discovery Call assessment form (10 categories scored 1–5)
- Add notes to franchise-prospect file in franchise management software (FranConnect — see `07-vendor-accounts-setup.md`)
- Trigger appropriate next-stage automation (NDA send, decline email, request-additional-info email)

## Stage 6 — Reference Check Questionnaire

**Trigger:** Day 14 of FDD waiting period (waiting period must elapse before signing; Stage 6 can run in parallel with the back half of the waiting period).
**Cadence:** All 3 references contacted within 7 business days; assessments compiled within 14 business days.
**Tool:** Reference contact via email + phone; recorded with reference's consent.

### Three Required Reference Categories

| Category | Who Qualifies | What We're Validating |
|---|---|---|
| Business reference | Former colleague, business partner, supervisor (not family) | Operational competence, integrity, reliability under pressure |
| Financial reference | Banker, accountant, attorney, financial advisor (with prospect's written authorization) | Financial sophistication, ability to manage to a budget, debt management track record |
| Personal reference | Long-term personal contact (not family) | Character, community standing, reliability of word-as-bond |

### Business Reference Questionnaire (15-minute call or email)

> 1. How do you know {{prospect_name}}? In what capacity have you worked together?
> 2. How long have you known the prospect?
> 3. Describe a high-pressure operational situation you observed the prospect handle. What did they do well? What could have gone better?
> 4. How does the prospect handle disagreement or conflict — with employees, with customers, with vendors?
> 5. Has the prospect ever been responsible for a budget, a Profit and Loss statement, or a team? What was the scope, and how did they perform?
> 6. Would you work with this prospect again? Why or why not?
> 7. Are there any concerns about character, integrity, or reliability we should know about?
> 8. Anything else you think is relevant to evaluating someone for a 10-year, $185,000+ business commitment?

### Financial Reference Questionnaire

> 1. How long have you worked with {{prospect_name}}?
> 2. In what capacity (banking, accounting, legal, advisory)?
> 3. To the extent you can disclose without violating client confidentiality: how would you characterize the prospect's financial discipline — budgeting, debt management, savings rate?
> 4. Has the prospect ever defaulted on a debt obligation, missed a tax filing deadline, or had a material financial misstep?
> 5. To the extent you can disclose: is the prospect's stated liquid net worth consistent with your professional knowledge of their finances?
> 6. Would you continue working with this prospect for the next 10 years?
> 7. Any concerns about financial sophistication or financial trustworthiness we should know about?

### Personal Reference Questionnaire

> 1. How do you know {{prospect_name}}? How long?
> 2. How would you describe the prospect's character in 3 words?
> 3. Does the prospect keep their word? Tell me about a time you observed it.
> 4. Does the prospect handle stress well? Tell me about a time.
> 5. How is the prospect regarded in the community?
> 6. Would you trust the prospect with your business or your money?
> 7. Anything else we should know about character, integrity, or community standing?

### Background Check + Credit Check

- Background check vendor: HireRight or similar (industry-standard franchise vendor)
- Credit check vendor: Experian Business + personal credit pull (with prospect's written consent)
- Cost: $550 application processing fee; reimbursed by prospect at this stage
- Timeline: 5–10 business days

### Stage 6 Decision Gate

VPFD compiles reference summary, background check results, credit check results, and Discovery Day assessment into a single Stage 6 file. File goes to Stage 7 Approval Committee.

## Stage 7 — Final Approval Letter + Signing Pack

**Trigger:** Stage 6 file complete + Approval Committee scheduled.
**Approval Committee:** Phyrom + VPFD + future COO + future CFO. Unanimous vote required.
**Cadence:** Committee meets within 7 business days of Stage 6 file completion. Decision within 24 hours of meeting.

### Approval Committee Decision Tree

| Outcome | Cadence | Communication |
|---|---|---|
| Unanimous APPROVE | < 24 hours | Approval letter (template below) + signing schedule |
| Unanimous DECLINE | < 24 hours | Decline letter with rationale + 12-month re-application invitation if appropriate |
| Split vote (any non-unanimous) | < 48 hours | Re-discussion with optional additional information request from prospect |
