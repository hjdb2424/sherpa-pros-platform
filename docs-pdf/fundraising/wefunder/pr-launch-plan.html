Working New Hampshire General Contractor Launches Sherpa Pros — the Vertically Integrated Trade Orchestration Platform Replacing the Labor + Materials + Delivery + Coordination Stack in New England — and Opens a Wefunder Community Round so Homeowners and Pros Can Own a Piece of the Platform

Dateline + lede

PORTSMOUTH, N.H. — [LAUNCH DAY DATE] — Sherpa Pros, the vertically integrated trade orchestration platform built by working general contractor Phyrom of HJD Builders LLC and launching first in New Hampshire, Maine, and Massachusetts, today launched a U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission Regulation Crowdfunding (Reg CF) community round on Wefunder, with a target raise of $250,000 to $500,000 and minimum investments of $100. The platform — live at www.thesherpapros.com — is the first home-services platform to integrate labor matching, materials orchestration, same-day delivery, and multi-trade coordination into a single system, addressing the four structural problems no lead-generation platform solves: license verification, code-aware quote validation, permit-aware workflows, and rebate matching for state utility programs like Mass Save. With this quarter's launch of Sherpa Materials (programmatic materials ordering through the Zinc API plus Uber Direct same-day delivery), Sherpa Dispatch (multi-trade timeline coordination across 6+ trades on a single project), and Sherpa Guard (Role-Based Access Control plus audit logs for institutional-grade compliance), the company's competitive set has shifted from the labor-marketplace category (Angi, Thumbtack) to the entire trade-supply-chain stack (FW Webb, Grainger, Uber Direct, and Procore combined).

Body

The platform launches into a market under acute strain. Massachusetts has the largest construction labor shortage in the United States, with 2.5% construction unemployment — the lowest in 17 years — according to reporting in Worcester Business Journal and Bisnow Boston. Thirty percent of Massachusetts residential construction workers are over 55, and project completion times across the state have stretched from seven to eleven months.

At the same time, the existing home-services platforms have come under sustained criticism. Angi (NASDAQ: ANGI) is not Better Business Bureau-accredited and has 1,800+ BBB complaints since 2023. The Vermont Attorney General settled with Angi for $100,000 in October 2025 over misleading "Certified Pro" terminology. Thumbtack is the subject of a BBB pattern-of-complaints review opened in November 2025 over documented contractor-deposit-theft cases. TaskRabbit charges a 22.5% effective platform fee and does not cover licensed trades. Sherpa Pros enters the market with a 5% take-rate during beta (10% at scale) — roughly half the all-in cost a contractor pays Angi today.

"Every week, the platforms my crew gets pitched sell us leads we can't afford and send homeowners to people who shouldn't be touching their wiring or their gas line," said Phyrom, founder of Sherpa Pros and HJD Builders LLC. "I built the tool I wished existed. Jobs, not leads. Licensed only. Code-checked quotes. We're raising on Wefunder so the homeowners and contractors who actually use this thing can own a piece of it."

The platform is built on a code-intelligence layer that validates every contractor quote against the National Electrical Code (NEC), International Residential Code (IRC), Massachusetts Electrical Code, New Hampshire state code (RSA), and Boston Inspectional Services Department (ISD) permit requirements before the homeowner sees the quote. The system catches missing line items, undersized panels, and code violations — surfacing a "code-checked" badge to homeowners. The code layer required a working general contractor and a multi-year codebase to build, and is what the founder describes as the platform's primary moat against incumbent response.

Sherpa Pros enters Boston with a deliberately narrow specialty positioning rather than a head-on assault on entrenched lead-generation incumbents. The five Boston specialty lanes are: Mass Save heat-pump installations ($8,500 maximum 2026 rebate per Mass Save program documents), EV charger installations under the National Grid Turnkey EV Charging Installation Program, electrical panel upgrades, old-house specialists (plaster, slate, brownstone repointing, historic restoration), and triple-decker porch and exterior work subject to Boston ISD permits. National platforms cannot filter for any of these.

The Wefunder community round is one of four parallel funding tracks. The other three — non-dilutive grants (MassCEC InnovateMass, NSF SBIR Phase I, MassDevelopment Biz-M-Power), accelerators (Suffolk Technologies, Techstars ConstructionTech, Y Combinator), and pre-seed venture capital (Building Ventures, Brick & Mortar Ventures) — are all running concurrently. Whichever closes first triggers the company's Phase 1 launch.

"The Wefunder round is the one where the community gets a seat at the table," said Phyrom. "We're running it because the homeowners and licensed contractors who use the platform should own a piece of the platform. That's the whole point."

NEW launch-day asset — 2-paragraph press blurb (the "vertical integration thesis" pitch)

For use in trade-press pre-briefs, the launch-day LinkedIn caption, and as the first 2 paragraphs of any condensed reporter pitch. Reads as the "what changed this quarter" framing for outlets who have already covered Sherpa Pros once.

The licensed-trade marketplace just stopped being a marketplace. When Sherpa Pros launched in beta last quarter, the company competed with Angi, Thumbtack, TaskRabbit, and Handy in one lane: matching homeowners with licensed contractors. This quarter, the platform shipped three capabilities that move it out of that lane entirely. Sherpa Materials orders the materials for every job through programmatic access to the major retail catalogs (the Zinc API) and delivers them to the jobsite the same day through Uber Direct. Sherpa Dispatch sequences six or more trades on a single multi-trade project (a kitchen renovation that normally runs 11 weeks finishes in 5 with Sherpa Dispatch). Sherpa Guard adds Role-Based Access Control and audit logs — the SOC 2-readiness signal institutional partners and large property-management cohorts require before signing.

The competitive set shifted accordingly. Sherpa Pros no longer competes with the labor-marketplace category. It competes with the entire trade-supply-chain stack — FW Webb (the supply house), Grainger (industrial supply), Uber Direct (same-day delivery), and Procore (enterprise general-contractor coordination software) combined. Per-job economics shift in lockstep: a $10,000 labor job has roughly $15,000 of materials passing through it, and the take rate stacks — 5–12 percent labor commission, plus an 8–12 percent materials coordination fee, plus the $49 monthly Pro subscription, plus the Property Manager retainer — for a blended 18 to 25 percent take rate per job all-in. Founder Phyrom calls it "the Stripe Connect moment for the trade economy" — the orchestration layer that turns single-job marketplaces into whole-project platforms. The Wefunder community round opens at $100 minimum so the homeowners and licensed contractors who use the platform can own a piece of the orchestration layer being built around them.

Press list operating notes

  • Verify every email before sending. Use Muck Rack (paid), RocketReach, or the outlet masthead. Bounces hurt domain reputation and reduce future deliverability.
  • Personalize subject lines. Generic blasts get ignored. Reference the reporter's specific recent piece in the subject line where possible.
  • Send Tuesday or Wednesday morning, 7:00–8:30 AM ET. Avoid Monday (full inboxes), Thursday-Friday (weekend bleed), holidays.
  • Lead with a 2-line pitch + 1-line ask + 1-line link. No attachments in the first email. Offer the press release as a clean Google Doc or copy-paste only on reporter request.
  • One reply-bump after 48 hours. If no response after the bump, drop. Don't spam.
  • Track responses in a simple Notion or Airtable database. Status: Sent / Opened / Replied / Declined / Wrote story / Ghosted. Refer back at Phase 1 PR push.
