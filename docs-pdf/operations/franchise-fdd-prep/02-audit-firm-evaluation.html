Audit Firm Evaluation — FDD Item 21 Financial Statements

1. Purpose

Federal Trade Commission (FTC) Franchise Rule Item 21 requires audited financial statements of the franchisor entity (Sherpa Pros LLC) for the three most recent fiscal years (or fewer if the franchisor has been in business less than three years). The audit must be performed by an independent Certified Public Accountant (CPA) firm in accordance with Generally Accepted Auditing Standards (GAAS). The audited statements must be embedded in the FDD as exhibits and refreshed annually to maintain FDD currency. Item 21 is a sales-stopper — without audited statements, no franchisee can be sold to a registration-state resident, period.

This document evaluates the top 2 candidate audit firms specializing in franchise practice, defines the scoring rubric, and recommends a primary engagement.

4. Decision Recommendation

Primary engagement: BDO USA Franchise Services Practice.

Rationale:

  1. Named franchise practice with established methodology — BDO Franchise Services has built explicit Item 21 audit templates, Item 19 substantiation file checklists, and franchise-counsel-coordination workflows. The audit firm's process is calibrated to franchise needs, not generic small-business needs. This shaves 30–60 days off the Year 1 audit timeline.
  2. Counsel-coordination history with Spadea Lignana (the recommended attorney firm in 01-attorney-evaluation-matrix.md) — BDO and Spadea Lignana have collaborated on multiple emerging-franchisor FDD launches. The two firms know each other's intake forms, schedule cadences, and substantiation expectations. This eliminates 2–3 weeks of cross-firm onboarding friction that would burn against the Phase 4 Year 1 launch timeline.
  3. Boston MA office — fieldwork at Hub #1 Atkinson NH is a 50-mile drive. In-person inventory observation, internal-controls walk-through, and management discussion can happen in a single day. Marcum's nearest major office is also reachable but BDO Boston is closest.
  4. Reference strength in emerging-franchisor segment — BDO has audited Year 1 franchisors regularly; the audit team understands the data quality and process maturity gaps typical of a Year 1 franchisor and prices accordingly.
  5. Within budget — $32K–$45K Year 1 fits the budget target, with upside to negotiate the high end down via multi-year engagement letter (commit to 3-year Year 1 + Year 2 + Year 3 audit engagement at locked annual fees).

Backup engagement (if BDO declines or pricing exceeds $40K): Marcum LLP Franchise Group. Lower fee envelope, comparable specialty depth, slightly less Spadea Lignana coordination history. Engage as primary if BDO unavailable.

5. Engagement Process

  1. Request for Proposal (RFP) issued — Phase 4 Year 1 Month 1 (parallel to franchise-attorney RFP). Send to BDO + Marcum simultaneously. Include Sherpa Pros LLC entity background (Delaware LLC, principal office Atkinson NH), Hub #1 operating data summary under mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA), expected fiscal-year-end date, projected revenue range, audit budget target.
  2. Vendor screening calls — Weeks 2–3. 60-minute video call with each candidate firm; named partner attends. Phyrom + future CFO + future VPFD attend.
  3. References checked — Weeks 3–4. Contact 3 emerging-franchisor references per finalist.
  4. Engagement letter signed — Week 5. Locked annual fees for Years 1 + 2 + 3; locked deliverable milestones; locked Item 21 exhibit delivery date (target: Phase 4 Year 1 Month 9).
  5. Audit kickoff — Week 6. Internal-controls walk-through at Atkinson NH HQ; Hub #1 inventory observation scheduled; books-and-records review begins.

7. Risk Notes + Open Questions

  • Sherpa Pros LLC entity formation status — confirm Delaware LLC status, standard operating agreement in place, separate books-and-records from HJD Builders LLC. If any commingling exists, audit firm will require restatement before Year 1 audit can be issued. Pre-engagement housekeeping critical.
  • Hub #1 corporate-owned status — Hub #1 is corporate-owned (not franchised) per spec. Hub #1 P&L flows through Sherpa Pros LLC (not HJD Builders LLC). Confirm legal structure before audit; if Hub #1 sits inside HJD Builders LLC currently for tax convenience, it must transfer to Sherpa Pros LLC before fiscal year-end to be in scope for Item 21 audited statements.
  • Multi-year engagement letter — negotiate locked-fee 3-year engagement to compress Year 2+ pricing. Standard franchise audit firms accept 3-year locked engagements at modest discounts (~10%).
  • Phase 5 international entity audit scope — when international Master Franchisees enter, country-level audited statements may be required (varies by country franchise law). Add international audit capability to RFP scoring criteria in Phase 5 firm re-evaluation.
  • Pre-audit accounting cleanup — anticipate $8K–$15K of pre-audit accounting cleanup work (chart of accounts standardization, intercompany cost allocation methodology between Sherpa Pros LLC and HJD Builders LLC, fixed asset depreciation schedule cleanup). Engage a fractional CFO or controller for this work in Months 0–2 before audit kickoff.
