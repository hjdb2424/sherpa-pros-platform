FW Webb Per-Branch Economic Model

Purpose

Quantify the incremental EBITDA contribution to FW Webb from hosting a Sherpa Hub at one of its branches, over a 5-year horizon, for each of the 5 pilot branches. Used in the FW Webb pitch deck (slide 9) and as evidence for LOI economics (03-pilot-loi-template.md §3).

Will-call delivery uplift = ~28% gross margin (services component)

The model is robust because the rent line is locked + counter-sales-lift breakeven is at +3.5% lift — well below the 15% plan.

What FW Webb gets that doesn't show in the table

  • Defensive moat vs Amazon Business Trade, Lowe's Pro, and Home Depot Pro Xtra — Sherpa-Hub-equipped branches lock in pro relationships
  • Recruiting + retention asset for FW Webb's own branch staff (modern co-located format = "I'm at the cool branch")
  • Innovation-partnership credentialing for FW Webb's parent-company narrative (private-equity-friendly story)
  • Optional acquisition pathway — FW Webb has right of first negotiation to acquire Sherpa Pros' Hub network at end of Pilot Year 5 [LOI optional addendum, deferred]

Assumptions register (§6)

All numbers above use:

  • FW Webb branch counter sales avg ~$2.8M/branch/yr — directional industry benchmark; refine with FW Webb data during diligence
  • Counter GM ~22% — typical PVF wholesale blended margin; refine with FW Webb data
  • Pro-spend per Hub member ~$5.2K/yr at FW Webb — derived from Sherpa Pros internal cohort assumptions (median NE plumbing/HVAC sub spends ~$60K/yr at PVF supply houses; ~9% capture)
  • Sherpa Materials orchestration GM ~18% — preferred-vendor margin slightly compressed
  • NPS retention uplift = $60K — assumes Hub-equipped branch reduces pro churn 4 pts on $1.4M of attributable pro account base; refine with FW Webb data
  • All figures conservative; sensitivity in §"What breaks the model"
