## 4. Membership Application Narrative Draft

Greentown Labs application typically asks: company overview, climate-impact statement, prototyping/facility needs, mentor asks, team. Map answers to those four pillars.

### Q: Company overview (~150 words)

Sherpa Pros is the national licensed-trade marketplace (international roadmap), launching first across New England, built by a working New Hampshire general contractor (HJD Builders LLC). We connect homeowners and property managers to verified licensed pros — electricians, plumbers, HVAC techs, roofers, GCs, specialty trades — with two structural differentiators that lead-gen platforms (Angi, Thumbtack, TaskRabbit, Handy, Houzz) cannot match:

1. **Every pro is license + insurance verified** before listing and re-checked on renewal.
2. **Every quote is validated against code** in real time — NEC, IRC, MA Electrical, NH RSA, 284 NH municipal codes — before the homeowner sees it.

The platform is live: web at https://www.thesherpapros.com, iOS via TestFlight, Android via PWA. Beta cohort transacting today across NH, ME, MA in 9 trade categories. Pre-seed; founder-funded; building toward a Phase 1 first close in Q3 2026.

### Q: Climate impact — describe the climate-deployment thesis (~250 words)

Sherpa Pros directly accelerates three climate-deployment pipelines in Massachusetts:

**1. Mass Save heat-pump installs.** ~75K heat pumps installed in MA since 2019 (Mass Save program data); 2025–2027 plan goal of ~50% YoY growth in low-income install volume; current install pipeline run-rate ~15K–25K/year statewide. Average install reduces a MA home's emissions ~3 tons CO2/year (vs. oil/propane/electric resistance baseline). The Mass Save Find-an-Installer listing is a static directory — no marketplace mechanics, no dispatch, no quote validation, no installer scheduling. **Sherpa Pros is the execution layer that listing is missing.** Capture even 5% of MA's annual install pipeline = 750–1,250 accelerated installs/year = **2,250–3,750 tons CO2/year reduction** directly attributable to Sherpa Pros at Phase 2 scale.

**2. National Grid Turnkey EV charger installs.** Boston goal: 5-minute walk to public charger; Turnkey program covers most/all install costs for hard-wired Level 2 chargers in EJ communities + single-family/2–4 unit homes. Each home charger displaces ~3–4 tons CO2/year vs. ICE vehicle equivalent. Sherpa Pros surfaces licensed electricians who are panel-upgrade certified + EV-cert qualified.

**3. Electrical panel upgrades as gateway work.** Heat pumps + EVs + future solar require panel capacity. The single biggest bottleneck on residential electrification is panel upgrade backlog. Sherpa Pros' code-aware quote validation pre-flags panel-upgrade requirements at the matching layer, compressing the install timeline from 12 weeks → 4 weeks.

**Aggregate Phase 2 climate impact (12-month estimate):** 5,000–10,000 accelerated electrification installs across MA + NH + ME + Boston specialty lanes = **15,000–30,000 tons CO2/year reduction** directly attributable to Sherpa Pros throughput.

## 7. Reviewer-Bias Tips for Greentown Labs

1. **Climate-impact quantification is mandatory.** Vague impact statements ("reduces emissions") fail. Specific, methodology-shown impact statements ("3 tons CO2/year per heat-pump install × 5K accelerated installs = 15K tons/year") pass. Use the numbers from §4 Q3 above.

2. **Address the "you're a software platform, not hardware" objection head-on.** Greentown was historically hardware-heavy and has expanded to software/services climate startups. **Frame Sherpa Pros as climate-deployment infrastructure**, not "yet another marketplace." The Mass Save and EV charger lanes are first-order climate-deployment plays.

3. **Mass-specific sponsor alignment.** Greentown's Boston / Massachusetts donor base includes Eversource, National Grid, MassCEC, MassDOER. **Name these explicitly in the application.** Show you understand the partner network.

4. **EJ (environmental justice) framing matters.** Mass Save's 2025–2027 plan has ~40% of program funding focused on equity initiatives. Reference **Environmental Justice Communities** specifically in the climate-impact narrative — Greentown reviewers track this terminology.

5. **Somerville/Boston commitment signals.** Greentown reviewers prefer founders who will be physically present at Somerville HQ, not virtual-only. **Indicate Coworking-tier intent for Phase 1+** even if starting with Virtual to validate fit. Shows commitment.

6. **Mention Mass Save Trade Partners Network in flight.** This is a free third-party validation signal — if Sherpa Pros is on track for Mass Save approval, Greentown reviewers will treat it as program-validated even before final approval.

7. **Founder authenticity over corporate polish.** Greentown's culture is anti-corporate. Phyrom's NH-GC voice + jobsite-shot video resonates more than a polished VC pitch. Use the same 1-minute video as the YC application if needed.

8. **Apply with the inquiry email first, formal application second.** Greentown's membership team prefers a low-friction conversation first. The inquiry email in §3 above gets a 20-minute call; the formal application follows after they greenlight fit.

9. **The Engine (MIT) is an alternative climate-tech vehicle if Greentown does not advance.** The Engine specifically funds "tough tech" — Sherpa Pros' code-intelligence layer is borderline-fit there. Mentioned in GTM spec §6.2. Keep as a backup track if Greentown declines.
