Research brief — who at Building Ventures

Firm summary: Building Ventures is a Boston/SF venture capital firm founded in 2017 by the ex-Borealis Ventures built-environment team. They invest seed → Series A in companies that improve the full lifecycle of the built environment. Fund II closed Sept 2022 at $95M. As of Aug 2025, ~36 portfolio companies; top sectors are Real Estate/Construction Tech (23 investments) and Enterprise Applications (20). Most recent investment: BuildFactory, Nov 2025. [^1]

Target partner: Jesse Devitte (Co-founder & General Partner, Boston) [CONFIRMED]

Why Devitte and not Connors first:

  • Devitte is Boston-based; Phyrom is in NH. Geographic alignment for in-person follow-up.
  • Devitte was the named subject of a 2022 Boston Globe profile titled "Boston VC firm wants to take the waste out of building trades" — language directly maps to Sherpa Pros' "jobs not leads" thesis. [^2]
  • Devitte personally manages the firm's content publishing (his author page on the BV site is a key voice for the firm's thesis).

Backup partner: Travis Connors (Co-founder & General Partner, SF) [CONFIRMED]

If Devitte is slow or out, Connors is the SF-coast partner. Same firm; same conviction will travel internally.

What Building Ventures has said publicly (cite in the opener)

  1. Boston Globe 2022 profile — Devitte described the built environment as the largest under-digitized sector and emphasized "industrialization of construction" and "deployment of space as a service" as core theses. [^2] Hook for Phyrom: Sherpa Pros is the digitization of the licensed-trade dispatch layer that all those previous investments depended on but never solved.
  2. Founding-team narrative — Devitte and Connors led "the most prolific early investor in the built world space with over 20 investments and 10 successful exits" at Borealis before launching Building Ventures. [^3] Hook: they've seen what wins. Sherpa Pros' founder-built-by-a-working-GC story is the missing link they haven't had a clean shot at.
  3. Recent investments — BuildFactory (Nov 2025); historically Built Robotics, Dandelion Energy. [^1] Hook: Built Robotics is a labor-augmentation thesis. Sherpa Pros is a labor-allocation thesis. The MA labor shortage data (per spec §2.1: largest in nation, 2.5% unemployment) IS the demand signal Built Robotics solves on the supply side — Sherpa Pros solves it on the marketplace side.

  • No "AI-powered," "disrupt," "revolutionize," "Uber for X," "gig," "task" (per spec §3.3)
  • Founder hook in line 1 (Phyrom = working NH GC) — per spec §3.3
  • www.thesherpapros.com link present (live software is the credibility lever — per deck slide 5 notes)
  • Specific recent BV reference in opener (Boston Globe 2022 profile, BuildFactory Nov 2025 investment, etc.) — proves Phyrom did the homework
  • Specific ask (15 min, deck review, OR fast pass — pick one or list in order of preference)
  • No PDF attachments in cold email — link to Notion deck instead. Attachments trigger spam filters.
