# Phase 4 Marketing Kit Refresh — Specification

## Purpose

This document specifies the Phase 4 marketing kit refresh. **Execution is deferred to Phase 4 Month 0+** — after Series B close, with Vice President Marketing (Phase 4 Month 3 hire) leading.

This specification is written now, in advance, for two reasons:

1. **The Series B announcement and Phase 4 launch happen in the same news cycle.** Marketing kit refresh delivered on a 90-day window after Series B close = a coherent Phase 4 brand moment, not 12 disconnected updates released over 6 months.
2. **The work cannot start cold.** A Vice President Marketing hire stepping into the role on Phase 4 Month 3 has a defined, scoped, ready-to-commission refresh package waiting. Reduces ramp time from 90 days to 30.

---

## 1. Why a refresh is needed

### 1.1 Phase 0/1/2/3 marketing kit narrative

The current marketing kit (created across Phases 0-3) was built around four sequential storylines:

- **Phase 0:** Founding-pro recruit, lean-launch beta, Wefunder $250K close, Phyrom-as-working-contractor founder voice.
- **Phase 1:** Public launch across Portsmouth, Manchester, Portland, Boston specialty wedge. "We launched."
- **Phase 2:** Series A close, Sherpa Hub network design, Sherpa Rewards + Sherpa Flex go live, materials orchestration design. "We're building the flywheel."
- **Phase 3:** Series B close, multi-region infrastructure, materials orchestration live, regional expansion. "The flywheel turns."

### 1.2 Phase 4 marketing kit narrative

Phase 4 narrative is fundamentally different and requires a fresh telling:

- **International scale.** Canada Year 1, United Kingdom Year 2 in build, Australia Year 3 scoped. Sherpa Pros is no longer a U.S.-only story.
- **Franchise.** The Sherpa Hub franchise model is live with a signed franchisee. The Hub network shifts from "company-operated" to "company-and-franchisee operated."
- **Vertical integration scale.** Sherpa Materials engine, Sherpa codes engine, Sherpa Lending pilot, Sherpa Insurance partnership, Software-as-a-Service-readiness investments. The platform is more than a marketplace.
- **Series C readiness.** Investor conversation moves from "Series B momentum" to "the path to $1B Annual Recurring Revenue."

A marketing kit built on the Phase 0-3 story does not tell the Phase 4 story credibly. Refresh required.

## 3. Brand-portfolio additions for Phase 4

### 3.1 Country sub-brand variants — DECISION LOCKED

**Decision.** No country sub-brands. Single Sherpa Pros umbrella global. Per brand bible (see `docs/operations/brand-portfolio.md`), the umbrella brand is non-negotiable.

**Rationale.** Country sub-brands ("Sherpa Pros Canada," "Sherpa Pros UK") fragment brand equity. Single umbrella with country-localized content (currency, language, regulatory references) preserves brand strength while respecting local context.

**Practical implication.** Visual identity (logo, colors, typography) identical worldwide. Copy localized. Country General Manager photographed and quoted as the local face when warranted (press, recruiting, founding-pro events) — but always under the Sherpa Pros umbrella.

### 3.2 International press positioning

**Founder voice leads.** Even in international press, Phyrom remains the lead founder voice. The story of "American working contractor builds the platform, now scaling to your country" is more compelling than "American company opens a Canadian office."

**Country General Manager as local face.** Local press placements feature the Country General Manager prominently — but always as a Sherpa Pros executive operating a country business unit, not as the founder of a Canadian (or UK or Australian) entity.

**No region-anchored brand language.** Avoid "the Canadian Sherpa Pros" or "Sherpa Pros UK" framing. Use "Sherpa Pros in Canada" / "Sherpa Pros in the United Kingdom" — country as adjective, brand as noun.

### 3.3 Franchise prospect brand voice

**Audience shift.** Franchise prospects are a different audience than consumers (homeowners) or pros (contractors). Franchise prospects are entrepreneurial buyers — typically existing business operators, contractor-owners stepping into ownership, or first-time franchisees with capital.

**Brand voice adjustments for franchise audience:**
- More financial-detail-forward (Item 19 Financial Disclosure Document numbers, unit economics, Return on Investment math).
- More entrepreneurial-aspiration tone (own your business, build wealth, be part of a growing system).
- Less "founder-on-the-tools" populism, more "proven model + executive support team."
- Discovery Day deck (`slides/07-franchise-prospect-discovery-day.md`) is the canonical franchise-voice artifact.

**Brand voice does NOT change:**
- Visual identity (logo, colors, typography, diagonal seam motif) identical.
- Promise pillars ("Jobs not leads, Licensed only, Code-aware, Permit-aware, Rebate-aware") still anchor — but reframed as franchise-system value props (the franchisee benefits from delivering these promises in their territory).
- Founder voice still present in franchise prospect materials — Phyrom's working-contractor credibility is part of the franchise prospect's reason to buy in.

### 3.4 Marketing fund management

Phase 4 introduces a marketing fund supported by franchisee 1.5% royalty contribution. Management framework:

- **Owner.** Vice President Marketing (Phase 4 Month 3 hire), with Director of Franchise (Phase 4 Month 12 hire) coordinating franchisee inputs.
- **Governance.** Marketing fund oversight committee includes Vice President Marketing + Director of Franchise + Franchisee Advisory Council representatives + Chief Financial Officer.
- **Allocation.** Brand-level (national / international) marketing 60%; local-market marketing (per franchisee territory) 30%; reserve 10%.
- **Reporting.** Annual marketing-fund Profit and Loss reported to all franchisees per Franchise Disclosure Document Item 11 disclosure requirements.

## 4. Trigger criteria

### 4.1 The trigger event

**T-zero = Series B close.** All refresh files build off the post-Series-B brand state. The Series B announcement press cycle is the launch moment for the refreshed kit.

### 4.2 The 90-day delivery window

**T+30 days:** Pitch deck v3 + Phase 4 onepager + investor pitch v2 + Building Ventures Phase 4 update delivered. These are the highest-leverage assets and need to land in the same news cycle as the Series B announcement.

**T+45 days:** LinkedIn editorial Phase 4 calendar + first country-specific founding-pro deck (Canadian variant) + first country-specific Property Manager sales deck (Canadian variant). These support Country General Manager Canada hire announcement and Toronto cohort recruiting.

**T+60 days:** Social content plan extension + visual asset prompt library expansion. Phase 4 social calendar lights up with the Phase 4 storyline.

**T+90 days:** Country-specific email sequences + United Kingdom Country General Manager hire announcement + first franchise-prospect Discovery Day program kickoff. Full Phase 4 marketing kit live.

### 4.3 Why 90 days, not 30 or 180

- **30 days too short.** Vice President Marketing hires Phase 4 Month 3, not Month 0. Cannot ramp + commission + ship a full kit in 30 days. Realistic minimum is 90 days.
- **180 days too long.** Series B announcement news cycle dies after ~90 days. Marketing kit launched on a separate news cycle from Series B announcement misses the compounding momentum.

## 7. Quality bar

The Phase 4 marketing kit refresh must meet the following quality bar:

- **Visual consistency.** Every asset uses the locked brand palette, locked typography, locked diagonal seam motif (per brand bible §2.1).
- **Tone consistency.** Every asset uses the locked brand voice (per brand bible §1.1 — confident, contractor-grounded, anti-jargon, Phyrom-flavored).
- **Numerical accuracy.** Every claim, number, citation matches the underlying source-of-truth document. Vice President Marketing + Chief Financial Officer joint review for any asset containing financial numbers.
- **Country-localization quality.** Country-specific content reviewed by the relevant Country General Manager before publication. No "American English with currency-symbol find-replace" — actual local idiomatic copy.
- **Founder voice integrity.** Phyrom personally reviews every asset that quotes him, claims his story, or carries his byline. No founder-voice content ships without Phyrom sign-off.
- **Accessibility.** All assets meet Web Content Accessibility Guidelines version 2.2 Level AA. Color contrast checked, alt text present, screen-reader-friendly.
