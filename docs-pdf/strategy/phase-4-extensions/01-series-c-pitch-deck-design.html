Sherpa Pros — Series C Pitch Deck Design

Document type: Deck design specification + first-pass slide content. Final presentation file (docs/slides/08-series-c-investor-pitch.md) gets generated as part of the Phase 4 marketing kit refresh (see 08-phase-4-marketing-kit-refresh-spec.md). This file is the brief, the slide-by-slide outline, and the data-source map the future deck builder works against.

Target audience. Tier-1 growth investors: Sequoia Capital, Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) Growth, General Catalyst, Insight Partners, Tiger Global, Bessemer Venture Partners (BVP). Strategic check candidates: Home Depot Ventures, Lowe's Strategic Investments, Procore Ventures.

Target close window. Phase 4 Month 30 to Month 36. Trigger: Phase 4 OKR 7 met (see 06-phase-4-okrs-and-measurement-framework.md).

Target raise. $40M-$80M (target $60M).

Target valuation. $400M-$800M (target $600M, ~10x trailing-twelve-month Annual Recurring Revenue at the $60M-ARR proof point).

Comparable benchmark transactions. Procore Series E ($150M at $3B in 2018), ServiceTitan Series F ($200M at $8.3B in 2020), Toast Series F ($400M at $4.9B in 2020). Sherpa Pros at the Series C target is ~5-10x smaller than these comparables — appropriate for the stage.

Deck design principles

  1. Receipts over rhetoric. The Series C deck is a receipts deck. Every claim — Annual Recurring Revenue, Gross Merchandise Value, Customer Acquisition Cost, take rate, retention — has a screenshot, a chart, or a citation. Investors at this stage have seen the founder story.
  2. The flywheel is the moat. Slides do not present products in isolation; they show how each new product compounds the others. The single most important slide is Slide 11 (the multi-product flywheel diagram).
  3. International and franchise are not promises — they are proven systems. The deck shows Canada Year 1 lived numbers and the first signed franchisee, not pro-forma projections.
  4. Path to one-billion-dollar Annual Recurring Revenue is shown, not stated. Slide 22 walks the model line by line.
  5. Founder voice stays. Phyrom is on every founder slide and in the closing slide. The Series A and Series B narrative was "working contractor builds the platform he wished existed." The Series C narrative is "working contractor turned out to be right — and now leads a company built to outscale the comp set."

Slide 20 — The competitive cost of NOT winning this round

Headline: The window is now.

Three forces converging:

  1. Home Depot Pro Desk + Lowe's Pro are accelerating their pro-loyalty programs but have no labor-matching layer. Sherpa fills the white space; if we don't, they will build it.
  2. Procore + ServiceTitan are exploring residential. Procore's 2025 acquisition rumors (unsourced; treat as directional) suggest they would buy a residential marketplace before building one.
  3. Insurance carriers are searching for embedded distribution. Sherpa Flex's per-project insurance tier is the proof-of-concept; carriers are watching.

Sherpa Pros either becomes the consolidator or becomes the consolidated. Series C funds the consolidator path.
