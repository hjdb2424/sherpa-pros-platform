# Secondary Distribution-Partner Targets

## Overall ranking + reasoning

Ranking criteria, weighted:
1. **Branch density × pro density** (40%) — more branches × more pros per branch = more Hub-ready locations
2. **Strategic pressure to defend pro share** (30%) — under existential threat from Amazon Business / HD Pro Xtra → most receptive to defensive moats
3. **Decision-making speed** (15%) — private/founder-led > PE-owned > public; faster LOIs
4. **Category complementarity to FW Webb** (15%) — non-overlapping pilot regions + non-overlapping trade categories so multiple pilots run in parallel

| Rank | Partner | Category | Branches | Decision speed | Why this rank |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Ferguson** | Plumbing/HVAC/PVF (overlaps Webb) | ~1,650 [VERIFY] | Slow (public, FERG) | Highest branch count by far; under most documented PE/activist pressure to defend pro share against Amazon Business + HD Pro Xtra. Pilot in non-Webb regions (TX/SE/Mountain West) avoids exclusivity conflict |
| 2 | **84 Lumber** | Lumber & building materials (NEW category) | 300+ [VERIFY] | Fast (private, founder-led — Maggie Hardy Knox) | Founder-led private = LOI signed in weeks not quarters. Lumber is a pure-additive category vs Webb's PVF — runs in parallel with zero exclusivity conflict. Deep GC relationships, not just trades |
| 3 | **Rexel USA** | Electrical (NEW category) | 400+ [VERIFY] | Medium (PE-owned, French parent Rexel SA) | Electrical pros have highest per-job materials spend ($80-100K/yr median per pro [VERIFY]) → highest Hub member economics. Adds the third leg of the trade-category stool (PVF + lumber + electrical) |

The strategic logic: Webb is the hero pilot in PVF/HVAC. The other three open three orthogonal beachheads (PVF national, lumber/GC, electrical) so that by Year 2 Sherpa Pros has signed pilots covering 80%+ of trade-category materials spend — and no single distributor's defection breaks the model.

## Target #1 — Ferguson Enterprises

### Who they are
Largest plumbing/HVAC/PVF distributor in North America. Public on NYSE under ticker FERG (UK-incorporated as Ferguson plc until 2024 redomicile to US [VERIFY]). ~$30B+ annual revenue [VERIFY], ~1,650 branches across US + Canada [VERIFY]. Pro counter is the core revenue driver. Heavy investment in digital pro tools (ferguson.com pro account, Ferguson Pro app) but no physical co-located format.

### Why they fit the Sherpa Hub thesis
- Direct PVF/HVAC overlap with FW Webb pilot — same pro persona, same materials engine, same buffer SKU list
- Branch count is **24x FW Webb's**: a successful Webb pilot translates directly into Ferguson's playbook
- Public-market pressure to defend pro counter share against Amazon Business Trade is documented in their last 3 earnings calls [VERIFY]
- Pilot is geographically segmentable: run pilot in Texas, Southeast, or Mountain West (non-Webb territory) — zero conflict with §7.1 Webb exclusivity

### Decision-maker title
**SVP / VP — Pro Sales** (or **VP Strategic Partnerships** — Ferguson has both roles [VERIFY exact titles via LinkedIn]). Final approver: **CEO Kevin Murphy** [VERIFY current CEO]. Owns trade pro relationship across all branches.

### Ice-breaker hook
> "FW Webb's leadership is in active LOI negotiation with Sherpa Pros to co-locate 5 Hubs inside their NE branches. The economic model produces $165-210K incremental EBITDA per branch in Year 1. Webb is asking us not to talk to other PVF distributors in the NE region during their 12-month pilot — but it explicitly carves out the rest of the country. Want to see the model and discuss a parallel pilot in your Texas or Southeast region?"

The hook uses **fear-of-missing-out** (Webb is moving) + **carved-out opportunity** (geography unblocks deal) + **proof point** (per-branch model already built).

### Warm-intro path candidates
- **Mutual contractor customers** — any large NE GC who buys at both Webb and Ferguson
- **NE Pro Council / MEP industry orgs** that include both Ferguson + Webb members
- **Phyrom's own buying relationships** — if HJD Builders has an open Ferguson account
- **PE/IB networks** — Ferguson's investor base includes BlackRock, Vanguard; cold path through Series-A investor introductions [VERIFY]
- **LinkedIn 2nd-degree** through any Sherpa Pros advisor [VERIFY who]

### Expected counter-offer to Webb's exclusivity
Ferguson will counter with one of three:
1. **"Match Webb's terms in our largest territory"** — they'll demand the Texas + Southeast region pilot be **wider than 5 Hubs** (likely ask for 10-15) to match scale
2. **"Reciprocal exclusivity"** — they'll ask for the same 25-mile competitor radius lock-out around their Pilot Branches, which is fine
3. **"Strategic investment instead of LOI"** — Ferguson's corporate development arm may offer to invest in Sherpa Pros at the LOI stage in exchange for category-wide preferred status. Phyrom should treat this as upside, not a redirect

The risk to manage: Ferguson asking for **national exclusivity** in PVF, which would block Webb. Counter with **time-boxed regional exclusivity only** (12-month pilot in named regions).

## Target #2 — 84 Lumber

### Who they are
Largest privately-held lumber + building-materials supplier in the US. Founded 1956 by Joe Hardy. Run by his daughter **Maggie Hardy Knox** as CEO/President since [VERIFY]. ~300+ stores, ~$5B revenue [VERIFY]. Sells to professional builders, custom-home builders, and large GCs primarily — not to DIYers (unlike Home Depot/Lowe's). Strong brand loyalty among framers and custom-home builders.

### Why they fit the Sherpa Hub thesis
- **New trade category**: lumber + building materials is **additive** to Webb's PVF pilot — zero category conflict, parallel pilots run cleanly
- **Founder-led private company** — fastest possible decision velocity. LOIs can move in weeks, not quarters
- **Customer overlap is GC-focused**, not trade-sub-focused — so the Sherpa Hub member acquisition pool is broader (whole-home GCs in addition to trade subs)
- **Existing pro-builder loyalty** — 84 Lumber's pro customers are already sticky, so Hub co-location strengthens an already-strong relationship rather than rescuing a weak one
- **No visible pro-marketplace partnerships today** [VERIFY] — clean sheet for Sherpa Pros to be the first

### Decision-maker title
**CEO/President Maggie Hardy Knox** directly, or **Chief Operating Officer** [VERIFY]. Founder-led private companies route partnership decisions to the top — there is no intermediate "VP Strategic Partnerships" gatekeeper.

### Ice-breaker hook
> "The custom-home GCs you've built 84 Lumber's reputation around are the exact pros bleeding 60-90 minutes a day to materials runs. We're piloting a co-located 'Sherpa Hub' inside FW Webb branches in New England — the per-branch economics produce $165-210K incremental EBITDA in Year 1. Lumber is a complementary category. Would 84 Lumber want to be the lumber-side hero pilot? I'd love 30 minutes."

The hook leans on **founder-to-founder peer alignment** + **complementary not competitive** + **specific economic outcome** + **simple ask** (30 min, not boil-the-ocean).

### Warm-intro path candidates
- **Custom-home GCs** who buy at both 84 Lumber and Webb (HJD Builders is a candidate)
- **Building-industry trade orgs** — NAHB, NHBA, regional builder associations
- **Family-owned business networks** — both Sherpa Pros and 84 Lumber are founder-led; conferences like EOS Worldwide, YPO Hardy Knox circles
- **Pittsburgh business community** — 84 Lumber is HQ'd in Eighty Four, PA; Phyrom can leverage NE-to-PA second-degree connections [VERIFY]

### Expected counter-offer to Webb's exclusivity
84 Lumber's category is non-competing with Webb, so the exclusivity counter is straightforward:
1. **Geographic preference** — 84 Lumber will likely ask for **first-look on lumber-side Hubs in any region** where they have stores (functionally national for lumber category)
2. **Faster expansion gate** — given founder-led decision speed, they may push to **expand from 5 to 10 Hubs by Year-2** rather than the standard 25-Hub Year-2 expansion. Phyrom should accept gladly
3. **Co-branding ask** — 84 Lumber may want stronger joint branding inside the Hub than Webb wanted. Manageable through Exhibit E (Joint Marketing Approval workflow)

Risk: 84 Lumber asks for **equity in Sherpa Pros** rather than (or in addition to) the standard LOI. Phyrom should be open to a small strategic investor slot if it speeds the LOI signature.

## Target #3 — Rexel USA

### Who they are
US arm of French parent Rexel SA (publicly traded on Euronext Paris). ~400+ branches across US [VERIFY]. Major electrical-distribution player serving electricians, electrical contractors, and industrial maintenance pros. Competitors include Graybar, CED, City Electric, WESCO. Electrical pros have the **highest per-pro materials spend** of any trade ($80K-$100K/yr median [VERIFY]) because of high copper/conduit/switchgear costs.

### Why they fit the Sherpa Hub thesis
- **Highest per-Hub member economics** of any trade category — electrical pro's annual spend is roughly 1.5-2x a plumbing pro's, so the **counter-sales-lift line in the per-branch model is structurally larger**
- **PE-owned parent** with public-market cost-of-capital pressure to defend share — strategic openness to defensive moats
- **Third trade category** alongside Webb's PVF + 84 Lumber's lumber — fills out the trade-category stool
- **Branch footprint** in major metros where Webb is absent (West, Southwest, Midwest) — geographic complementarity
- **Electrical pros are highly digital-native** — already using mobile-first ordering; the Sherpa Hub workflow layer slots in cleanly

### Decision-maker title
**President — Rexel USA** (currently **Brad Paulsen** [VERIFY]) or **VP Strategic Partnerships** / **VP Pro Sales**. Parent company in France approves multi-branch partnerships above a threshold ($X CapEx) [VERIFY threshold].

### Ice-breaker hook
> "Electrical pros have the highest per-job materials spend of any trade — and the longest counter wait times. We're piloting co-located Sherpa Hubs inside FW Webb's plumbing/HVAC branches in NE; per-branch economics: $165-210K Year-1 incremental EBITDA, with Year-1 counter-sales lift driving most of it. Electrical economics should be 1.5x stronger because of higher per-pro spend. Would Rexel USA want to be the electrical-side hero pilot? Non-overlapping with Webb, additive category."

The hook leans on **higher-than-Webb economic upside** (because of higher per-pro spend) + **non-conflicting category** + **leverage Webb proof point**.

### Warm-intro path candidates
- **IBEW + electrical-contractor industry orgs** (NECA — National Electrical Contractors Association)
- **Mutual electrical-contractor customers**

### Expected counter-offer to Webb's exclusivity
Rexel's category is non-competing with Webb:

Phyrom should accept the smaller pilot and adjust the per-Hub economic model accordingly
2. **Deeper integration ask** — Rexel may ask Sherpa Materials to integrate with their existing **Trade Service / Eclipse ERP** [VERIFY] rather than going Zinc-only. Manageable in Exhibit C
3. **French-parent approval delay** — multi-branch partnership above a CapEx threshold likely requires parent-board approval, adding **4-8 weeks** to LOI timeline. Build into Phase 1 calendar accordingly

Risk: parent-company approval delay pushes the LOI past Phase-1-Month-4 deadline. Mitigation: start Rexel outreach in Phase-1-Week-1 in parallel with Webb, not sequentially.

## Cross-target dependencies + parallel-track strategy

All three targets should be **in active conversation by Phase 1 Week 4**, not sequenced. The reasoning:

- Webb is the **hero pilot**: deepest economic model, signed LOI, full deck
- Ferguson is the **scale demonstrator**: same category, 24x branch count, validates the playbook works beyond NE
- 84 Lumber is the **speed demonstrator**: founder-led, signs fastest, proves the category-template works
- Rexel is the **economics demonstrator**: highest per-pro spend, validates Hub economics scale up with trade-category density

By Phase 1 Month 6, the goal is **Webb LOI signed + at least one secondary LOI signed + at least one secondary in active diligence**. The secondary slate de-risks the hero-pilot dependency.

Founder-only communication channel: Phyrom owns all four conversations directly until LOI execution. No delegation of partner-relationship management until post-LOI.
