# Sherpa Materials Engine Goes Live, Compressing 90 Minutes of Daily Jobsite Friction Into Background Coordination for New England Builders

**Sherpa Pros' new materials surface coordinates supplier orders across 40+ partners and routes same-day delivery via Uber Direct — without the contractor or skilled trades worker leaving the jobsite.**

**MANCHESTER, NH — [VERIFY-DATE]** — Sherpa Pros, the New Hampshire-based skilled-trades dispatch and coordination platform, today announced that its Sherpa Materials engine is live in production and coordinating real orders for the company's beta cohort of New England general contractors and skilled-trades professionals.

The Materials engine is the third major surface in the Sherpa Pros operating system, joining the Dispatch surface (live since the platform's founding) and the Code surface (live earlier this quarter). Together, the three surfaces represent what founder Phyrom describes as "the operating system for the residential and small-commercial GC — the segment of the construction market that's been served by spreadsheets, group texts, and 18-year-old habits since long before any of us got into the trades."

The Materials engine resolves natural-language materials requests typed by a Sherpa on a phone — often one-handed, on-site, between cuts — against a structured catalog of approximately 28,000 SKUs spanning 40+ supplier partners, including big-box pro accounts, regional distributors, and specialty trade suppliers. The system places orders through Zinc's cross-retailer API and books same-day last-mile delivery via Uber Direct. Total elapsed time from Sherpa job-acceptance to materials on the jobsite curb: typically under 30 minutes.

For New England GCs participating in the beta, the operational impact has been immediate. "I've never had this happen before in 18 years," wrote one Manchester-area builder in a message to the company during week one — describing materials arriving at his jobsite before he had finished demoing the existing fixtures. The platform's beta cohort is currently coordinating [VERIFY-METRIC] orders per week across the partner Sherpa workforce.

"We built the Materials engine because every Sherpa on our platform was bleeding 90 minutes a day to supply runs, and every GC was absorbing the cost," said Phyrom, founder of Sherpa Pros. "The construction industry has tried to solve this for 30 years with bigger trucks, bulk purchasing, and pro accounts at the chains. None of those approaches changed the fundamental problem, which is that the skilled tradesperson is the one driving for parts. We changed who drives. Now Uber drives, and the Sherpa works."

Phyrom continued: "The vertical-integration thesis here is straightforward. The competitive set for what we just shipped is not Angi or Thumbtack — those are lead-generation tools. The right comp set is FW Webb plus Grainger plus Uber Direct plus Procore, stitched into one transaction layer for the residential GC market that none of those four serve well alone. The U.S. materials TAM in our segment is approximately $540 billion annually. We're taking a coordination fee on materials that were already going to flow through every job we touch."

The Sherpa Materials engine charges an 8-12% blended coordination fee, structured per the company's published Spec R7 economics. Critically, the fee is paid from the time, fuel, and rework waste the platform eliminates — not from the GC's margin on the materials line itself. Average residential GC margin on materials under the prevailing model sits at 0-3%; under the Sherpa Materials model, that line-item margin is unchanged, but the GC recovers an estimated 90 minutes of crew labor per day previously lost to supply runs.

The Materials engine is currently available to Sherpa Pros' closed beta cohort of New England general contractors and skilled-trades professionals. The waitlist for the next cohort wave is open at the company's Wefunder page; Wefunder backers receive priority placement.
