Sherpa Pros Operations Manual — Outline

0. About This Outline

This document is the structural outline for the Sherpa Pros Operations Manual (Volume 1 of the Franchisee Manual Set; Volume 2 is the Brand Manual per 09-brand-manual-outline.md). The full Operations Manual is approximately 150 pages when complete and is delivered to every franchisee at signing as a perpetual operations license under the Sherpa Operations License Agreement (FDD Item 22 exhibit).

The Operations Manual is the day-to-day "how do I run this Hub" reference — calibrated for the working Hub Manager who needs to know, at 5:43 AM on a Tuesday, what to do when an inbound supplier delivery is short three pallets and the morning Uber Direct dispatch wave is 47 minutes away.

Critical: The Operations Manual is mandatory compliance. Item 16 of the FDD restricts the franchisee to operating the Hub per the standards in this manual. Material deviation triggers cure-period language in Item 17 and may result in termination. Standards drift quietly over years — the annual Quarterly Business Review (QBR) cycle catches drift.

2. Daily Operations Runbook (Pages 9–34)

(Per docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-25-sherpa-hub-integration-design.md §6)

Section 2.1 — Pre-Open (4:30–7:00 AM)

  • 4:30 AM — Hub Manager arrival; alarm disarm; security walkthrough
  • 5:00 AM — Materials staging start (warehouse side; receiving dock pre-pull, scan against staging manifest, build job-labeled racks)
  • 5:30 AM — Discrepancy reconciliation (any short-shipped pallets logged via Sherpa Guard, HQ Operations notified)
  • 6:00 AM — Top-50 SKU buffer-stock spot count
  • 6:30 AM — First Uber Direct wave dispatches (early-start trades: framing, drywall, masonry)
  • 6:45 AM — Hub Associate arrival (if scheduled)
  • 7:00 AM — Hub opens (showroom side opens to members)

Section 2.2 — Mid-Day (7:00 AM – 12:00 PM)

  • 8:00 AM — Second Uber Direct wave (general trades)
  • 9:00 AM – 11:00 AM — Member walk-ins peak; kit pickup, parts, rentals, training event RSVP
  • 10:00 AM — Third Uber Direct wave (mid-morning catch-up)
  • 11:30 AM — Lunch coverage transition (Hub Manager + 1 Associate; one stays on counter)
  • 12:00 PM — Fourth Uber Direct wave (last morning push)

Section 2.3 — Afternoon (12:00 – 5:30 PM)

  • 12:00 – 2:00 PM — Lower-traffic window; maintenance tasks, inventory restocking, training event prep, member newsletter drafting
  • 2:00 – 4:30 PM — Mid-afternoon walk-ins; rental returns, equipment exchanges
  • 4:30 PM — Afternoon SKU spot count (top-200 high-velocity items)
  • 5:00 PM — End-of-day cash drop (if applicable; default cashless per security playbook)
  • 5:30 PM — Final inventory check; Sherpa Pros corporate P&L pull (automated)

Section 2.4 — Close (5:30 – 6:30 PM)

  • 5:30 PM — Last walk-ins
  • 6:00 PM — Hub closes; member badge access disabled until next morning
  • 6:00 – 6:30 PM — Hub Manager closing checklist (alarm armed, lights off, exterior signage on, security camera verification)
  • 6:30 PM — Hub Manager departs

Section 2.5 — Training Event Evenings (Tuesdays + Thursdays, 6:00 – 8:00 PM)

  • 5:30 – 6:00 PM — Training room setup; manufacturer sponsor arrival
  • 6:00 PM — Hub re-opens for training event (member-only; badge required)
  • 6:00 – 7:30 PM — Training delivery
  • 7:30 – 8:00 PM — Q&A + networking + sponsor sample distribution
  • 8:00 PM — Training event ends; Hub Manager closes

Section 2.6 — Saturday Optional Open Hours (Phase 1: closed default; Phase 4+: 8 AM – 12 PM optional based on Hub Manager judgment + member demand)

  • Setup, member-only, light-staffing model

3. Weekly Cadence (Pages 35–50)

Section 3.1 — Monday: Inventory Audit

  • Cycle count workflow (1/4 of warehouse per Monday so full audit completes monthly)
  • Variance investigation protocol (any SKU off by > 3% or > $200)
  • Reconciliation of prior week's deliveries against Zinc invoices
  • Week-ahead training event prep (RSVP count check, sponsor confirmation, materials staging)

Section 3.2 — Wednesday: Returns Processing Batch

  • Sort returns by category (warranty, restock, customer return, defective)
  • Photograph each return for RMA submission
  • Tag with RMA number per supplier-specific format
  • Stage in returns area for Friday supplier pickup

Section 3.3 — Friday: Supplier Pickup + Newsletter + P&L Pull

  • Supplier pickup of returns batch (FW Webb truck, Grainger truck, supplier-direct freight)
  • Member newsletter draft sent to corporate Brand Lead for review (24-hour turnaround)
  • Weekly P&L snapshot pulled from POS system (automated)
  • Hub Manager weekly journal entry (operational notes, member feedback, supply issues, staff issues)

Section 3.4 — Saturday Optional

  • 8 AM – 12 PM open hours if Hub Manager elects
  • Light staffing (Hub Manager + 1 Associate)
  • Reduced services (no kit assembly, no training events; pickup + walk-in only)

4. Monthly Cadence (Pages 51–66)

Section 4.1 — First Tuesday: P&L Review with Corporate Operations

  • Hub Manager pulls full month P&L from POS system
  • Pre-call: review prior-month variance against budget; prepare commentary on top 3 variances
  • Call (60 minutes, video): regional VPOPS + Hub Manager
  • Topics covered: revenue mix vs target, COGS variances, OpEx variances, royalty + Marketing Fund + Tech Fee remittance status, member count + churn, materials throughput, rental utilization, training revenue, capex needs, staffing changes, brand standards compliance
  • Deliverable: signed monthly P&L review summary; action items logged in FranConnect

Section 4.2 — Mid-Month: Member Net Promoter Score (NPS) Survey

  • Corporate-supplied 10-question survey (90-second target completion time)
  • Sent via Short Message Service (SMS) to all active Hub members
  • Target response rate: 35%+
  • Target NPS score: 50+ at maturity (Year 3+)
  • Survey results visible in real-time on Hub Manager dashboard
  • Action: Hub Manager personally responds to any NPS detractor (score < 7) within 48 hours

Section 4.3 — Last Friday: Capex Review

  • Hub Manager submits capex requests for next month (any equipment needing replacement, inventory category needing rethink, IT/security upgrade)
  • Approval thresholds:
    • Up to $5,000: HQ Operations approval (typical 48-hour turnaround)
    • $5,000 – $25,000: Monthly P&L review meeting decision
    • $25,000+: Annual budget cycle decision (or emergency capex committee for true emergencies)
  • Capex log maintained in FranConnect

Section 4.4 — Monthly Royalty + Marketing Fund + Tech Fee Remittance

  • 15th of following month: Hub Manager uploads prior-month P&L to FranConnect
  • 18th of following month: FranConnect calculates royalty + Marketing Fund + Tech Fee
  • 20th of following month: Stripe Connect ACH auto-debit pulls funds (per 07-vendor-accounts-setup.md §5)
  • 25th of following month: Reconciliation report generated; Hub Manager reviews + signs

5. Quarterly Cadence (Pages 67–78)

Section 5.1 — Quarterly Business Review (QBR) — Corporate Hubs

  • 4-hour deep dive at the Hub or at Sherpa University
  • Attendees: regional VPOPS + corporate VPOPS + Phyrom (presence at first 5 Hubs; delegated thereafter)
  • Format: 90-min Hub financial health, 60-min member health, 60-min operational health, 30-min growth plan for next quarter
  • Deliverable: QBR scorecard with red/yellow/green status across 12 metrics; signed action plan

Section 5.2 — Quarterly Business Review — Franchise Hubs

  • 4-hour deep dive at the franchisee's Hub (rotating between in-person and video for Year 2+)
  • Attendees: franchisee + Hub Manager + regional VPOPS + Franchise Advisory Council (FAC) liaison
  • Format: same as Corporate Hub QBR
  • Deliverable: QBR scorecard + signed action plan; results aggregated to FAC quarterly review

Section 5.3 — Quarterly All-Hub Call

  • All Hub Managers + regional VPOPS + corporate VPOPS
  • 90-minute video call
  • Format: knowledge sharing (1 Hub Manager presents a winning playbook), cross-Hub incident debriefs (security incidents, materials orchestration disruptions, member churn investigations), Sherpa Materials throughput trends network-wide, training event playbook refinements, upcoming corporate-led campaigns
  • Recording archived in FranConnect Knowledge Base

Section 5.4 — Quarterly Continuing Education Course (per FDD Item 11)

  • 2-hour online course; topic varies per quarter (per 08-80-hour-training-curriculum.md)
  • Asynchronous delivery (must complete within 30 days of quarter-end)
  • Hub Manager + franchisee both required to complete
  • Completion tracked in FranConnect Learning Management System

6. Member Onboarding Workflow (Pages 79–88)

Section 6.1 — New Pro Discovery → Member Conversion (Days 0–7)

  • Pro discovers Sherpa Pros via Marketplace job acceptance, training event walk-in, FW Webb branch referral, NHHBA referral, or word-of-mouth
  • Pro registers via Sherpa Pros Mobile app
  • Subscription tier selection (Founding / Gold / Silver / Bronze / Flex per Hub Integration spec §5)
  • Hub Manager auto-notified of new member in territory
  • Welcome email triggered (corporate template + Hub Manager personalization)

Section 6.2 — Day 1: Welcome Packet

  • Hub Manager schedules in-person welcome (within 7 days of conversion)
  • Welcome packet handed off (per 09-brand-manual-outline.md §5.1): welcome letter, member benefits one-pager, Hub map + hours, contact card, branded hat + sticker pack, first training event invitation
  • Hub Manager 15-minute orientation: Hub tour, badge issuance, Sherpa Mobile app walkthrough

Section 6.3 — Day 7–30: First Engagement

  • First training event invitation (auto-RSVP'd; Hub Manager personal follow-up)
  • First materials order via Sherpa Materials (Hub Manager monitors for friction)
  • First marketplace job dispatch (Hub Manager monitors Sherpa Score progression)
  • Day 30 check-in call from Hub Manager (15 minutes)

Section 6.4 — Day 30–90: Activation

  • Member attends 1+ training event (target)
  • Member places 5+ materials orders through Sherpa Materials (target)
  • Member completes 3+ marketplace jobs (target for full-time pros)
  • Hub Manager logs member activation in FranConnect

Section 6.5 — Day 90+: Retention

  • Member moves to retention cadence (monthly newsletter, quarterly NPS survey, annual member appreciation event)
  • Hub Manager flags any churn risk (no engagement 60+ days, NPS detractor)
  • Churn intervention playbook (see Section 9 below)

7. Sherpa Materials Hub-Side Operations (Pages 89–106)

(Per docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-25-sherpa-hub-integration-design.md §7 + §8)

Section 7.1 — Inbound Receiving Workflow

  • 4:00–9:00 PM prior-day delivery window
  • Supplier truck or Zinc-coordinated freight arrival
  • Pallet manifest scan into POS
  • Reconciliation against hub_materials_staging.expected_at
  • Discrepancy logging via Sherpa Guard
  • HQ Operations notification protocol for material variances

Section 7.2 — Staging Workflow (5:00 – 7:00 AM)

  • Materials pulled from receiving dock to job-labeled racks
  • Each rack labeled with: job ID, pro name, dispatch wave, dispatch method, expected dispatch time
  • Inventory system updates hub_materials_staging.staged_at
  • Quality check: photograph each rack before dispatch (audit trail)

Section 7.3 — Wave Dispatch Workflow (6:30 / 8:00 / 10:00 / 12:00)

  • Uber Direct API called from Hub system per wave
  • Each wave bundles all jobs in same dispatch window
  • Hub Associate hands off to Uber Direct driver
  • QR code scan on rack label
  • System writes hub_materials_staging.dispatched_at

Section 7.4 — Pro-Pickup Workflow

  • Pro arrives at Hub between 6 AM – noon
  • Member badge scan
  • System surfaces staged materials assigned to that pro
  • Pro takes materials; system writes dispatch_method = 'pro_pickup'
  • Higher Hub margin per line (no Uber Direct cost subtracted) — per Hub Integration spec §8

Section 7.5 — Returns Processing (Wednesday Batch)

  • Pro returns materials at any time (logged in member return queue)
  • Wednesday: Hub Materials Specialist processes batch
  • Sort, photograph, RMA-tag, batch-label
  • Friday supplier pickup or scheduled return freight clears queue
  • Returns credited to Sherpa Materials gross margin pool

Section 7.6 — FW Webb Partnership-Specific Workflow (per Hub Integration spec §4)

  • FW Webb default supplier for stocked SKUs
  • FW Webb branch ships to Hub (when Hub is co-located inside FW Webb branch, internal transfer; when standalone, freight)
  • Sherpa pro discount card honors FW Webb in-branch pricing
  • Hub-FW Webb branch reconciliation: weekly inventory variance sync
  • Escalation: corporate-FW Webb account manager for any variance > $1,000/week

Section 7.7 — Margin Calculation Reference

  • Hub buffer stock (Hub stocked it, Hub fulfilled it): 40% margin share to Hub
  • Hub staged + Uber Direct (Hub received + staged + dispatched): 25% margin share
  • Hub staged + pro pickup (Hub received + staged, pro picked up): 35% margin share
  • Supplier direct (Hub not involved): 0% margin share

8. Training Event Programming Standards (Pages 107–116)

Section 8.1 — Annual Training Calendar

  • 12 events per year (1 per month + 0 in slow months + extras in busy months — Hub Manager judgment)
  • Mix: manufacturer-sponsored events (8 per year, free or low-fee), corporate-led events (2 per year, NHHBA-equivalent CEU credit), Hub Manager–led events (2 per year, member spotlight)

Section 8.2 — Event Format Standards

  • 90-minute session + 30-minute Q&A + networking
  • Tuesday or Thursday evenings (6–8 PM)
  • Maximum capacity: 12 attendees (per Hub Integration spec — 12-seat training facility)
  • Member-only (badge required)

Section 8.3 — Sponsor Coordination

  • Manufacturer sponsor pays $2,000–$5,000 per event (depending on Hub size + member count)
  • Sponsor brings: presenter, materials, samples, branded handouts (corporate-approved per Brand Manual §6)
  • Hub provides: venue, equipment demo space, refreshments (corporate-supplied snack budget), member RSVP management

Section 8.4 — Member Recruitment for Events

  • Calendar published 90 days in advance
  • Monthly newsletter promotes upcoming event
  • SMS reminder 7 days, 1 day, and 2 hours before event
  • Member RSVP via Sherpa Mobile app
  • Walk-in attendance allowed if capacity permits

Section 8.5 — Post-Event Workflow

  • Attendance scanned via member badge
  • Sherpa Score bonus for event attendance (member benefit)
  • Sponsor follow-up email (template-driven, manufacturer co-signed)
  • Event recording archived in FranConnect
  • NPS survey to event attendees

9. Member Retention Playbook (Pages 117–128)

Section 9.1 — Retention Strategy Pillars

  • Community (Hub lounge as social hub for working pros)
  • Career (Sherpa Score progression, training, certification path)
  • Cash flow (more jobs via marketplace, lower materials cost via Sherpa Materials)

Section 9.2 — Lounge Programming Standards

  • Daily: free coffee + snacks during Hub hours
  • Weekly: themed lounge programming (Wednesday "Tools of the Week" demo, Friday "Pro Spotlight" feature)
  • Monthly: member appreciation Friday (catered lunch + raffle)

Section 9.3 — Annual Member Events

  • Hub Anniversary celebration (date varies by Hub opening date)
  • Sherpa Pros Founding Pros recognition (annual; corporate-coordinated)
  • Local trade community day (Hub partners with local NHHBA chapter)

Section 9.4 — Churn Diagnosis

  • Member dashboard surfaces 60-day no-engagement flag
  • Hub Manager investigates: outreach, why-disengaged-survey, exit interview if member cancels
  • Root cause categorized: schedule changed, business closing, moved out of territory, dissatisfied with platform, dissatisfied with Hub specifically, found competing platform

Section 9.5 — Churn Intervention

  • 60-day no-engagement: Hub Manager personal call (target conversion to active member within 14 days)
  • Cancellation request: Hub Manager exit interview (target save rate 35%)
  • Lapsed member re-engagement: 90-day re-activation campaign (corporate-supplied template)

10. Financial Management (Pages 129–140)

Section 10.1 — Royalty Payment Schedule

  • 15th of following month: P&L upload
  • 20th of following month: ACH auto-debit
  • Per 07-vendor-accounts-setup.md §5

Section 10.2 — Marketing Fund Contribution

  • 2% of gross revenue, monthly
  • Pulled to Sherpa Pros Marketing Fund LLC dedicated account
  • Per 07-vendor-accounts-setup.md §6

Section 10.3 — Local Marketing Minimum

  • 1.5% of gross revenue, monthly
  • Spent at franchisee's discretion in approved channels (paid search, paid social, local print, NHHBA-equivalent sponsorship, manufacturer co-op)
  • Verified via FranConnect spend reporting (monthly)
  • Underspend triggers cure-period notice; overspend allowed

Section 10.4 — Audit Cooperation

  • Annual audit: BDO USA Franchise Services (per 02-audit-firm-evaluation.md) cooperates with franchisor entity audit; franchisee P&L data flows in via FranConnect roll-up
  • Ad-hoc audit: triggered if FDD Item 6 audit fee threshold trips (underreporting > 2%)
  • Franchisee provides: bank statements, POS export, supplier invoices, employment records (limited to franchise-relevant scope)
  • Cooperation expectation: 14-day response window for any auditor request

Section 10.5 — P&L Review Cadence

  • Monthly: Hub Manager + regional VPOPS (60 minutes, video)
  • Quarterly: Hub Manager + franchisee + corporate (4-hour QBR)
  • Annual: Hub Manager + franchisee + corporate (full annual budget review)

Section 10.6 — Annual Budget Submission

  • Franchisee submits annual operating plan by November 15 for following calendar year
  • Corporate reviews + approves by December 31
  • Plan covers: revenue goals, pro-recruiting goals, capital investments, marketing spend
  • Variance reporting throughout the year against approved budget

11. Security + Insurance Procedures (Pages 141–146)

Section 11.1 — Physical Security

  • Keypad + member badge access
  • 8-camera IP surveillance (cloud + local NVR)
  • High-value cage for tool rental fleet (RFID + steel mesh)
  • Cashless preferred; if cash, end-of-day drop into safe
  • Per Hub Integration spec §6 security playbook

Section 11.2 — Cyber Security

  • POS system: corporate-managed (no franchisee-side configuration)
  • Member data: never stored locally on Hub computers (cloud-only via Sherpa Pros platform)
  • Wi-Fi: separate guest network and operations network
  • Sherpa Guard audit logs all access events

Section 11.3 — Insurance Coverage (per FDD Item 9 obligation)

  • General Liability: $2M aggregate / $1M per occurrence (~$1,400/year)
  • Property (building improvements + inventory + equipment): ~$1,100/year
  • Business Interruption (90-day): ~$400/year
  • Crime / Employee Dishonesty: ~$350/year
  • Cyber (POS + member data): ~$300/year
  • Workers' Compensation per state requirement
  • Total per Hub: ~$3,550/year (per Hub Integration spec §6)
  • Carrier: The Hartford or Chubb preferred; corporate-supplied broker introduction at signing

Section 11.4 — Incident Response

  • Theft: documented escalation tree (Hub Manager → regional VPOPS → corporate → law enforcement)
  • Fire: documented evacuation + corporate notification protocol
  • Medical emergency: documented response procedure
  • Quarterly drill on each scenario

12. Hub Manager Hiring + Management Framework (Pages 147–150)

Section 12.1 — Hiring Framework

  • Job description: corporate-supplied (modifiable for local market language only)
  • Sourcing channels: FW Webb branch managers, Lowe's Pro Desk, Home Depot Pro Desk, trade-supply-house counter managers, NHHBA referrals
  • Structured interview (5-stage funnel)
  • HireRight background check (per 07-vendor-accounts-setup.md §7)
  • Reference checks (3 minimum)

Section 12.2 — Compensation Framework

  • Salary: $58–72K NH/ME / $65–85K MA/RI metro
  • Bonus: 8% of net Hub revenue, paid quarterly, capped at 25% of base
  • Benefits: health insurance (corporate-recommended group plan; franchisee can opt out for own plan), 401(k) with corporate-recommended provider
  • PTO: 15 days year 1, 20 days year 3+

Section 12.3 — Performance Management

  • Monthly 1-on-1 with franchisee (60 minutes)
  • Quarterly performance review (90 minutes)
  • Annual performance review (120 minutes; ties to bonus payout + base raise)
  • Documented in FranConnect

Section 12.4 — Retention Strategies

  • Career pathing: Hub Manager → Multi-Hub Operations Director (Phase 4 Year 3+ when franchisee owns 2+ Hubs)
  • Recognition: Hub of the Year award at Annual Franchisee Summit
  • Training: full continuing education access (8 quarterly courses + Annual Summit)
  • Equity-style profit-sharing: optional bonus structure for Hub Managers exceeding revenue targets 2 years running

Section 12.5 — Succession Planning

  • Always-on identification of next Hub Manager candidate (internal Hub Associate or external pipeline)
  • Documented succession plan reviewed annually at QBR
  • Backup Hub Manager designated for all extended-leave scenarios (vacation, family leave, medical leave, transition)
