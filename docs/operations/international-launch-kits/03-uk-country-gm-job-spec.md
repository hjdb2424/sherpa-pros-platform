## Required experience

- **10+ years UK construction industry leadership**, OR **8+ years construction + 4+ years marketplace / Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) leadership**.
- Demonstrated **Profit & Loss (P&L) ownership** of GBP £20M+ annual revenue line of business.
- **Local trade industry network** — relationships with the major builders' merchants, online tradesperson marketplaces, and trade associations listed below.
- Day-1 ability to navigate UK regulatory regime: **United Kingdom General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)**, **Consumer Rights Act 2015**, **British Franchise Association (BFA) Code of Ethics** (voluntary self-regulation).
- **English fluency required.**
- Bachelor's degree minimum; UK Master of Business Administration (MBA) (London Business School, Saïd, Judge, Cranfield) a plus.

## UK regulatory regime — required day-1 knowledge

| Domain | Statute / regulator | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Data privacy | **UK GDPR** (post-Brexit UK adaptation of EU General Data Protection Regulation), Data Protection Act 2018, Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) | Pro and client personal data |
| Consumer rights | **Consumer Rights Act 2015**, Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) | Online marketplace consumer protection |
| Franchise regulation | **No statutory franchise law**; voluntary **British Franchise Association (BFA) Code of Ethics** | Master-franchisee model is governed by contract + general commercial law |
| Worker classification | HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) IR35 / off-payroll working rules, Employment Rights Act 1996 | Independent contractor vs employee vs "worker" 3-tier UK status |
| Tax | Value Added Tax (VAT) 20% standard, Corporation Tax 25%, Pay-As-You-Earn (PAYE) | Marketplace VAT collection + remittance |
| Trade licensing | No single national licensing regime; voluntary trade-body schemes (Gas Safe Register, NICEIC for electrical, OFTEC for oil heating, Federation of Master Builders (FMB) accreditation) | Pro vetting per trade |
| Building safety | **Building Safety Act 2022**, Health and Safety Executive (HSE), Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015 (CDM 2015) | Codes engine + safety surfacing |
