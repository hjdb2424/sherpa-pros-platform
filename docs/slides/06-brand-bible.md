# Who Sherpa Pros is

> *The licensed-trade marketplace that thinks like a contractor.*
> *Built by a working GC. Code-aware. Permit-aware. Rebate-aware.*

We are the **national** licensed-trade marketplace (international roadmap) — built by a working New Hampshire general contractor for the contractors he hires every week. Launching in NH/ME/MA Phase 1, expanding nationally through Phase 4.

We solve the two things lead-gen platforms structurally cannot: **verified licensed work** and **code-aware matching**.

We are not selling leads. We are sending jobs.

<br>

<span class="small">Read this paragraph before you write any external copy. Every line of marketing flows from it.</span>

---

# Who we are NOT

Saying who we are not is how we say who we are.

| Platform | What they do | Why we are different |
|---|---|---|
| **Angi** | Sells leads to contractors | We send jobs, not leads. Pros pay only when work happens. |
| **Thumbtack** | Sells leads + collects deposits | We hold customer payment until the job is done. Documented deposit-theft losses $300–$1,600 are not our model. |
| **TaskRabbit** | Unskilled task / assembly | We are licensed trade work. Toilet installs, panels, heat pumps. |
| **Houzz** | Designer portfolio + magazine | We move money and dispatch crews. Not a magazine. |
| **Handy** | Subscription cleanings + assembly | We are jobs by the job. No subscription bundle. |

<br>

<span class="small">The licensed + code-aware + true-marketplace quadrant was empty. We planted the flag there.</span>

---

# Four audiences. One DNA.

Same brand, different headline per audience. Each line is approved copy from the bible (§3.2).

| Audience | Headline |
|---|---|
| **Residential homeowners** | Trusted neighbor pros. Real people, verified work, no leads-for-sale. |
| **Specialty / older-home owners** | Licensed specialty work. Code-aware. Permit-aware. Rebate-aware. National platforms can't filter for this. |
| **Contractors (national)** | Jobs. Not leads. Get paid for work, not for paying to bid. |
| **Property Managers (national)** | Work orders, preferred vendors, unit-level finance in one platform. Stop juggling 40 contractors in spreadsheets. |

<br>

<span class="small">Audience messages reference *use cases* (residential, specialty, older-home, multi-family), not *regions*. Every market has homeowners, specialty work, and property managers. The launch sequence is operational; the audience is national. When you write for an audience, pull their headline. Do not invent a new one. Do not blend two.</span>

# Voice principles

**Plainspoken. Direct. Tradesperson-friendly. Dry. Zero marketing-speak. 8th-grade reading level.**

He talks like a guy who actually swung a hammer this morning — because he did.

<div class="good">

**ON-VOICE** — *"I just paid $87 for a lead that ghosted me. The plumber I called paid $112 for the same lead. The 'homeowner' was never real."*

</div>

<div class="bad">

**OFF-VOICE** — *"Sherpa Pros leverages an AI-powered, next-generation marketplace solution to disrupt the legacy lead-generation paradigm and unlock unprecedented value for the construction ecosystem."*

</div>

<div class="good">

**ON-VOICE** — *"A homeowner called me yesterday. She'd had 3 contractors no-show in 2 weeks."*

</div>

<div class="bad">

**OFF-VOICE** — *"Customer churn driven by supply-side reliability friction is a key vertical opportunity."*

</div>

<div class="rule">

# NEVER say

The bright lines. Each one is banned for a reason.

<div class="bad">

**"Wiseman"** — internal product name. If it leaks externally we look like an AI gimmick instead of a contractor's tool. Externally call it: *the platform · code validation · permit assist · rebate lookup · the matching engine.*

</div>

<div class="bad">

**"Gig" / "Task"** — frames our pros as TaskRabbit-style unskilled labor. They are licensed trade.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**"Uber for X" externally** — internal shorthand only. Externally it cheapens the licensed-trade positioning.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**"AI-powered" as headline** — investors and pros tune out. AI is supporting cast. The contractor is the lead.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**"Disrupt" / "Revolutionize"** — buzzword tax. We do not pay it.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**Jargon abbreviations on first use** — CO, SOV, AR, RFI, ARR, NPS, CAC, GMV. Spell out on first use, then the abbreviation is fine.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**Region-anchored brand language** — "the New England licensed-trade marketplace," "Sherpa Pros for NH/ME/MA," "the NE marketplace." The brand is **national** (international roadmap). Launch geography is a Phase 1/2/3 sequence, not the brand identity. Spec §3.4 is canonical.

</div>

# Geographic brand rules

This is the rule that took the longest to learn. Read it twice.

| Rule | Brand line |
|---|---|
| **Brand identity** | Always **national** (international roadmap). Sherpa Pros is THE national licensed-trade marketplace. |
| **Launch geography** | Named operationally where relevant (e.g., "Phase 1 launches in Portsmouth, Manchester, Portland, and Boston"), never as a brand cap. |
| **Founder origin** | "Working New Hampshire general contractor" — true, specific, builds trust. Reference Phyrom's NH-GC identity freely; this is biography, not brand cap. |
| **Pricing bands** | Keep per-metro operational pricing (NH/ME band, MA band, NYC band as we add them). These are real cost-of-labor differences, not brand statements. |
| **State-specific operational notes** | Keep grant programs (MassCEC), trade associations (NHHBA, MEHBA), supply houses (FW Webb), licensing (MA Office of Consumer Affairs) — these are how the work gets done in our launch markets, not brand identity. |

<br>

<div class="good">

**RIGHT** — *"Sherpa Pros is the national licensed-trade marketplace, launching in NH, ME, and MA — expanding nationally."*

</div>

<div class="good">

**RIGHT** — *"Phyrom is a working New Hampshire general contractor running HJD Builders LLC."* (founder biography — true and specific)

</div>

<div class="bad">

**WRONG** — *"the New England licensed-trade marketplace"* / *"Sherpa Pros for NH/ME/MA"* / *"the Northern Triangle marketplace"*

</div>

<br>

<span class="small">**The test:** if you removed the region word, would the sentence still make sense in five years when we're operating in Texas? If yes, the word is operational and stays. If no, it's brand-capping and goes.</span>

# The founder story is the lead hook

Always. Slide 1 of every deck. Line 1 of every pro recruit. Paragraph 1 of every PR placement.

<br>

> *"Hi, I'm Phyrom. I run HJD Builders — a licensed general contractor in New Hampshire."*
>
> *"I built Sherpa Pros because the platforms my crew gets pitched every week — Angi, Thumbtack, TaskRabbit, Handy — sell us leads we can't afford and send homeowners to people who shouldn't be touching their wiring."*

<br>

**Why this works:** people follow people. Logo posts underperform Phyrom's face by 3–5×. Investors back founders, not decks. Pros trust other pros, not platforms.

<br>

<span class="small">If your copy does not have Phyrom's voice in the first paragraph, rewrite it.</span>

# Pricing comparison framing

Brand Guardian audit recommended this be standardized. Pricing is a value comparison, not a price-shopping comparison.

<div class="good">

**ALWAYS** — *"5% take rate vs Angi's effective $400–$800 per closed job."*

</div>

<div class="good">

**ALWAYS** — *"Roughly half the all-in cost a contractor pays Angi today."*

</div>

<div class="good">

**ALWAYS** — *"Pros pay only when work happens. Angi sells the same lead 4 times."*

</div>

<div class="bad">

**NEVER** — *"Cheaper than Angi"*

</div>

<div class="bad">

**NEVER** — *"Lower fees"*

</div>

<div class="bad">

**NEVER** — *"Better deal forever"* · *"5 cents on the dollar"*

</div>

<br>

<span class="small">Why: "cheaper / lower / better deal" invites price-shopping. We invite value comparison. The numbers tell the story; we don't editorialize them.</span>

# Quick Job lane brand consistency

The hardest brand call we made. Quick Jobs ($200–$1,500 toilet installs, paint, ceiling fans) are ON-BRAND because the pros are still licensed where state requires.

**Headline for Quick Job tab:**

> *"Real licensed pros. Pre-priced. Confirmed in 30 minutes. The handyman lane Angi can't deliver."*

<div class="good">

**WE CAN CLAIM** — *"Licensed plumber for $325 toilet install — confirmed in 30 minutes."*

</div>

<div class="good">

**WE CAN CLAIM** — *"Licensed electricians for ceiling fans and GFCIs at handyman prices."*

</div>

<div class="bad">

**WE CANNOT CLAIM** — *"Sherpa Pros = licensed-only"* — true for Project mode, mostly true for Quick Job, but handyman general work is unlicensed in many states.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**WE CANNOT CLAIM** — *"Code-validated"* on every Quick Job — light validation only, not full code engine.

</div>

# Common mistakes the Brand Guardian caught

Real P0 + P1 violations from the audit. Each one is a pattern to learn.

<div class="bad">

**Surname leak** — *"Phyrom Doung, founder of Sherpa Pros."*
**Right** — *"Phyrom, founder of Sherpa Pros."*
**Why it matters:** the surname is unknown. Guessing it forever puts a wrong name in print.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**Internal name in deck** — *"the broader BldSync platform that powers the code-aware layer."*
**Right** — *"the underlying code-intelligence layer that powers code-aware quote validation."*
**Why it matters:** investors think we shipped a different product than the one they bought.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**Stat contradiction** — *Mass Save heat-pump rebate $8,500 max in 2026.*
**Right** — *$10,000+ in 2026* (matches deck, one-pager, for-pros, LinkedIn).
**Why it matters:** one wrong number in front of an investor reads as "founder doesn't know his own numbers."

</div>

<div class="bad">

**Unhyphenated** — *"Old House Verified"*
**Right** — *"Old-House Verified"* (hyphenated everywhere).
**Why it matters:** trademark filing depends on a single canonical spelling.

</div>

<div class="bad">

**AI as headline** — *"AI-powered marketplace for contractors."*
**Right** — *"The licensed-trade marketplace that thinks like a contractor."*
**Why it matters:** "AI-powered" is supporting cast. The contractor is the lead.

</div>
