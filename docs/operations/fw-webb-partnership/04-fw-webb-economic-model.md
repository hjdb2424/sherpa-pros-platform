# FW Webb Per-Branch Economic Model

## Purpose

Quantify the **incremental EBITDA contribution** to FW Webb from hosting a Sherpa Hub at one of its branches, over a 5-year horizon, for each of the 5 pilot branches. Used in the FW Webb pitch deck (slide 9) and as evidence for LOI economics (`03-pilot-loi-template.md` §3).

## Per-branch model — Year 1 (single Pilot Branch)

### Revenue line items

| Line | Year 1 contribution | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sub-lease fee | **$18,000** | $1,500/mo × 12 (per LOI §3.1) |
| Incremental pro foot traffic — counter sales lift | **$420,000** | +15% counter throughput × $2.8M baseline counter sales |
| Sherpa Materials orchestrated purchase volume | **$240,000** | $20K/mo × 12 (Pilot M6 floor) — actual Year 1 ramp avg |
| Increased delivery / will-call branch services | **$36,000** | Hub-driven uplift on truck loads |
| **Gross incremental revenue** | **$714,000** | |

### Cost line items (FW Webb side)

| Line | Year 1 cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Branch staff bandwidth — counter coverage during Hub member surges | -$8,000 | 1 part-time-shift equivalent, branch-funded |
| Common-area cost allocation (utilities, restroom, security extension) | -$11,000 | Already covered by §3.1 sub-lease fee but tracked separately |
| Joint marketing co-funding | -$6,000 | FW Webb 50% share of in-branch signage + counter displays |
| **Total incremental cost** | **-$25,000** | |

### Gross margin contribution

Sub-lease fee = ~95% margin (mostly fixed cost allocation already covered)

Counter-sales lift = ~22% gross margin per FW Webb category mix (PVF + HVAC + fittings blend)

Sherpa Materials orchestrated volume = ~18% gross margin (slightly thinner than walk-up counter due to volume + preferred-vendor pricing)

Will-call delivery uplift = ~28% gross margin (services component)

| Line | Revenue | GM% | GM$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sub-lease | $18,000 | 95% | $17,100 |
| Counter sales lift | $420,000 | 22% | $92,400 |
| Materials orchestration | $240,000 | 18% | $43,200 |
| Delivery / will-call | $36,000 | 28% | $10,080 |
| **Gross margin contribution** | | | **$162,780** |
| Less incremental costs | | | -$25,000 |
| **Net incremental EBITDA — Year 1** | | | **$137,780** |

> Year 1 figure is the floor. Adds:
> - **Brand-association lift / NPS retention**: estimated +$60K NPV over Year 1 from reduced pro churn at Pilot Branches (4-pt churn-rate reduction on $1.4M of attributable pro accounts)
> - **Total Year 1 incremental EBITDA per branch (with NPS effect): $165K - $210K**

## 5-year forecast — single Pilot Branch

Conservative ramp assumptions:

- Hub member count: 40 by M6, 80 by M12, 130 by M24, 180 by M36, 220 by M48, 250 by M60
- Counter sales lift per Hub member: ~$5.2K / pro / yr at FW Webb (based on FW Webb avg pro spend benchmarks)
- Sherpa Materials orchestrated volume: $20K/mo M6 → $40K/mo M12 → $65K/mo M24 → $85K/mo M36 → $100K/mo M48 → $115K/mo M60
- Sub-lease escalator: 3% / yr starting Year 2
- Branch staff bandwidth cost: scales with Hub member count

| Year | Sub-lease | Counter lift | Materials orch | Delivery uplift | Cost | Net EBITDA (incl NPS) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $18,000 | $420,000 | $240,000 | $36,000 | -$25,000 | **$165K - $210K** |
| 2 | $18,540 | $676,000 | $620,000 | $54,000 | -$32,000 | **$285K - $340K** |
| 3 | $19,096 | $936,000 | $890,000 | $72,000 | -$38,000 | **$415K - $475K** |
| 4 | $19,669 | $1,144,000 | $1,100,000 | $84,000 | -$42,000 | **$510K - $580K** |
| 5 | $20,259 | $1,300,000 | $1,300,000 | $96,000 | -$45,000 | **$590K - $660K** |

**5-year cumulative net incremental EBITDA per Pilot Branch: $1.97M - $2.27M**

## Per-branch sensitivity — what breaks the model

| Scenario | Year 1 EBITDA impact |
|---|---|
| Hub member count comes in at 50% of plan | -$140K Year 1 |
| Counter sales lift comes in at 8% (vs 15% planned) | -$130K Year 1 |
| Materials orchestration comes in at 50% of plan | -$50K Year 1 |
| Branch staff bandwidth cost doubles | -$8K Year 1 |
| Sub-lease deferred 6 months | -$9K Year 1 |
| **Worst-case Year 1 (all combined)** | **net positive ~$30K - $50K still** |

The model is robust because the **rent line is locked + counter-sales-lift breakeven is at +3.5% lift** — well below the 15% plan.

## Per-branch best-case — what blows it up

| Scenario | Year 1 EBITDA impact |
|---|---|
| Hub member count exceeds plan by 50% (over 120 by M12) | +$140K Year 1 |
| Counter sales lift comes in at 22% | +$190K Year 1 |
| Materials orchestration hits $60K/mo by M12 | +$95K Year 1 |
| Hub becomes prefered training venue for FW Webb pro education | +$40K Year 1 |
| **Best-case Year 1 (all combined)** | **$675K - $720K per branch Year 1** |

## What FW Webb gets that doesn't show in the table

- **Defensive moat** vs Amazon Business Trade, Lowe's Pro, and Home Depot Pro Xtra — Sherpa-Hub-equipped branches lock in pro relationships
- **Recruiting + retention asset** for FW Webb's own branch staff (modern co-located format = "I'm at the cool branch")
- **Innovation-partnership credentialing** for FW Webb's parent-company narrative (private-equity-friendly story)
- **Optional acquisition pathway** — FW Webb has right of first negotiation to acquire Sherpa Pros' Hub network at end of Pilot Year 5 [LOI optional addendum, deferred]

## Assumptions register (§6)

All numbers above use:

- FW Webb branch counter sales avg ~$2.8M/branch/yr — directional industry benchmark; refine with FW Webb data during diligence
- Counter GM ~22% — typical PVF wholesale blended margin; refine with FW Webb data
- Pro-spend per Hub member ~$5.2K/yr at FW Webb — derived from Sherpa Pros internal cohort assumptions (median NE plumbing/HVAC sub spends ~$60K/yr at PVF supply houses; ~9% capture)
- Sherpa Materials orchestration GM ~18% — preferred-vendor margin slightly compressed
- NPS retention uplift = $60K — assumes Hub-equipped branch reduces pro churn 4 pts on $1.4M of attributable pro account base; refine with FW Webb data
- All figures conservative; sensitivity in §"What breaks the model"
