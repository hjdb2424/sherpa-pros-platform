# The structural gap

Home-services platforms split three ways:

- **Lead-gen pay-to-play** (Angi, Thumbtack) — pros buy contacts, not jobs
- **Task-based gig** (TaskRabbit, Handy) — unskilled labor, no licensed-trade depth
- **Designer/inspiration marketplaces** (Houzz) — aspirational, not execution

**All have well-documented structural weaknesses around licensed-trade verification, code/permit awareness, and contractor economics.**

State-run installer-finders (Mass Save) cover narrow scope (energy efficiency), no marketplace mechanics — no dispatch, no escrow, no review system.

**Sherpa Pros plants the flag in the empty quadrant.**

# Per-Competitor Analysis

---

# Angi (NASDAQ: ANGI)

**FY2025 revenue $1.03B** (down 13% YoY) · Spun off from IAC Q2 2025

**Model:** Lead-gen — $30–$100/lead, often shared with up to 40 competitors. Angi Pro subscription starts $200/mo.

**Documented weaknesses:**
- BBB: not accredited; **1,800+ complaints** 2023–2026
- **Trustpilot 2.1/5** with thousands of 1-star pro reviews
- **Vermont AG settlement Oct 2025** — $100K over misleading "Certified Pro" terminology
- 30% cancellation penalty on remaining contract balance

**Sherpa Pros advantage:** 5% beta take (10% post-beta) vs. Angi's effective $400–$800/closed-job spend. Code-aware quote validation. "Jobs not leads" — exclusive matching, not lead-blast.

---

# Thumbtack

**~$400M FY2024 revenue · $3.2B valuation (June 2021) · ~$699M raised**

**Model:** Lead-gen — pros pay variable per-quote price; consumers post job; pros buy contact.

**Documented weaknesses:**
- Documented **contractor-deposit-theft cases** ($300–$6,500 per BBB/Sitejabber)
- Widely-reported **fake leads** (invalid phone, non-existent customers)
- BBB **pattern-of-complaints review completed Nov 2025**
- Does not mediate "price disagreements" with deposit-keeping contractors

**Sherpa Pros advantage:** Stripe Connect escrow with 7-day hold reduces deposit-theft risk. License + insurance verified before listing. No shared/blasted leads — single matched dispatch.

---

# TaskRabbit (acquired by IKEA 2017)

**~$75M revenue 2025** (private subsidiary, not separately disclosed)

**Model:** Hybrid hourly tasker rates — 15% service fee + 7.5% trust/support fee = **22.5% effective fee load.** IKEA assembly on flat per-item pricing.

**Coverage:** Unskilled tasks (assembly, moving, errands, cleaning, handyman). **Does not cover licensed trades** (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, roofing).

**Documented weaknesses:** High effective fee. Last-minute cancellations. IKEA-flat-rate roll-out cut tasker earnings. Not built for code-bound or permitted work.

**Sherpa Pros advantage:** Different category. When TaskRabbit users need licensed work (electrical for ceiling fan, plumbing for dishwasher), they have nowhere to go inside the platform — Sherpa Pros captures that referral.

---

# Handy (acquired by ANGI 2018, $165.5M)

**Not separately disclosed** since acquisition (rolled into ANGI consolidated revenue)

**Model:** Subscription + per-task; pre-priced cleaning, handyman, basic installs.

**Coverage:** Narrow — cleaning, handyman, basic furniture/TV installs. Limited licensed-trade coverage.

**Documented weaknesses:** Limited trade coverage means most homeowner-significant projects (heat pump, panel upgrade, roof, plumbing rough-in) cannot be booked. ANGI parent absorbs strategic priority away from Handy.

**Sherpa Pros advantage:** Covers the licensed-trade depth Handy explicitly avoids. We own the **high-ticket workflow ($5K–$50K jobs)** where Handy stops at ~$300 tasks.

---

# Houzz

**~$4B valuation · $614M total raised** from Sequoia/NEA/GGV

**Model:** Houzz consumer marketplace (free directory + lead-gen) + **Houzz Pro SaaS** $59–$999/mo per seat.

**Documented weaknesses:**
- **Houzz Pro: 1.03★ BBB across 500+ complaints** in 3 years
- **Sitejabber 2.5★** over 159 reviews
- Contractors report **$5K+ spend with zero leads**
- Lead-blast model (customers contact many pros from one listing)
- 2024 industry-wide design-firm revenue decline; valuation markdown filings

**Sherpa Pros advantage:** Houzz competes on aspirational visuals; we compete on **completing the job today**. Code-aware quote validation is execution-grade, not portfolio-grade.

---

# Mass Save Find-an-Installer

**Utility-funded program tool** (Eversource, National Grid, Unitil, etc.) — N/A revenue, non-commercial.

**Model:** **Basic listing only.** Zip-code search returns EPA-certified contractors.

**Coverage:** Energy efficiency only — heat pumps, HPC contractors, insulation/air-sealing IICs.

**Critical gap:** **No marketplace mechanics.** No dispatch, no escrow, no scheduling, no quotes, no reviews, no in-app messaging, no payment, no permit assist, no rebate calculator, no matching algorithm. **It is a static listing.**

**Sherpa Pros opportunity:** Become the **execution layer** Mass Save's listing is missing. Apply for Mass Save Network → inherit funnel → provide dispatch/escrow/review layer the program cannot.

# What we don't compete on

Stating this clearly de-risks the pitch ("they understand their lane"):

1. **TaskRabbit-style assembly + errands** — gig-economy unskilled. We link out to TaskRabbit when needed.
2. **Houzz-style designer portfolios** — Houzz owns aspirational. Belle Vie (sister property) handles design-concierge.
3. **Home Depot / Lowe's "Pro Services" installs** — tied to retail purchase channels. **Partnership** (materials integration via Zinc), not competition.
4. **Pure SaaS for contractors** (Houzz Pro, Buildertrend) — we don't sell a contractor CRM.
5. **National handyman chains** (Mr. Handyman, Ace) — franchise plays with W-2 tradespeople. Different supply model.
6. **Insurance-tied repair networks** (Servpro, Belfor) — carrier referral lock-in. **Future partnership** (Phase 4).
7. **New-construction GC selection** (BuildZoom, NewHomeSource) — months-long six-figure relationship sale. We focus on $500–$50K jobs.

---

# Defensibility — five moats

1. **Code-aware quote validation** (NEC, IRC, MA Electrical, NH RSA — 192 codes, 480K entries, 284 NH municipalities) — multi-year build no national lead-gen incumbent has incentive to replicate.

2. **Founder-as-GC narrative** (Phyrom of HJD Builders, actively building in NH) — unforgeable trust signal vs. corporate platforms.

3. **Mass Save Network application + Eversource / National Grid Turnkey relationships** — utility-funnel lock-in competitors cannot enter without state-level certification.

4. **5% take rate (Founding Pros forever)** vs. Angi's effective $400–$800/closed-job lead spend — permanent supply-side cost advantage.

5. **Old-House Verified USPTO trademark** — defensible branded category for pre-1950 Boston/triple-decker work no platform owns.
