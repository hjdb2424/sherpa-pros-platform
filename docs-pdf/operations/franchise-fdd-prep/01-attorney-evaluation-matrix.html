Franchise Attorney Evaluation Matrix

1. Purpose

Sherpa Pros LLC must engage a qualified franchise attorney to lead Franchise Disclosure Document (FDD) drafting, state registration filings, ongoing renewal cycles, and franchisee-relationship-law compliance. Item 19 financial performance representations, Item 7 estimated initial investment ranges ($185,000–$420,000), and the 14-day Federal Trade Commission (FTC) Franchise Rule waiting-period mechanics all require franchise-specialist counsel — not a general-corporate lawyer with a "franchise practice on the side." This document evaluates the top 3 candidate firms, defines the scoring rubric, and recommends a primary engagement.

4. Decision Recommendation

Primary engagement: Spadea Lignana, LLC.

Rationale:

  1. Geographic proximity — Philadelphia is a 5-hour drive from Atkinson NH; in-person Discovery Day attendance and on-site Hub #1 walk-throughs are practical without significant travel cost.
  2. Emerging-franchisor specialty — Sherpa Pros at Phase 4 Year 1 has 1–7 franchisees, not 200; Spadea Lignana's practice is calibrated to the emerging-system phase. DLA Piper's strength (large multi-system franchisors) is wasted on Sherpa Pros' initial scale.
  3. Item 19 single-unit substantiation expertise — Hub #1 Atkinson NH provides 12–18 months of operating data when FDD Version 1.0 issues. Spadea Lignana has done this exact substantiation pattern dozens of times.
  4. Within budget — $90K–$120K fits the $80K–$150K budget target with margin for state filing add-ons.
  5. Phase 5 transition path — when international Master Franchisees enter (Phase 5 Year 1+), Sherpa Pros will retain Spadea Lignana for US compliance and add DLA Piper or country-specific counsel for non-US filings. The two-firm structure is industry-standard for franchisors expanding internationally.

Backup engagement (if Spadea Lignana declines or pricing exceeds $150K): Cheng Cohen LLC. Same emerging-franchisor specialty; Midwest location compensated by video-first engagement model.

Defer to Phase 5: DLA Piper. Add as international counsel when international Master Franchisees enter the pipeline (international-expansion spec defers to Phase 5 timeline).

5. Engagement Process

  1. Request for Proposal (RFP) issued — Phase 4 Year 1 Month 1. Send to all 3 candidate firms simultaneously. Include FDD scope summary, Hub #1 operating data summary (under mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement — NDA), priority state filing list (NH/ME/MA non-registration first; NY/RI/MD/VA Phase 4Y2; remaining 10 registration states Phase 4Y3), budget target.
  2. Vendor screening calls — Weeks 2–3. 60-minute video call with each candidate firm; named partner attends. Phyrom + future VPFD attend.
  3. References checked — Weeks 3–4. Contact 3 emerging-franchisor references per finalist (preferably franchisors at Year 1 of FDD).
  4. Engagement letter signed — Week 5. Locked named-partner commitment; locked monthly cap on hours; locked deliverable milestones (Item-by-Item draft delivery dates per WS1-3 through WS1-15 in the franchise-model-plan).
  5. Kickoff meeting — Week 6. In-person at Hub #1 Atkinson NH. Phyrom + VPFD + Chief Financial Officer (CFO) + lead partner attend. Review Hub #1 operating data, walk through every Item, lock Item 19 substantiation methodology.

6. Risk Notes + Open Questions

  • Conflict check — confirm no existing engagement with a directly competitive licensed-trade marketplace (Angi, Thumbtack, TaskRabbit, ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro). Spadea Lignana is most likely conflict-free given their boutique focus on emerging franchisors.
  • Phyrom surname — must be added to Item 2 (Business Experience) before any FDD draft circulates. Flag for Phyrom at engagement letter signing.
  • HJD Builders LLC litigation review — Item 3 disclosure trigger is any HJD Builders LLC contracting matter exceeding $50,000 claimed damages or alleging fraud. Pre-engagement task: Phyrom inventories all HJD Builders LLC pending and concluded litigation past 10 years; counsel reviews materiality.
  • Trademark status — Item 13 disclosure depends on USPTO application status of "Sherpa Pros," "Sherpa Hub," and product-name trademarks. Verify status with intellectual property counsel (separate engagement, possibly same firm) before FDD Version 1.0 finalization.
