### 2.A — 1099 Worker Classification Opinion Memo

**Background facts the memo must work from:**

- Pros on Sherpa Pros are independently licensed contractors in their state of operation (NH OPLC, MA Office of Consumer Affairs / state board, ME OPR / municipal). Trades include licensed electricians, plumbers, HVAC technicians, EPA-certified heat pump installers, general contractors, painters, handymen.
- Each pro carries their own Commercial General Liability (minimum $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate; $500K minimum for handyman-only Quick Job lane), Workers' Compensation (statutory minimums, sole-proprietor exemptions where applicable), Commercial Auto ($1M combined single limit), and state surety bond (NH/MA/ME each differ).
- Pros set their own bid prices (in Project mode) or accept a posted price band (in Quick Job mode). Pros may decline any job. Pros own their tools, vehicles, materials inventory, and scheduling.
- Pros may freely use other platforms (Angi, Thumbtack, direct customers).
- Sherpa Pros provides: pro/customer matching, payment processing via Stripe Connect, dispute mediation, license/insurance verification, code-aware quote validation (referenced externally as "code-aware quote validation," internally as the Wiseman service), in-app messaging (masked), and a customer-satisfaction work guarantee.
- Sherpa Pros does NOT supervise, schedule, train, equip, or direct the manner of work. Sherpa Pros does not require uniforms, set hours, or restrict pros from competing platforms.

**The memo must address, with citations:**

1. **Massachusetts ABC test** (M.G.L. c. 149, § 148B). Apply each prong (A, B, C) to the Sherpa Pros pro relationship and identify the prong of greatest exposure (almost certainly Prong B — service "outside the usual course" — since Sherpa Pros is a marketplace, not a contractor).
2. **New Hampshire employment law** — RSA 281-A and the NH Department of Labor multi-factor test. Identify any NH-specific risks (NH is more contractor-friendly than MA but the analysis must be explicit).
3. **IRS 20-factor common-law test** for federal 1099-NEC reporting. Identify the highest-risk factors (typically: training, instructions, integration into business).
4. **Maine employment law** — the Maine Department of Labor's multi-factor test and any 2024–2026 statutory updates.
5. **Defensive documentation Phyrom must keep on file** (per pro): signed Pro Service Agreement, current Certificate of Insurance with Sherpa Pros LLC named as Additional Insured, current state license verification, current Workers' Comp certificate or sole-prop exemption form, signed acknowledgment that the pro is free to work for other platforms, signed acknowledgment that the pro sets their own prices and accepts/declines jobs at will, 1099-NEC issued for any pro paid >$600/year.

**Deliverable format:** A written legal opinion memo, 8–15 pages, jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction sections, an executive summary, and a one-page checklist of defensive documentation.

### 2.B — Client-Facing Terms of Service

**Required provisions (attorney to draft compliant language):**

- Sherpa Pros' role as a marketplace, not a contractor. Sherpa Pros does not perform work; the pro performs the work and is the contracting party for the work itself.
- Liability limitation: Sherpa Pros' aggregate liability to a single client is capped at the platform-paid amount for the job(s) at issue (i.e., the booking dollar amount, not the work value).
- The Sherpa Pros Free Work Guarantee:
  - **Project mode:** $5,000 per job cap, $25,000 per customer per year cap, 30-day post-completion window. Coverage: workmanship defect, code defect, scope mismatch.
  - **Quick Job mode** (per `quick-job-lane.md` §6): $1,000 per job cap, 14-day post-completion window. Coverage: workmanship defect, immediate-post-work leak, paint failure, obvious-mistake reversal.
  - Explicit exclusions: pre-existing conditions homeowner failed to disclose; damage caused by homeowner's own actions; acts of nature; work outside agreed scope; cosmetic preferences not in scope.
- Mandatory binding arbitration clause (American Arbitration Association consumer rules; or JAMS — attorney to recommend). Arbitration seat: NH (or DE — attorney to advise).
- Class-action waiver, with the strongest enforceable language for NH, MA, and ME consumer contracts in 2026 (state-specific carve-outs as needed).
- 30-day dispute window for non-safety claims; safety claims (injury, major property damage, regulatory violation) follow Track B in `liability-insurance-framework.md` §7.
- Choice of law: attorney to recommend Delaware (standard for tech/marketplace) or NH (Sherpa Pros home state). Phyrom prefers NH if defensible; defer to attorney recommendation.
- Acceptance mechanism: clickwrap with affirmative-assent button at signup AND at first job posting (double-consent for arbitration enforceability under recent MA precedent).

**Deliverable format:** Production-ready Terms of Service document + a one-page change log explaining each major drafting decision.

### 2.C — Pro Service Agreement (Pro-Facing)

**Required provisions:**

- Independent contractor classification (1099-NEC). Pro acknowledges they are not an employee, are responsible for their own taxes, do not receive employee benefits.
- Pro indemnifies and holds harmless Sherpa Pros LLC for any jobsite incident, property damage, bodily injury, or regulatory violation arising from the pro's work.
- Pro carries and maintains the required insurance:
  - Commercial General Liability: $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate (minimum $500,000 for handyman-only Quick Job lane participants)
  - Workers' Compensation: state statutory minimums (or sole-proprietor exemption form on file)
  - Commercial Auto: $1,000,000 combined single limit
  - State surety bond as required by the pro's state of licensure
- Sherpa Pros LLC named as Additional Insured on the pro's Commercial General Liability policy. Pro provides Certificate of Insurance at onboarding and at every annual renewal; pro consents to automated expiry monitoring.
- Pro authorizes Sherpa Pros to withhold earnings (from Stripe Connect balance or from future job payouts) to fund valid customer disputes resolved against the pro under the Sherpa Pros Work Guarantee.
- Mandatory binding arbitration for disputes between Sherpa Pros and the pro (separate from customer disputes). Same forum and rules as the client Terms of Service for consistency.
- **Beta-pricing grandfathering for Founding Pros:** the take rate offered at onboarding (5%) is locked in for the life of the pro's account on the platform, regardless of future general pricing changes. This commitment is contractual, not promotional, and survives the agreement's termination only on the specific terms negotiated for accrued but unpaid earnings.
- Pro consents to annual re-verification of license and insurance, including Sherpa Pros' use of third-party verification services (TrustLayer, Evident, Certificial, or equivalent) and direct outreach to the issuing state board.
- Pro cooperates with 1099-NEC reporting: provides W-9 at onboarding, updates on change of address or entity name, accepts electronic 1099 delivery.
- Pro acknowledges they may freely work for other platforms, freely set their bid prices in Project mode, freely accept or decline any job offer, and own their own tools and vehicles. (This language is the defensive evidence for the 1099 memo's facts.)
- Termination: either party may terminate for any reason on 30 days' written notice; immediate termination for cause (insurance lapse, license revocation, unresolved safety incident).

**Deliverable format:** Production-ready Pro Service Agreement + a separate one-page Founding Pro term-sheet summary that the Pro Success Manager can hand to recruits before the full agreement.

### 2.D — Per-Job Work Order Terms (Auto-Generated)

**Required provisions:**

- Job scope: Sherpa Pros' platform auto-generates the scope from its code-aware quote validation engine and the pro's accepted bid. The Work Order memorializes the agreed scope at the moment of acceptance. (Internally: this is the Wiseman scope; externally: never use that name — attorney's draft must use "code-aware quote validation" or a synonym Phyrom approves.)
- Code-validation acknowledgment: both customer and pro affirmatively confirm the code-aware quote at acceptance. Confirmation is logged with timestamp and forms part of the evidence chain in any dispute.
- Permit responsibility allocation: pro is responsible for pulling all required permits; customer is responsible for signing permit applications as the property owner and providing access for inspections. If the job is later determined to require a permit not pulled, the pro bears the rectification cost (customer is held harmless on permit cost up to the platform-paid amount).
- Payment terms: Stripe Connect escrow. **Project mode:** 7-day hold post-completion before release to pro. **Quick Job mode:** 24-hour hold post-completion. Pro may request earlier release via in-app button (waives the dispute window for that specific job). Customer may file a dispute during the hold and trigger a freeze pending Concierge mediation.
- 30-day work guarantee invocation procedure (Project mode) / 14-day procedure (Quick Job mode): customer files in-app dispute, Concierge triages within 24 hours, resolution tiered per `liability-insurance-framework.md` §7 Track A.
- Out-of-scope work: any work the pro performs beyond the agreed scope is a separate transaction and not covered by the Work Order or the Work Guarantee. Pro must request a scope change through the platform; customer must accept the scope change in-app for it to be platform-covered.
- Cancellation: customer may cancel up to 24 hours before scheduled start with full refund (Project mode) / 4 hours before (Quick Job mode) with full refund. Pro may cancel without penalty in cases of safety risk discovered onsite, customer no-show, or undisclosed scope. Other cancellations may incur a service-call fee.

**Deliverable format:** A template attorneys can confirm the engineering team renders into the per-job auto-generated Work Order document. Include language hooks (variable placeholders) for the engineering team.

## 3. Eight Open Questions for Attorney (Direct Prompts)

These are formatted as questions the attorney can answer directly — not Sherpa Pros internal-speak. Phyrom paste-drops these into the engagement letter as a numbered exhibit.

1. **Work guarantee cap defensibility.** Is a $5,000 per-job + $25,000 per-customer-per-year cap on a marketplace's voluntary work guarantee defensible under Massachusetts Chapter 93A consumer protection law, New Hampshire RSA 358-A, and Maine's Unfair Trade Practices Act? Specifically: is a contractual cap on a guarantee enforceable against a consumer who claims the cap is unconscionable in light of actual property damage exceeding the cap?

2. **Reserve fund.** What percentage of trailing-90-day Gross Merchandise Volume should Sherpa Pros LLC hold in reserve to fund Layer 3 (work guarantee) payouts? Are there any state-mandated reserve requirements for a marketplace operating a customer-satisfaction guarantee in NH, MA, or ME (analogous to escrow reserves required for some service contracts or home warranty companies)?

3. **Massachusetts contractor-referral-service licensing — HIGHEST PRIORITY.** Does the Massachusetts Office of Consumer Affairs and Business Regulation require Sherpa Pros LLC to register as a "home improvement contractor referral service" or analogous regulated entity under M.G.L. c. 142A or 201 CMR 14.00? If yes, what is the registration timeline, fee structure, bond requirement, and ongoing reporting obligation? If unclear, how should we structure the company's MA-facing relationship to minimize this exposure? **This question's answer most directly affects our Phase 0 public launch timing in Boston.**

4. **PM tier customer-of-record.** When a property manager dispatches a Sherpa Pros pro to a tenant's unit, who is the customer of record for liability purposes — the property manager, the property owner, or the tenant? This affects the Free Work Guarantee mechanics, who can file a dispute, who is bound by the Terms of Service arbitration clause, and to whom the pro owes the contractual duty of workmanship.

5. **Specialty-lane risk concentration.** Boston specialty lanes (heat pump install, EV charger install, electrical panel upgrade) have higher per-job dollar values ($10,000–$50,000) and higher per-job stakes (electrical fire risk, code-violation risk, Mass Save program risk). Should the Free Work Guarantee cap be different per lane (e.g., higher cap for heat pump, lower cap for handyman)? Is differential capping itself a Chapter 93A risk?

6. **Mass Save / National Grid partnership coverage requirements.** The Mass Save program and National Grid's Turnkey EV Charging Installation Program both reference qualifying installer-finder platforms. What insurance limits (Commercial General Liability, Tech Errors & Omissions, Cyber) do these programs require Sherpa Pros LLC to carry as a referenced platform? Is there published guidance, or do we need to request the requirements directly from each program's legal team?

7. **Mandatory arbitration enforceability — 2026 trends.** Maine and Massachusetts state courts have shown increasing skepticism toward mandatory arbitration clauses in consumer contracts (see recent decisions on adhesion contracts and unconscionability). What is the strongest enforceable clause language in 2026 for our client Terms of Service? What carve-outs (e.g., small-claims-court access, public-injunctive relief in MA) does the attorney recommend including to maximize enforceability?

8. **Class-action waiver enforceability — 2026 trends.** Same enforceability question for the class-action waiver, with specific attention to (a) AB5-style worker-classification class actions that pros might attempt (not consumers — pros, alleging misclassification as 1099 vs employee) and (b) consumer class actions for marketplace deceptive-practices claims. Recommend defensible waiver language, and recommend whether we need separate waivers in the Pro Service Agreement vs the client Terms of Service.
