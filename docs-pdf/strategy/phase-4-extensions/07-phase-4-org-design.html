Phase 4 Organizational Design and Executive Hires

Purpose

Phase 4 takes Sherpa Pros from a Series B-stage company (Phase 4 Month 0: ~25 employees, founder-and-engineering-led, single-metro deep + 4 metros wide) to a Series C-ready scaling company (Phase 4 Month 36: ~140 employees, full executive bench, multi-country, franchise network). This document is the executive hiring sequence, organizational structure, headcount trajectory, and compensation framework.

Design principles.

  1. Hire ahead of inflection points, not after. Country General Manager Canada hired Month 9 (3 months before international Year 1 begins). General Counsel hired Month 12 (before franchise-related legal load + embedded-fintech regulatory exposure compounds).
  2. Equity-heavy compensation preserves cash. Executive base salaries set at the lower end of market range; equity at the higher end. Cash runway preserved for international + franchise + product investments.
  3. No middle-management bloat. Vice Presidents own outcomes; Directors own programs; Individual Contributors execute. Three layers maximum below the Chief Executive Officer until Phase 5.
  4. Country General Managers report to Chief Operating Officer, not directly to Chief Executive Officer. Keeps Phyrom's span-of-control bounded as the company internationalizes.
  5. Founder time-allocation. Phyrom's job in Phase 4 is fundraising (Series B then Series C), executive recruiting, and brand. The Chief Operating Officer absorbs operational decision-making.

1. Chief Executive Officer — Phyrom (founder, full-time)

Background. New Hampshire-based working general contractor, twelve-plus years operating HJD Builders LLC, founder of Sherpa Pros at Phase 0.

Phase 4 time allocation.

  • 35% — Fundraising (Series B close + Series C prep + investor relations).
  • 25% — Executive recruiting (Chief Operating Officer + Chief Financial Officer + Vice Presidents + Country General Managers).
  • 20% — Brand, founder voice, press, community.
  • 10% — Strategic decisions and board management.
  • 10% — Customer + pro-community time (Phyrom-on-the-tools-Tuesday remains a brand-and-credibility commitment).

Compensation. Base salary $250K. Equity: founder stake (subject to Series B and Series C dilution). Cash bonus tied to Series C close.

2. Chief Operating Officer — Phase 4 Month 0 hire

Background sought. Operations executive with marketplace + multi-location experience. Ideal background: VP Operations or COO at a company that scaled from $10M to $100M+ revenue across multiple geographies. Bonus: hospitality, retail, or franchise operations experience (translates well to Hub network).

Reports to. Chief Executive Officer.

Owns. PM Success organization, Client Concierge organization, Hub network operations, Country General Managers reporting line, Vice President Operations dotted-line, day-to-day operating cadence.

Why Phase 4 Month 0. Phyrom's time-allocation cannot absorb both Series B fundraising and operating leadership simultaneously. Chief Operating Officer must be in seat at start of Phase 4 to free Phyrom for the fundraise.

Compensation range. Base $250K-$350K + equity 1.0%-2.0% + cash bonus 30%-50% of base tied to Phase 4 Objectives and Key Results.

Recruiting strategy. Executive search firm (recommend Heidrick and Struggles or True Search). Target hire-close by Phase 4 Month 0; recruiting runs Months -3 to 0 in parallel with Series B close.

3. Chief Financial Officer — Phase 4 Month 6 hire

Background sought. Chief Financial Officer with marketplace + Series A-to-C scaling experience. Ideal: prior CFO of a company that completed a Series C raise of comparable scale ($40M-$80M). Public-company-readiness experience (prior CFO of a company that went public, or VP Finance who was on the IPO team).

Reports to. Chief Executive Officer.

Owns. Finance, Financial Planning and Analysis, accounting, audit, tax, treasury, fundraising preparation for Series C, embedded-fintech compliance partnership with General Counsel.

Why Phase 4 Month 6 (not Month 0). Phase 4 Month 0-6 finance load is manageable by an interim Vice President Finance (existing senior finance hire from Phase 3) plus outside fractional Chief Financial Officer engagement. Permanent Chief Financial Officer becomes essential by Month 6 because (a) Series C audit prep takes 12-18 months and starts at Month 6, and (b) embedded fintech (Sherpa Lending pilot) requires Chief Financial Officer-level fintech compliance ownership.

Compensation range. Base $230K-$320K + equity 0.5%-1.0% + cash bonus 30%-50% of base tied to Phase 4 Objectives and Key Results.

Recruiting strategy. Same executive search firm as Chief Operating Officer. Target hire-close by Phase 4 Month 6.

4. Vice President Engineering — Phase 4 Month 0 promotion

Background. Existing Phase 3 senior engineering lead (specific name to be added when promotion is finalized) gets the Vice President Engineering title at Phase 4 Month 0 transition.

Reports to. Chief Executive Officer (until Chief Operating Officer is hired); Chief Operating Officer thereafter.

Owns. Platform development, multi-region rollout, SOC 2 Type 2 readiness, Application Programming Interface design discipline (per 05-vertical-saas-spinout-strategy.md commitments), Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning team build (per 03-ai-ml-roadmap.md).

Compensation range. Base $250K-$330K + equity 0.75%-1.25% + cash bonus 25%-40% of base tied to Phase 4 Objectives and Key Results 4 and 5.

Why this is a promotion not an external hire. Engineering leadership continuity is critical during multi-region rollout + SOC 2 audit. Internal promotion preserves institutional knowledge of the codebase. External Vice President Engineering hire would take 3-6 months to ramp during a window where the platform cannot afford a leadership gap.

5. Vice President Marketing — Phase 4 Month 3 hire

Background sought. Vice President Marketing with marketplace experience. Ideal: prior VP Marketing at Angi, Thumbtack, Lyft, DoorDash, or another two-sided marketplace at scale. Bonus: brand-led marketing leadership (vs purely performance-marketing background).

Reports to. Chief Executive Officer.

Owns. Brand, demand generation, paid acquisition, content, public relations, marketing fund management (including franchisee marketing fund), social-media-manager oversight (per Wave 8.2 social-media prompt library).

Why Phase 4 Month 3. Phase 0-3 brand work was founder-led plus contracted-design support. Phase 4 needs a permanent owner because (a) marketing-fund management for franchise system requires dedicated leadership, (b) international launches need country-localized marketing, and (c) Series C narrative requires consistent voice across investor pitch, press, founder voice, and demand-generation channels.

Compensation range. Base $200K-$280K + equity 0.5%-0.75% + cash bonus 25%-40% of base tied to Phase 4 Objectives and Key Results 6 and 7.

6. Vice President Operations — Phase 4 Month 12 hire

16. Compensation philosophy

Cash conservation principle

Executive base salaries are at the 50th-60th percentile of comparable Series B/C marketplace companies (per Pave / Carta executive compensation benchmarks). Equity grants are at the 75th-90th percentile. The trade preserves cash for international + franchise + product investments while attracting talent who believe in the long-term equity value.

Equity vesting

Standard 4-year vest with 1-year cliff. Phase 4 hires receive their grants based on Series B post-money valuation. Refresh grants annually based on performance + tenure.

Cash bonus

Variable compensation tied to Phase 4 Objectives and Key Results (per 06-phase-4-okrs-and-measurement-framework.md §Compensation tie-in). Paid quarterly based on Key Result attainment.

Country General Manager compensation localization

Country General Managers compensated in local currency at local-market-comparable rates. Equity grants in U.S. Dollar terms standardized across countries. Tax equalization handled via local payroll providers (Deel or Remote.com).

17. Recruiting infrastructure

  • Executive search firm. Heidrick and Struggles or True Search retained for all Vice President + Chief Officer + Country General Manager hires. ~$200K-$400K total search fees across Phase 4.
  • Director-tier recruiting. Mix of executive search + internal Director of People-led sourcing.
  • Individual contributor recruiting. Internal Director of People + 3-4 talent-acquisition hires + Greenhouse Applicant Tracking System.
  • Diversity and inclusion in hiring. Every Vice President+ search includes a slate with at least 50% from under-represented backgrounds (per Director of People-developed sourcing framework). Bias-mitigation training for all hiring managers.
